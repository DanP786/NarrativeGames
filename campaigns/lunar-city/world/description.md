# World Description

The world lived beneath the Veil, a mysterious atmospheric phenomenon that blocked direct sunlight for twenty-three hours of every day. One hour of natural light remained. Cities scheduled commerce, energy capture, medicine, worship, crime, and public movement around that brief sunrise.

Lunar City was a vast rain-soaked megacity of arcologies, industrial canyons, stacked tenements, neon markets, transit arteries, and corporate enclaves. Artificial illumination was constant but unevenly distributed: wealthy districts simulated daylight while poorer blocks endured flicker, rationing, and darkness.

Cybernetic augmentation was common across medicine, policing, labor, and crime. Full synthetic bodies existed but remained expensive and politically charged. Human cloning was impossible. Artificial intelligence had been outlawed after a historic catastrophe that official accounts discussed only in fragments; sophisticated expert systems remained legal so long as they did not cross the forbidden line into true autonomy.

Lunar City maintained a police department, courts, and elected offices, but law competed with corporate sovereignty, gang control, patronage networks, and unofficial arrangements. Justice existed, but access to it was uneven and often purchased.
