# Campaign Setup

- Created: 2026-07-11
- Campaign slug: lunar-city
- Genre: Cyberpunk-style noir thriller.
- Core premise: The sun is visible for only one hour each day because a mysterious atmospheric phenomenon blocks it for the remaining twenty-three hours.
- Voice: Third person.
- Tense: Past.
- Prose style: Fast-paced tech thriller — cinematic, action-forward, with investigations, chases, hacks, conspiracies, and combat; worldbuilding emerges through action.
- Length: Open-ended.
- Tone and content: Dark, gritty thriller. Graphic violence is permitted. Nothing else is categorically off-limits.
- Technology: Cybernetics are common. Artificial intelligence is outlawed. Human cloning is impossible. Full synthetic bodies exist.
- Stakes and scope: Begins as a personal survival story about a young cop supporting his family, with room for a city-scale conspiracy.
- Agency posture: Gritty realism; choices have lasting consequences and failure is real.
- Death rule: Hard death is possible when Jarrin enters genuinely lethal danger and fails.
- Mind-reading: None. Thoughts are private; no telepathy or technological direct access to inner thought.
- Character seed: Jarrin is a young, inexperienced police officer who has just moved to Lunar City. He wants to survive, make ends meet, and provide for his young family. He excelled at most academy field exercises and is keen to prove himself, but he is naïve and initially unwilling to follow the city's deep and dark ways.
- Opening situation: Jarrin's first shift in Lunar City begins with veteran Officer Mara Voss and a supposedly routine domestic disturbance in Block Seventeen that escalates into reports of shots fired and possible gang involvement.
