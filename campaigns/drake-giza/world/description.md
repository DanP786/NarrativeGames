# The world — late 2025

## The frame
Present-day Earth, with the Uncharted-style undercurrent: most of the world's old legends are paranoid stories told around fires. A few of them aren't. The ones that aren't are usually worse than the storybooks said.

## Modern Cairo and Giza
Cairo is twenty million people on the Nile, two thirds of them under thirty. The Giza Plateau, eight kilometres from downtown, is a tourist site with a perimeter that is more permeable than the signs imply if you know who to pay. The pyramids are open by day, locked at night, surveyed continuously by Egyptian Tourism Police, by Egypt's General Intelligence Service, and (per recent leaks) by at least two foreign services who route cover through legitimate archaeological grants.

The Old Kingdom — the Fourth Dynasty especially — built the pyramids. Khufu's, the Great Pyramid, was finished c. 2560 BCE. It is the only one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World that still stands. The orthodox archaeology says it was a tomb. The orthodox archaeology has not satisfactorily explained why Khufu's mummy has never been found, why the so-called burial chambers were apparently never finished, why the construction techniques exceed what we know they had, or why the alignments and proportions are what they are.

## The intelligence-services / antiquities milieu
- Looted antiquities are a multi-billion-dollar trade. Major laundering routes run through Hong Kong, Geneva, the Gulf, and London freeports.
- Both the CIA and the MSS (Chinese state security) have run archaeology-cover programs since the early Cold War. Most were petty. A few were not.
- The CCP and the CIA do not, as a rule, cooperate. When they do, it is because something is wrong enough that ordinary rivalry has been suspended.
- Egyptian state authorities know foreign services run programs on their soil and tolerate them within limits. The limits move. The current Sisi government is more permissive than the Mubarak years; the Tahrir generation is less.
- Private collectors with the right money can hold things that should be in museums for entire generations. The line between "private collector" and "intelligence cutout" is, in practice, the same line drawn twice.

## The supernatural register (narrator-only — surfaces late)
Khufu's pyramid was not a tomb. It was a *machine* — purpose unknown to anyone alive, lost in the records the Ramesside priests systematically destroyed in the late New Kingdom. Whatever it generated, channelled, or sealed in still does. The 1973 program found a piece of evidence sufficient to keep the search going for forty years. The recent reactivation is a response to something one side or both have just discovered, which neither fully understands.

This register stays under the surface for most of the campaign. Until late, every supernatural-flavoured event has a plausible intelligence-services or environmental explanation. Late, it doesn't.
