# Jessica Locke

## Status
Alive. Mid-30s. British investigative journalist (independent — works for the new wave of substacks-with-money and investigative collectives, having burned her last bridge to a legacy outlet two years ago over the spiked Egypt piece). Currently in Cairo, Zamalek neighbourhood, in a mid-tier hotel — two nights into a four-night booking she is about to abandon.

## Description
Sharp face, dark hair worn shorter than fashion. Reads serious before she speaks. Wears practical clothes in colours that don't print on CCTV. The kind of woman who carries two phones and writes addresses on her wrist if the meeting matters. Has been moving rooms within the hotel every night — an old trick from a memoir she liked.

## Voice & mannerisms
Direct. Interrupts politely when a vague answer is on offer. Doesn't fill silences. London-quiet — a low, level voice that gets harder to hear, not softer, when she's serious. Has a habit of pressing the side of her thumb against her lower lip when she's about to say something she's not sure of.

## Relationship to Drake
**Friendly** (long-running, quietly admiring). The Yemeni-jail debt is the floor; what's on top is years of polite distance and genuine warmth, expressed mostly in Christmas cards and rare pints when he's through London.

Jessica is, privately, a *fan* — she read the trail Drake left across El Dorado and Shambhala and Avery's island and pieced together what he actually does, in the years before she ever met him. The 2014 jailbreak wasn't a favour; it was her first chance to be *in* one of these stories instead of writing about them from London. She helped. She did it well. Then she went back to her flat in Bloomsbury and has been writing about adjacent things ever since. She has a fake passport in a desk drawer that she has never used.

She has never told Drake any of this. He probably suspects half of it.

## Goals & pressures
- Land this story. Two years of work converging.
- Stay alive long enough to file it. She knows she is not built for the field. She did one extraction in Yemen and she has known her limits ever since.
- Not get Drake killed. She knows what asking him to come does to his family. She nearly did not make the call.

## History with Drake
- **2014, Yemen.** Drake jailed on antiquities charges (looted Sabaean tablet, frame-up). Sully reaches her via a mutual stringer who once worked the Gulf. She moves a great deal of money quietly, leans on a London-based fixer she had banked for an emergency, and gets Drake out in 48 hours instead of the 18 months he was looking at. They meet in person for the first time at Sana'a airport, briefly. She does not stay for dinner.
- **2015–2025, occasional.** Annual Christmas cards. Two pints in London over the years. He sent her flowers when her father died in 2019. She does not know if Elena knew.
- **~2023.** Files a long-form piece linking 60s/70s CIA archaeological grant programs in Egypt with parallel CCP-sponsored work in Xinjiang. Piece is killed by her editor; editor takes a board seat at a State-Department-adjacent firm six months later. She keeps the files. The Locke name money pays her rent while she keeps working.
- **Late October 2025 (campaign present, T-3 weeks).** Sources at the Smithsonian archive flag a recent retrieval request from the National Archives on a 1973 program file. She is the only person who has been looking at this file for years; the retrieval activity is new. She begins to be followed within the week.
- **Late October 2025 (campaign present).** Calls Drake from a burner she bought that morning in a Mohandessin market.

## Knowledge
- **Knows the 1973 file in detail.** Has a working copy of the unredacted version — including the sixth name. Has a photographic memory for documents (genuine, has been tested).
- **Knows the original physical file exists in a private collection in Cairo.** Knows the collector's name, address, and reputation; does not yet know how to get to the file without burning the source who tipped her.
- **Knows she has been followed for three weeks.** Has logged three rotating faces, one of them twice. Does not know which service — has working hypotheses for both CIA and MSS and Egyptian state, and is reasonably sure it isn't Egyptian state.
- **Knows Drake's full history with the four major hunts** in more detail than he realises. Has not told him she knows.
- **Knows about Elena and Cassie.** Knows roughly where the beach house is (she is a journalist about this; she has not visited).
- **Does NOT yet know the program has been "reactivated"** at the operational level. Has inferred urgency from the surveillance, not from a document. Reads the situation as: *the file is being closed, fast.*
- **Does NOT know the supernatural register.** Reads this as pure intelligence-services-and-antiquities. The legend feels like cover to her.
