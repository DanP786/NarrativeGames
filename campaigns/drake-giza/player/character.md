# Nathan "Nate" Drake

## Vitals
- **Age:** 43
- **Wife:** Elena Fisher (Drake), 41 — former television journalist, now editor of a small online longform outlet she runs from the spare bedroom
- **Daughter:** Cassie Drake, 10 — fifth grade, terrifying at puzzles, currently reading her way through Sully's old paperbacks against her parents' better judgement
- **Brother:** Samuel Drake, 47 — back in the work, based loosely out of Madagascar these days, runs his own thing
- **Mentor / business partner:** Victor "Sully" Sullivan, 70 — runs Drake & Sullivan Marine Salvage out of Key West; the closest thing Drake has to a father
- **Home:** the beach house, Florida coast — stilts, hurricane shutters, salt-eaten railings, jacaranda in the back garden, a porch he built himself the first year
- **Cover business:** Drake & Sullivan Marine Salvage. Real, registered, light-touch — small charter recoveries, the occasional insurance job. Mostly Sully runs it. Drake is on the paperwork.

## Backstory
Orphan turned thief turned treasure hunter. Sully picked him up at fourteen in a museum in Cartagena and never quite let him go. By his mid-twenties he'd run El Dorado in Panama and survived what was inside it. In his late twenties, Shambhala — Tibet, the sap, losing Elena and getting her back. In his thirties, Iram of the Pillars in the Rub' al Khali, which broke his marriage and his shoulder and got both back together over a year. His last big one, Avery's treasure at Libertalia, almost killed him and the brother he didn't know was alive.

He retired after Libertalia. Bought the beach house with what was clean of Avery's money. Married Elena properly (a second time, smaller, with Sully officiating and Cassie not yet in the picture). Had Cassie. Lasted eighteen months before he set up the salvage business with Sully as cover for what he insisted he wouldn't do, and then another eighteen months before he didn't do it. The phone in the kitchen drawer is his one concession — never used, never thrown away.

## Current state
Forty-three. The shoulder he dislocated in Yemen aches when it's about to rain. He runs three mornings a week along the beach and tells himself it's for Cassie. He has not lifted anything from anywhere in eighteen months. He is not sure if that makes him a different man or just the same man waiting.

He loves Elena. He loves Cassie. He has not stopped looking at the horizon when no one is watching.

## Goals
- Pay back Jessica Locke for Yemen.
- Find out what Khufu's pyramid was actually for.
- Come home alive.
- Be the man Elena married and the father Cassie has.

(Two of these don't sit comfortably with the other two. He hasn't done the math on that yet.)
