# How the world sees Drake

## Public reputation
Doesn't exist. Nathan Drake is officially a small-time salvage operator out of Key West who married a former television journalist and is raising a daughter. The four big hunts left almost no public paper trail he didn't burn, and the ones he didn't burn Sully did.

## In the right circles
A name. The man who got El Dorado out of Panama, the man who came back from Shambhala, the man who walked out of the Rub' al Khali after Marlowe died, the man who finished what his namesake confusion at Libertalia started. Spoken about by certain auction-house specialists, certain Interpol art-crimes desks, certain private collectors, certain mercenaries who served opposite him once and lived.

The story most people tell about Drake is wrong in at least one important particular. He prefers it that way.

## Among friends
- **Sully:** the closest thing he has to a father. Knows him better than Elena does in some ways. Has been waiting eighteen months for the phone to ring.
- **Elena:** the woman who chose him with her eyes open after seeing him at his worst. Knows what he is. Married him anyway. Wants to be enough.
- **Sam:** brother, complication, presumed dead for fifteen years, alive now and back in the work the way Drake isn't. They speak monthly. They have not been in the same room in eight months.
- **Jessica Locke:** a debt and a friend and (though he would not say so) a window onto a life he doesn't live anymore. He likes her. He has never told Elena how much.

## To enemies
Recognisable. Has put enough people in prison or in the ground that there are short lists with his face on them in three or four places. None of those people know about the beach house. Yet.
