# Main thread

**Status:** sketch

## The hunt
A 1973 CIA archaeological program at Giza, cover-storied as a Smithsonian-routed "site preservation consultancy," was not a preservation consultancy. Six American names on the program inventory; five have died of natural causes spread across forty years; the sixth name is redacted from every declassified version of the file in circulation — except Jessica Locke's. She has a working copy of the unredacted version. The original lives in a private collection in Cairo whose owner does not know what he has.

The program has been quietly reactivated in the last 18 months as a joint deniable operation funded by CIA cutouts and CCP intelligence. Two services that should not be cooperating are pooling resources because what they are chasing is bigger than the Cold War habit of denying each other anything.

Whatever Khufu's Great Pyramid was actually *for* — not a tomb, but something — that program is looking for it. Again.

## Antagonist or obstacle
A joint CCP/CIA deniable program, manifesting operationally as a small professional surveillance and wet-work team in Cairo (and presumably more elsewhere). Behind them: institutional patience and money on a scale Drake has not faced before. Behind them: whatever the 1973 team actually found. Names and faces to be discovered.

## The PC's want (sketch — to harden through play)
**Surface:** pay back the Yemen debt. Help Jessica get the story.

**Under that:** he chose this life, the beach house, Elena and Cassie, and he is not certain he chose right. The salvage business with Sully is a polite fiction now; he hasn't lifted anything in eighteen months. The phone in the drawer rang because some part of him left the line open.

## Central question
*What does it cost to be the man who finds this — and who is left at the end to live in the house he came home to?*
