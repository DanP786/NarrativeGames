# Session 01 — 17 May 2026 (real-world)

## Scene 1 — The procession from the Spire to the Tilting Field (14th of First-Frost, late morning)
Sera rides out from the Spire's outer courtyard on Whitewater, her grey mare. The royal order: Aelric, Theodric and Eira, Lysandra, Sera, Daron behind. The Queen, in passing, gives her one soft sentence on the courtyard stones — *"Smile when we ride past the bakers' guild, my love. They came out for you."* — and Sera does not smile. She keeps the level-something she has worked on for four years. Lysandra hears the silence, files it, does not turn.

In the procession Daron drifts up to Sera's flank without protocol's permission and says, low: *"Are we going to do it her way today, or are we going to make her work for it."* Sera turns her head a fraction — heard him, no answer — and Daron's mouth does the thing that is less than a smile.

At the bakers' guild a four-year-old girl is pushed forward with a wreath of bay-and-berry, three winter roses bound in red thread. Sera slows Whitewater to a walk, takes the wreath, plucks one rose and hands it back to the child. Her face softens by a fraction no one beyond ten feet will see; the child sees it. The crowd makes a small, surprised, *human* sound. The bakers' guildmaster calls the formal blessing: *"The bakers of Caldemere keep your name in the bread, my lady."* Sera does not say the traditional answer. The Queen, ahead, does not turn her head — and *also* does not fail to hear the sound behind her.

Sera hangs the wreath on Whitewater's pommel, the two remaining roses turned to the wind. Visible. Read.

## Scene 2 — The opening ceremony at the Tilting Field
At the field's gate Daron offers his hand to dismount. Sera hisses *"I fell once"*; Daron, without turning his head: *"Twice. You were seven. Gravity was a suggestion."* She tells the groom Hessen to keep the wreath on Whitewater's saddle.

Royal seating: King at centre, Crown Prince and Princess to his right, Queen to his left, Sera further left, Daron set in the **second row** behind the Queen rather than beside Sera — a chair-choice Sera reads immediately. The Queen, mildly, before the presentations begin, drops Cassian's name into the cold air between them: *"Cassian Verrent, my love. In case you have not yet placed him."*

The presentations open. Three Caldros knights (Otrim of the Frostmarch, Wendrick Pell, Marrick Ardenmark), four Therondians, then **Aerinhal — Prince Cassian Verrent, alone up the three steps.** Twenty, taller than Sera remembered, red-and-gold, hazel eyes. He has read four volumes about Caldros to be ready. The steward turns the ribbon-tray to **Sera** — the Queen's choice. Sera holds the beat one count too long, rises, ties the green-and-silver around Cassian's offered wrist a fraction too tight, lifts her face into the half-lidded look she's been working on for a year, lets him see her mouth part — and sits back down. Twelve seconds. The pavilion releases a breath it did not know it had been holding. Daron, behind, laughs into the back of his glove.

The Queen, mildly, after, gloved hand resting beside Sera's hand on the chair-arm without touching: *"My love."* When Sera turns and confronts her — *"You did that on purpose"* — Lysandra: *"Of course I did. You wanted to see him. And I wanted you to see what wanting looks like on you, when you are not refusing it."* And: *"You wounded him. Cleanly. Tied him a little too tight, did you not? I felt the moment you did it from here."*

The Ostvar presentations follow. The third claimant — a personal envoy for **Aerwin Vornlin**, a son of a younger Vornlin prince long thought dead in exile — gives his speech cleanly. The Rime Court has not formally acknowledged him before this morning. Aelric goes briefly, visibly still. A second-row council ripple. The King's closing invocation includes, more quietly than the rest: *"And let the realm see who has come."*

Sera takes her wine, stands, blocks Daron's view, ends his small private toast. *"Set your sights on tonight's conquest?"* she says scathingly, hurt by *cruelest.* Daron — the dock-cant gone, the voice he uses with her in the orchard at midnight: *"That is not what I said. I said cruelest. That is a different word, sera-min, from cruel."* He tries to ask what their mother said. *"The usual,"* Sera says, and turns.

Sera crosses two seats to sit with Eira, alone. Offers her wine cup. Eira drinks, blushes — a girl's blush, not a Crown Princess's. Eira, plainly: *"You have not sat with me before. I have wondered if I should have asked you to."* They talk about Otrim — Eira's cousin from the Frostmarch — and Sera realises with a jolt: *"You sound as if you have working practice."* Eira gives the long *e* and tells Sera that Frostmarch girls of her house were taught long-knife and short-spear with the boys until twelve. Otrim was eleven when she was nine. *"He is the first man whose feet I learned to watch."* Then, the curtain down for one breath: *"I miss it."* Then, plainly: *"Why did you come to sit with me, Sera?"*

Sera, soft: *"You always sit alone, it bothers me. It's one thing for one as cold-hearted as me, but you..."* And: *"Are you lonely, Eira?"* Eira, plainly: *"Yes."* And: *"I do not say it to Theodric."* And, watching Sera's hands: *"Cold-hearted women do not do different things with their hands."*

Sera returns to her own seat and tells her mother, in Lysandra's own soft register: *"I will speak with you. Tonight. After the closing of the day. In your sitting room."* The Queen turns fully toward her daughter for the first time all morning. *Interested.* *"After the closing of the day,"* Lysandra agrees, and brushes the back of her gloved knuckle against Sera's for one breath.

The rest of the afternoon: Halryn of Vaerost beaten by an Aerinhal knight (Solveye); Daron disappears and reappears with snow on his shoulders and a vague *the hawking field, with a friend*; the Verrent senior knight in low conversation with Lysandra at the back of the pavilion; the third Ostvar envoy at the rail every bout; **Caelum returns to the pavilion at Theodric's chair-back at midday and does not look at Sera once for the whole afternoon.**

## Scene 3 — The Queen's small sitting room (after the closing of the day, ninth bell)
Sera comes to her mother's smaller private room. Lysandra is in slate-grey with her hair down — first time Sera has seen her mother's hair down outside her own chambers in months. The Aerinic accent at the surface. Sera kisses her cheek (the Queen's hand briefly to her daughter's jaw, gone before Sera has straightened — a small private thing).

Sera, at the fire: *"Eira is lonely."* Lysandra: *"Yes. I know."* She has known since spring. *"It is the loneliness of a wife. Which is a different thing. And the husband of a lonely wife is my son."* Sera: *"Do you think she would be less lonely if she had a child?"* The Queen's two-part answer — yes in the short term, no in the long, and a third reason: Eira's loneliness is new, it arrived in the second year and is fully present now. *"A child put into that chamber, before that thing is named, is — a child put into that chamber before that thing is named. I would not wish to be the woman who arranged the cradle to cover what is happening to her."*

Then: *"Why are you asking me this, my love."*

Sera, after a beat: *"Then you know Theodric loves another?"*

The Queen does not move for a long moment. Then, plainly: *"No."* She has wondered about the chamber. She has not known he loves another. *"How long have you known."*

Sera: *"The whole time."* Three years. Nearly four. She wants to bring a friend up from the Frostmarch for Eira — perhaps a sister of Otrim's.

Lysandra closes her eyes. The long blink. *"You have been carrying it almost four years and I have not seen it on you. And I am your mother."*

She picks up the wine, drinks properly, and changes tack: *"Yes. We can bring someone north."* Then, into the fire: *"Who is he, Sera."*

Sera, feigning ignorance: *"He? What makes you think it's a he?"*

Lysandra, the Aerinic accent at the surface: *"My love."* She enumerates the three years of seeing — Caelum not looking at his plate when Theodric speaks at the table, Theodric's head coming up half a heartbeat when Caelum speaks at his shoulder, the harvest-feast last autumn at which she sat across from them and watched them not look at each other once. *"You and I have been carrying it in the same room and not telling each other... do not pretend to me, please, that you did not also come here to find out whether I knew, because I have just told you, and we cannot now both pretend I did not."*

She tells Sera she will ask one thing before the tourney closes — *not now, not in this room* — *"whether you think he can stop. Your brother."* The question of whether he can stop is the question of what happens next.

Sera, stunned, fearful: *"Mother please, what are you going to do with Caelum?"*

Lysandra's promise, plainly, with her gloveless palm open on the table between them but not reaching: *"I am not going to do anything with him tonight. I am not going to do anything tomorrow."* She lists what she could have done in the first month and did not — sent him home, posted him to a border garrison, had a word said on a stairway. The not-doing has been her answer for three years. *"I am not going to have him killed. I am not going to have him disappeared... I am not going to take him from you."* And: *"I love you and I love your brother and I love Caelum Vesper in the in-his-way."* And: *"I would be grateful, in the months ahead, for company in the looking."*

Sera names the Frostmarch friend solution: Otrim has three sisters, Eira will pick which. Lysandra will write to Lord Aelthyn in the morning; the girl will be on the road within the week. *"Thank you, my love."* The first thank-you of the evening. *"Will you come for breakfast. Just the two of us. The small parlour at the seventh bell. You do not have to. — I am asking."* Sera agrees.

## Scene 4 — The corridor; Cassian; Caelum walking past (after the ninth bell)
Without thinking Sera heads to Theodric's apartments to warn Caelum. Marrow at the wing's outer door: *"The Crown Prince is in. With the Princess in the apartments. Half the bell ago."* Theodric has retired with Eira. Whether Lysandra has spoken to him, Sera does not know.

Caelum is in the east garret. Sera turns toward the servants' stair. At the foot of the stair she stops, weighs the absence of a story, climbs anyway.

Two stairs up, a footfall. **Cassian Verrent.** Red-and-gold cloak, dark Verrent court robe, sword-belt, a small gold-stamped ledger under his arm. He has been with Ser Yarrick — tomorrow's pairings, his match in the third hour against Marrick Ardenmark. The discipline holds: the small private corridor-bow, his eyes to the floor, the absence of a story he offers her (*"the gallery is quieter than I had expected"*) — a clean offer.

Sera steps down off the stair to him. Shaking, hands under her arms. *"Did you learn what you wanted?"* Cassian's two-part answer — yes, the pairings; *what I came north for I did not learn today.* Then, mildly: *"I learned that the binding I was given this morning was tied by a woman who had decided, before she rose from her seat, what she was going to do with the wrist she had in her hand."* He offers the cloak — the corner, not draped — and says he will walk south and not see her on the way. Clean offer.

Sera tucks herself under his arm. *"My room is this way."* Cassian goes still, then translates the moment into the most neutral grammar he can find: *"My lady, I will walk you to your door."* He invents, on the spot, a cover story — her dropped handkerchief, which he produces from his own pocket: a folded square of fine linen with a small embroidered V at the corner. *"I am sorry to have presumed to invent a thing on your behalf, my lady. But it is a thinner story than your own thinkings would have produced, and I judged that the night was already long for you."*

A footfall in the cross-corridor. Cassian moves fast: hand at the small of her back, turns her, her shoulders to the wall, his head down to her neck, the cloak spread against the wall to hide her gown. The pose is unmistakable. His face at her throat without his mouth on her skin. Free hand on the wall, not on her body.

**Caelum** rounds the corner. He has come down from the garret looking for her. His pace does not stop. As he passes them he clears his throat — once. The sound he has been making in Sera's vicinity since they were eleven and twelve. *I have seen, and I am choosing not to be in this room.* He keeps walking. His pace barely alters at her door (*she is not in her rooms*) and he goes on, around the next corner toward the Crown Prince's stair.

Cassian holds the position another four breaths, listens, straightens, restores the geometry. *"You know him,"* he says, very low. *"The walker. I felt you breathe."* He does not press the *who.*

Sera: *"Is it your fire powers that keeps you so warm?"* Cassian explains that Verrent fire warms what it is sent at, not the man who carries it — *the land does not bless its blood with comfort, only with use* — and that what she is feeling is Solinhar wool, twice the weight of anything Caldros weavers would put on a man. He offers the clean withdrawal a second time.

Sera agrees. At her door she says, lightly: *"Any chance I could have a cloak as warm as yours?"* Cassian agrees to send to the Aerinhal mercer in the morning. Will have the cloak delivered to her mother with his compliments. Asks the colour.

Sera **laughs** — small, half a breath, the soft catch in the throat — and Cassian Verrent goes still in the two paces between them. *He has heard her laugh.* The first laugh in months. Sera names a colour that is a mix of both their houses' (a red so deep it is almost old wine, with silver-grey wool inside; *not green, not a flag*) and tells him no Verrent crest. *"The mercer will know what I mean."*

She smiles at him — *the small private one Daron has known since he was nine* — and says: *"Goodnight, Cassian."* The first name. On purpose. She lets it land.

She closes the door. Inside her dark cold apartments she sits on the edge of the bed with the coverlet around her shoulders. She is, perhaps, not entirely the woman she was this morning. Half past the ninth bell. Caelum has not knocked. He will not. He will be at breakfast tomorrow.
