# Session 03 — Real-world date 19 May 2026 (in-fiction 23rd First-Frost late evening → 24th First-Frost dawn, ~7 hours)

This session opens immediately after the closing feast of the Hoarfrost Tourney and runs to the first miles of the road south of Caldemere. Two scenes. The session begins with a corridor encounter that places the wider-map Ostvar thread directly in Sera's lap, and ends with the public placement-of-herself in Cassian's carriage as the Spire passes the window for the last time. Several canon corrections and additions were locked at session opening — see end of session-02.md for the full list; relevant ones surface in narration here.

## Scene 1 — The upper corridor of the family wing, half past the eleventh bell, 23rd First-Frost

Sera has said her goodnights at the dais and left the Long Hall. The corridor between the Hall and the family wing — the one with the four bay windows that face down over the inner court — is almost empty at half past the eleventh. The braziers have burned down. The great window-lamps are out. Six wall-lamps remain.

Anwen is at her right shoulder, half a pace behind, the way Anwen walks when she is escorting her mistress home from a state evening rather than waiting for her in a chamber. Sera, just now, has the small pleasant tiredness of a woman who has spent four hours in deep blue and silver being kissed by the entire court without being touched. Her real goodbyes have already been said across the nine days; tonight was the public one. Cassian has gone back to the Aerinhal apartments to close his evening with his uncle's man and the senior knights.

She is halfway down the corridor when she sees the movement.

**Aerwin Vornlin** rises from the stone seat in the deepest bay window — the third one — where he has been waiting since the third quarter past the tenth bell. The brown working cloak is folded across his arm. He is in the same dark working wool Sera filed under her hood at his tent on the second morning of the tourney. The small Marchwarden pin is at his collar. He has put himself in the bay deliberately so he is not in her path; he is asking, by his position, to be acknowledged or to be passed.

He steps out one pace into the corridor. **Bows.** The proper bow — the bow a senior knight of the marches gives a princess of the line. Holds the bow a half-second longer than the courtesy strictly requires. On rising he does not move closer. He has set the conversation up to be refused. Sera could walk past him in two paces and he would not put a hand on her sleeve.

Caldros tongue, working register, low.

*"Your Highness. — Forgive me. I have waited in this bay since the bell at the third quarter past the tenth, in the small hope that you would come this way back to your apartments. I have not been received by the King today, and I will not be received by him tomorrow before you ride. I would not have you go south without three sentences I think you should hear from me. I am not sent, Your Highness. I have no message from your father. I am here, badly, on the credit of being a man you have noticed at the rail. Three sentences only, Highness. — And then I will go. To you, or to your woman, or to the wall. You choose."*

He has not lifted his eyes higher than her cloak. He is asking, in the small Caldros way, for one further permission before he raises them to her face.

**Sera, in Caldros, even:** ***"Speak then."*** She masks herself back into what Cassian has spent the nine days slowly removing. The face she came down to Long Hall with is not the face standing in the corridor now; the face standing in the corridor is the older one, the one she carried at sixteen and seventeen at the rail of the suitors' tournaments. She accepts no posture from Aerwin and gives him none.

Aerwin lifts his eyes to her face — to her throat first, the cloak's clasp, the line of the jaw, and only then to her eyes. He does not hold them longer than the work asks for. Speaks the three sentences as a working report. The chapel tower is between strikes; the corridor is silent.

**Sentence 1 (the standing).** He has come to the Hoarfrost Tourney on a Marchwarden's purse, in plain colours, with two men and no banner — the eastern marches do not, this autumn, produce banners. Has not been sponsored by Aelric and has not asked to be. Has been seen by the King in nine days at the rail and not asked for. Has been told that **the council on Ostvar will be held in the King's writing-chamber in eleven days,** after the Aerinhal delegation rides south. The Marchwarden will be in it. The Lord of the eastern lakes will be in it. The Master of the Border Roads will be in it. *He,* whose father held the small fort at Ostvar for thirty-one years before the pass closed, **will not.**

**Sentence 2 (the geography).** Tomorrow at first light Sera rides south with the Verrent delegation. She will be at the Verrent court in eight days; within the month she will be married. The Aerinhal route by the **Westmark road** passes within forty miles of Ostvar at its closest. The Westmark road is kept open through the winter at Aerinhal's own cost because it carries their grain. Within a year of marrying, Sera will be in possession of a Verrent inner-court name, an Aerinhal court's ear, and **an Aerinhal queen-mother who was once a daughter of the senior eastern Verrent house and has, on her own ledger, fifteen years of writing letters about closed mountain passes.** The eastern marches of Caldros and the western marches of Aerinhal share one pass. It has been closed for seven years. *"It has been closed because no one at either court has been the woman who wanted it open."*

**Sentence 3 (the ask).** Not asking Sera to remember him. Not asking for letters from Aerinhal. Asking only — when in five or seven years she is at a table where closed passes of two countries are being discussed and someone asks the small question *what is at Ostvar* — **that she not, in the moment, say *nothing.*** His father held the fort for thirty-one years and has been four winters dead; his father's men have, this autumn, sown their second crop of barley on the slope where his father used to walk the lower wall in the evenings. The barley is going to be the only thing off Ostvar in ten years if the pass stays closed. *"I would prefer that something else came off it. — That is the third."*

He lowers his eyes again to her cloak. The Marchwarden pin at his collar catches the wall-lamp. *"I have said my three sentences, Highness. I will go now. — I have not asked your woman to keep the meeting, and I will not. You may tell your mother. You may tell your father. You may tell your husband. You may tell no one. The choice is entirely yours. I have not put a thing in your hand that you need to carry. — Safe travel south, Your Highness. The Westmark road is hard going in First-Frost. Wear the cloak."*

**Memorable line, Aerwin, parting:** ***"Wear the cloak."*** The detail places him precisely — he has noticed which cloak, and what it means, and that she wore it in front of her father.

He bows again. Walks to the back stair at the corridor's far end at a working pace. The sound of the boots fades in four steps.

**Sera's response.** She has not moved. She has not given him a single inch of her face. She has not said yes; she has not said no; she has not thanked him; she has not had him removed. She heard three sentences and let him go. *She is carrying information south. She has committed to nothing.* Anwen, at her shoulder, has not moved either; Anwen will not put this in the Queen's hand in the morning unless Sera tells her to.

The chapel tower counts the twelfth.

Sera walks the remaining length of the corridor to her own chamber door and goes through it without speaking. Anwen follows. She does not ask Anwen to be silent about the encounter; she does not have to.

## Scene 2 — The inner courtyard at first light, 24th First-Frost, and the first miles of the Westmark road

Three hours of sleep for Sera; perhaps two for Anwen. First light at Caldemere in First-Frost is a slow grey — the sun does not so much come up as the dark thins. By the half before the sixth bell the sky over the lake is the colour of old pewter and the snow that came down in the small hours has put an inch of white over the inner court, the courtyards beyond, and the long line of the Westmark road as it leaves the south gate.

Anwen dresses her in the dark. **Deep slate-blue travelling skirt; fitted jacket of the same close wool; fine cream linen inner; long fur-lined riding boots; the leather riding-gloves she has worn since fifteen.** The Holsbrand cloak laid out on the bed, to be put on at the courtyard door — old-wine-red over silver-grey, the silver leaf-pin, no Verrent crest. The circlet stays in the small box; on the road she wears the **working hair** — tight plait at the temples, knot at the nape, three plain silver pins. Caldwyn women travel as they work.

She does not look at the chamber as she leaves it.

### The inner court at half past the sixth

The Aerinhal delegation has come up from its quarters in good order — **Ser Yarrick** at its head in working steel, the senior knights at his shoulders, **Lord Vesper** in a fur-collared coat at the second remove, the squires behind, the Aerinhal horse-master with the strings of grooms. The Aerinhal carts have been at the gate since before first light. **The Caldros escort — two hundred men of the Royal Guard, by Aelric's decision, as far as the marches' border** — has formed up at the far side of the court. **Caelum is at their head in working steel** with the small Caldwyn-rose helm under his arm, not on. The Master of Arms does not ordinarily ride a foreign princess to a foreign court. He is making the exception; Aelric granted it last night without being asked.

**The family is on the steps of the great hall.**

Aelric in working blue with the small fillet — not the great crown. Lysandra in deep slate, the small state pin, a fur over her arm she has not yet put on. Theodric and Eira on the step below — Eira's hand at his elbow, where it has stayed since the closing feast. Daron at the foot of the steps **with the small kestrel Wren on his wrist** — Sera's own falcon, the bird Daron has been mews-master to for two years on her behalf. Anwen at Sera's heel.

**The Aerinhal carriage is in the centre of the court.** The long inner-court carriage that brought Lord Vesper north — deep-glassed, the Verrent rose in dull gold at each door, four matched bays the same colour as Cassian's tilting horse. The driver and the two footmen in Verrent livery. The carriage is **for two.** It has been understood, since the closing feast, that Sera will travel in her own carriage at the head of her own train with Anwen and her Aerinhal maid-of-honour (Lord Vesper has provided one — **Riva,** a quiet woman of about thirty who came north with the delegation), and that the Verrent Prince will ride.

Cassian is at the second carriage with **Hallin,** overseeing the last cart. He sees Sera the instant she comes out onto the steps. Stops what he is saying. Gives Hallin three more words. Crosses the court to her, taking his gloves off as he comes.

He bows to her father first. Then her mother. Then her — properly, the public bow, the foreign prince's bow to his betrothed at the family threshold on the morning of her leaving. *"My lady."* *"My lord."*

She has decided in the small dark hour before first light what she is going to do. She has not told Anwen. She has not told her mother. She has not told Cassian.

She steps down from the second step to the courtyard. She crosses to him. **The Caldros court and the Aerinhal contingent both watch.** In Caldros, low but not so low that it is private:

**Memorable line, Sera, to Cassian in front of the inner court, by her own choice:** ***"My lord. — I should like to ride with you in your carriage today. Mine may go before us with Anwen and Riva, with one of the Caldwyn guard at the box. Yours and mine — together, if you will have me."***

Cassian's face does not change in front of the court. The not-quite-smile arrives at the corner of the mouth and stays there. He inclines his head — not the foreign-prince bow this time, the **husband-elect-to-betrothed inclination,** smaller, closer to the family register. In Caldros, at her volume: *"As Your Highness wishes. — My carriage is yours."*

Aelric on the step has heard. The small private downward-turn at the corner of his mouth — the smile the King allows in public. He sent her south to be placed where the match would take; she is, on the morning of leaving, **placing herself.**

Lysandra confirms in family register loud enough for court to hear: *"Riva can travel with Anwen in Sera's carriage. The two women will keep each other company. — That is a sensible arrangement, my love."* The Queen has, in front of the court, given Sera's choice her seal as if it had been the plan all along. The Aerinhal delegation registers the seal. Lord Vesper, who has just had to revise his morning's order of march, gives the Queen a small inclination — inner-court Aerinhal for *very well.* Aelric mildly: *"As your mother says."* Done.

### The goodbyes

**Daron** walks up the steps with Wren on his wrist. In the brittle bright voice he uses when he is afraid of his own face: *"Sister. — You will, in Verrentine, find no decent hawking. The country is too soft. I am giving you Wren as a reminder."* Holds out his wrist. Wren steps onto Sera's glove without hesitation — has known her hand since the spring of the year Sera was sixteen.

**Memorable line, Sera, in Caldros, quietly to Daron:** ***"Wren is yours, Daron. — Keep her. Fly her. Write to me about her. — When I have a household at Verrentine I will send for her. Not before."***

Daron, very low, in Caldros: *"Yes, sister."* Takes Wren back. Does not, this morning, make his second joke. The gift declined was the kindness preserved — Daron keeps his mews-master role; the bird is the long-distance bond.

**Theodric:** *"Travel safely, sister. Write to Mother every week. — Write to Eira every two. She will need it."*

**Eira** at his elbow in pale grey, hand at his arm. **Does not let go** to embrace Sera; leans across and kisses Sera's cheek without releasing her husband. *"Be happy, sister. — I have written to Riannon."* Sera, very quietly: *"I know."*

**Aelric** does not come down off the step. Extends his hand. Sera steps up, puts her hand in his. He closes his fingers — **third time in nine days** she has had her hand in her father's hand in front of an audience — and holds for one breath. Does not pull her in. Does not embrace her. Family register, only loud enough for her:

**Memorable line, Aelric:** ***"Sera-min. — Go well, my child. — Write to me. Not only to your mother. To me."*** Sera: *"Yes, my lord Father."*

He releases.

**Lysandra** last. Steps **down off the step** into the courtyard. Crosses to Sera in front of the Aerinhal contingent, the Caldros household, Lord Vesper and Ser Yarrick and Cassian. Puts her hands on Sera's upper arms. Looks at her face for a count of three.

She does not speak in Caldros. She does not speak in Aerinic.

She speaks the **old high Aerinhal — the inner inner-court tongue, the language her own mother used to her at nineteen at the door of the western palace** — three words. The three words a mother of the Verrent inner house says to her daughter at the door on the morning the daughter rides away to a marriage. Older than Caldros.

Sera, in the same old Aerinhal, gives **the three-word answer.** She has it; it is hers; her mother set it in her years ago without naming what it was for.

Lysandra's hands tighten on her arms once. Lets her go. Steps back. Turns her face to the Aerinhal contingent — the small lift of the chin: *I have been shown a thing, and I find it good.* The Caldros court has, between the lower step and the courtyard cobbles, just witnessed something it does not understand. The court will be talking about it for a week.

**Caelum** walks his horse up to the carriage as Sera is about to step into it. Stops three paces from the door. **Master-of-arms bow,** public, deep. In Caldros, working register: *"Your Highness. — I will ride at your left wheel as far as the border."*

She inclines her head back. *"My thanks, Captain."* He has not been her *Captain* since she was eleven. He is, this morning, **choosing the title in front of the court because the title is the one he can be seen using.** His eyes meet hers — one breath. He turns and rides to the head of the escort.

### The carriage

The Verrent footman holds the door. Cassian hands her up himself. The cloak gathered at the door; she lifts it the way she has been lifting it for nine days. The carriage is **warm** — a small enclosed brazier under the seat, the heat coming up through a slatted board at her feet, Verrent-cut. Deep leather over Verrent wool. **Small Caldros silver-grey cushions** — placed deliberately. *(Sera will, in three days on the road, learn whether it was Vesper's thought or Cassian's.)*

She settles. Cassian comes up after her — sits across from her at first in the seat facing back, the courtesy a foreign prince gives his betrothed in her own country on her first day of travel. Does not, immediately, sit at her side.

The footman closes the door. The latch falls. The horn from the head of the procession sounds. The Caldros escort moves. The first cart pulls. The Aerinhal outriders follow. Sera's own carriage with Anwen and Riva begins to move. Their carriage moves after it.

**The Spire begins, slowly, to pass the window.**

Sera looks out. The family is on the step. Aelric has not moved. Lysandra one step lower than him now, having come down for her three words and stayed. Daron at the foot, Wren on his wrist. Theodric and Eira on the step between. Behind them, household servants, the Hall doors open, the great Caldwyn rose on the lintel.

Sera looks at her mother last. **Lysandra is not waving. Lysandra does not wave.** Has, instead, lifted one hand to her own throat and touched the small slate-blue brooch at the shoulder — once, briefly — and let the hand fall.

**The carriage passes the gate. The Spire is behind them.**

### The Westmark causeway

The road opens out from the gate onto the long causeway at the lake's east shore — a mile of it, before the road turns south at the first stone and begins to climb out of the basin. The escort sets a working pace. The Verrent suspension is better than the Caldros, the wheels are wider, the road in good repair this far from the city; the carriage is quieter than Sera expected. The only sound is the steady working of hooves on packed snow and the soft creak of the carriage on its straps.

Cassian, after perhaps four minutes, in Caldros, quietly: *"Sera. — May I sit beside you."*

She does not answer in words. **Moves the small cushion at her left.**

He crosses to her seat. Sits at her left, his shoulder against hers, right hand free, left hand resting on his thigh because he has not yet decided what to do with it. Looks out of the window past her, at the lake. After perhaps half a minute, very quietly, in Caldros:

**Memorable line, Cassian:** ***"I am glad you asked, my love."*** He does not say *to ride with me.* He does not have to.

She looks at him. The carriage has, in the half-mile, taken her another half-mile from her mother and her father and Caelum, who is, just now, somewhere at her left wheel, riding the same pace.

She lifts the gloved hand and rests it, palm down, on Cassian's left hand. He turns his palm up under hers. **His fingers close, slowly, around her glove.** The carriage rolls south.
