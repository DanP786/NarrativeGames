# Current scene — The first inn on the Westmark road, evening of 24th First-Frost

*Opens session 04. Time-skip from the causeway at mid-morning to the inn at evening; the carriage day compressed.*

## Beats
- The road south after the morning's first stone. Mid-afternoon way-house (name spoken when Sera asked — to be canonised on next mention if it surfaces in play): half an hour for the horses, hot cider in a glass at the door, Caelum at his horse's shoulder in the yard, the snow already harder underfoot than the morning's.
- Sky slate by the sixth bell. Lamps lit at the inn yard before the carriage clears the rise. **First of the three pre-arranged inns** on the road south.
- The inn: long coaching house under a heavy peaked roof, slight rise, road bends west around a stand of pines. Wide yard. Stables at the back. A painted board over the lintel — Sera does not catch the name in the dusk. *(Name not yet established in canon; Cassian or someone may surface it in narration.)*
- Anwen and Riva arrived ~quarter-hour ahead with Sera's trunks. Hallin already in the stables working with the Caldros groom. Lord Vesper at the common-room door with the innkeeper. The Royal Guard deploys without speaking; **Caelum has the bunkhouse half cleared** by the time Cassian's carriage rolls in.
- Cassian hands Sera down himself. Cold harder than at Caldemere; lake-air gone. Keeps her hand the half-breath longer than the step requires; releases on the threshold. **In Caldros, for the innkeeper's hearing:** *"My lady. The house has your room ready. Anwen has gone up with the lamps."*
- Common room: low beams, working fire, three round tables, smell of stew and the small ferment of an inn that has just been told to feed an Aerinhal delegation and a Caldros Royal Guard and is rising to it.
- **The innkeeper:** stout woman with red hands and a clean apron. Bows once, deep, hands flat at the apron — the way a working woman bows to a princess. *"Your Highness. — Hot water has gone up. Supper will be on the table at the seventh."*
- **Caelum at the yard threshold, has not come in.** Master-of-arms voice, Caldros working register: *"Your Highness. The corridors are walked. Your woman is in the chamber. The lock closes from the inside. I'll be at the foot of the stair until the second of the watch. — My lord."* The last to Cassian, a half-degree of the inclination. Back out into the yard before he can be thanked.
- **Sera's chamber:** top of the inn stair, second door on the right. The best room. Fire laid; two lamps lit; a small writing-table by the window; a deep bed in dark wool; a copper basin steaming on the stand. The two travelling-cases in the corner. The Holsbrand cloak on the peg, where anyone who comes through the door can see it. Window faces south; the road continues toward Verrentine in a thin grey line in the last of the light.
- **Cassian's chamber is the door across the corridor.** He told her on the step as he handed her up. *(He did not, in saying it, say it as a question. He said it as information.)*

## State as the scene opens
- **The seventh bell approaching.** Sera's choice: down for the common table at supper, or a tray to the room.
- **Aerinic** between her and Cassian still not yet surfaced. Both still in Caldros register. The first private exchange in Aerinic remains its own waiting beat.
- **The standing-rule of consent with Cassian** is operative for the first time in a private chamber not in either parent's court. The chamber across the corridor is the proximity Cassian has arranged.
- Anwen is in Sera's chamber. Riva (Aerinhal maid-of-honour) somewhere in the house, working.
- The Royal Guard's bunkhouse is half-cleared (other guests likely moved on by Caelum's working). Caelum at the foot of the stair until the second of the watch.
- Sera carries Aerwin Vornlin's three sentences. Has not raised them with anyone. The Westmark road they have travelled today has, somewhere south and east of where they sit, passed nearer to Ostvar than it will tomorrow.
- Cassian's working belief about Aldwin is, as of this evening, two weeks and one day out of date.
