# Session 02 — Real-world dates 18–19 May 2026 (one long play session, 15th–23rd of First-Frost in-fiction, ~9 days)

This session opens the morning after session 01 and runs the entire Hoarfrost Tourney to its closing feast. It is the establishing arc's resolution: the central question — *can a woman who has refused to be moved be moved by anything she chooses* — is answered, *yes,* and the marriage of Sera converges hard onto the Aerinhal match. The other live threads (Theodric / Caelum / Eira; Aerwin Vornlin) advance but do not resolve.

## Scene 1 — The Queen's small parlour, 15th First-Frost, seventh bell

Sera comes down to her mother's small parlour having slept badly. She has dressed for war: the silver Caldwyn circlet at her brow, dark grey gown with the Caldwyn-frost pattern, the grey fur half-cloak. Lysandra is in deep blue wool over cream, hair loosely caught at the nape — private register, fires lit since the fifth bell, the small Aerinic morning. She pours for her daughter herself.

The mother-and-daughter conversation that opens the day:
- Sera deflects the circlet question (*"I'm just ready for the tournament, I thought I might go early"*). Lysandra hears the seam (*"You will be early, my love, by five hours and change"*) and lets it pass.
- Lysandra names three things she wants to work through this morning: the Aelthyn letter (which she wants to write *with* Sera, not alone); Sera's own marriage (one question only, no press); the King and Aerwin Vornlin (informational).
- The Aelthyn letter. Lysandra had intended to write *on Eira's behalf*; Sera recites what she has of the three Aelthyn sisters — Maerwyn (~26, widowed, runs the Keep), Riannon (22, yard-trained until twelve, writes Eira long monthly letters), Soren (18, still trains) — and proposes Riannon as closest in age and a paper-friend already. Sera asserts, not as a question: *"You are not going to commit any of this to paper without telling Eira first."* Lysandra revises the letter to be on her own behalf (*"having spent a long autumn watching her daughter-in-law grow further from her own house than the marriage intended"*) and agrees to take it to Eira at the third bell.
- **Memorable line, Lysandra (Aerinic, fully forward):** *"Riannon, then. — Sera. You have known the wrong things about the Aelthyn women for three years, my love. You have known them exactly right. Thank you."*
- The second thing: Lysandra asks plainly what Sera thought of *him* (does not say his name; does not have to). Promises a thin answer will be accepted.
- The third thing: Aelric will ask Lysandra what she thinks of Aerwin Vornlin before the week is out. And — **Ser Yarrick of Aerinhal has requested a private audience with the Queen at the tenth bell tonight.** Lysandra will take the meeting. Lord Vesper's brother (the King of Aerinhal) has prepared three drafts of a formal proposal letter. The first will be written only if Lysandra says yes tonight.
- Sera, sitting back: *"Every candidate has been your choice, Mother. Prince Cassian is — I don't know what he is. I do not know him."* Lysandra: *"Every candidate has been mine because you would not put one of your own forward. The first man you have not looked through is the one in this room."*
- The Holsbrand intelligence. Lysandra knows by the fifth bell that Cassian has sent for a cloak from the Aerinhal mercer Holsbrand, to be delivered to her with his compliments. She does not yet have the colours. She asks Sera to tell her.
- Sera flops down childlike in her chair — first time at this table in years — and confesses: she met Cassian last night, asked for a cloak in colours combining both houses, was teasing him. Lysandra rises, crosses to her daughter, rests her fingers against the silver circlet for a breath, then to the dark hair at the temple, then leans down and kisses the crown of Sera's head. Says nothing.
- **Memorable line, Lysandra (the colours named): "Red of old wine. Silver-grey wool. No Verrent crest."** Repeats them as she fixes them in her mind.
- The plan: Holsbrand will send the cloak through the third Aerinhal courier this afternoon, so it arrives at Sera's door without a story on either side. She may wear it or not.
- Sera tells her mother: *"If I am to entertain this pairing I will torture him first with impropriety. See what he's made of. I know you won't approve, but I get to do it my way for another year."*
- **Memorable line, Lysandra's warning:** *"Cassian Verrent is twenty-six. He is the son of a Verrent prince and descended of kings on both sides. He is not, in my judgement after one day, an unfeeling man. He is a man whose feelings have a long fuse. When you torture him with impropriety — and you will, I do not doubt you — he will not break in front of you. He will break in front of someone else, three days later, when no one is watching, and the someone else will be Ser Yarrick, or his father, or the page who lights his fire. Verrent fire warms what it is sent at, not the man who carries it. The man who carries it warms in private, when no one has sent him anywhere."*

Sera eats the cheese, excuses herself, leaves the parlour with the Queen's *"I heard the* if*"* held but not pressed.

## Scene 2 — The Tilting Field, 15th First-Frost, mid-morning (~8:00–9:00)

Sera rides down two hours early on Whitewater, with Hessen, in the heavy slate-grey fox-lined cloak (the older one, not the one being made), the silver circlet still under the hood. The field at the eighth bell is a different country to the field at midday: royal pavilion empty, frost on the rails, knights' end busy, baker's stall in the lee of the lowest stand. She buys cheese-and-sage and hot cider from a Lower City baker who does not place her under the hood. Walks the field perimeter, observing. Files at the Vornlin tent — small, clean, Marchwarden purse, two men reading and writing at the open flap, no consequential visitors yet at the eighth bell. Files at the Ardenmark pavilion — Marrick himself on the practice ground, taking a heavy black horse hard through a turning drill, *good,* she forms an opinion her brother would have formed in five seconds.

Comes round behind the Aerinhal tents and sees Cassian from across the boards. He is at the corner of the gear tent, on a low stool, no cloak in the cold, teaching a fourteen-year-old ginger Verrent squire how to straighten a bent buckle-tongue with a punch and a stone. He is teaching in silence — not speaking, only showing, letting the boy lean closer and try and fail and try again. He nods once when the strap is right, hands the punch to the boy, and lets him run.

Sera walks up the boards-line and stands beside the Aerinhal squire master (Hallin) without speaking. Hallin clocks her — woman, slight, hooded, cloak the wrong slate-grey for any working Caldros household, the boots wrong — and waits with her. Cassian, on standing, sees Hallin first and then the cloaked woman beside him. Goes still for one breath. Sends Hallin away on a working pretext (*"send Vael in to me at the third quarter"*). Bows to her at the head only — half a degree, the inclination a Verrent prince gives an unintroduced woman.

Sera walks into his gear tent and pulls the flap closed.

### The first kiss (averted) and the favour

She lowers her hood. He says *"Highness"* and *"you are very early."* She tells him she was cold and he had said he could warm a tent. He works the brazier coals up — it is warmer in two minutes. He asks plainly: *"You did not come here only for luck. — What is it, Highness."*

She tells him her mother told her this morning he will be formally requesting her hand. He confirms — Yarrick made the approach the night before; three drafts, the third favoured; he asked his father to do nothing that would commit him before he met her; his father sent the drafts anyway. **He says he is willing to be refused, plainly, to her face, before the letter is written; the letter will not be written if she refuses now.** *"I am not asking you to choose me. I am asking you to decide whether the letter goes."*

She says: *"If I am to entertain this pairing I will torture him first with impropriety."* Then: *"I do not know what impresses me. Every suitor has been watched — they perform to those around them. It is not who they really are."*

He listens; he steps a half-step closer, then back. *"You will not ruin me. I am — perhaps — better matched. Whatever you do to me, I have nothing for it to fail."*

She gives him her own handkerchief from the inside of her cloak — *not* a cut favour, her actual handkerchief, lavender and the darker scent at her wrists. *"For luck."* Cassian receives it the Verrent way: folds it once with both hands, presses it flat in his left palm, closes his right hand over it, bows from the waist. *"My lady. — I will ride with it at the third hour. Ardenmark is the harder man in this tournament; he will use the third pass to put me on the grass if he can. If your handkerchief is on my left wrist when he comes down the rail, my lady, he will not."*

She tells him she should thank him — for last night, twice. He confesses Caelum saw them in the corridor and walked past, and that he has spent the night trying to read the message. (See *scene 3* for what Caelum did with it.)

She teases: *"If we are to be betrothed does it truly matter if we are seen together?"* He laughs once and does not bow at it. He half-steps closer and explains that, *until the proposal is in her father's hands,* the seeing matters: the realm is watching for *arranged vs chosen.* He asks only that the corridor not be the only place — that they be seen in the open at the lists, at the Whitelake tent, at the closing wine.

She steps fully close. *"Call me Sera."*

He says the name, carefully — the first syllable Caldros, the second with an Aerinhal warmth on the *a* — and tells her *"call me Cassian, not Prince."* He steps back. *"If I do not, in fifteen seconds I will not be able to. — I have not yet earned the right to not be able to."*

He asks to bring her a cup at the closing wine tonight. She smirks: *"If you win."* Pulls her hood up and walks out confidently. Hallin's bow on her exit is half an inch lower than the bow he gave her on the way in.

## Scene 3 — The royal pavilion's king's withdrawing, 15th First-Frost, late morning

Sera rides up to the empty royal pavilion. Three household servants at work in the box. **Caelum is at the rail** in working steel and the small Caldwyn rose pinned at his collar — not a servant, not master-of-arms-yet-on-duty, simply present. He has been waiting for her since the ninth bell last night.

She takes his hand and pulls him into the king's withdrawing — the small curtained retiring room behind the box, blue wool heavy enough to muffle the sound. He lets his hand be taken; releases it carefully when the curtain falls.

**Memorable line, Caelum:** *"I have been waiting for you to come and find me since the ninth bell last night."*

She vomits the night's worry: she confessed Theodric and Caelum to Lysandra by accident; Lysandra had been talking around Eira's loneliness and Sera thought her mother knew everything; Sera begged Lysandra not to say or do anything; tried to get to Caelum but he was not in his rooms; Theodric's guards told her he had retired with Eira; she nearly went up to the bachelors' apartments; Cassian found her in the corridor and — and — *"I'm sorry Caelum. I betrayed your trust."*

Caelum's long answer:
- *"You did not betray me."* Said three times. Lysandra has known three years and Caelum has known she knew. She has, in three years, made four small not-acting kindnesses (a horse moved, a page sent away, a door left open, etc.). The Queen has been managing them for years; Sera gave her confirmation, not information.
- *"You are sixteen years old. — I misspoke. You are twenty."* Catches himself; corrects. **(Caelum's tell: his memory of her age fixed at the year he first told her the secret, four years ago. Canon: Sera is 20. The slip stays in his voice as his small tic.)**
- *"That you finally said the wrong thing to the one person who already knew is not a betrayal. It is the cost of asking a twenty-year-old to be older than her years for four years."*
- He saw her in the corridor with Cassian. Cleared his throat once and walked past. Asks: *was it a thing being done to her, or a thing she was choosing?*

Sera hugs him — first hug since their grandfather's funeral four winters ago — and tells him Cassian was protecting her, that *we're all good,* and that he will ask for her hand tonight. Caelum closes his eyes once, opens them steady. *"Then I am going to put him on the grass for a different reason."* Dry joke. He says he had thought Cassian had risked scandal because he did not know the Caldros corridors; he is revising the thought.

**Memorable line, Caelum (his three points, edited to their substance):**
- *"Your mother will not move on Theodric in the next nine days. — Theo is safe through the closing feast at least. Eira is safe through then. We have time."*
- *"The Aelthyn letter is good work, Sera. Better than you know. — I am proud of you."*
- *"If, when you sit with it tonight before the wine, you find that you can imagine waking in twenty years and being glad it was him — then accept, and do it gladly, and do not let the fact that your mother chose him first take a single morning of that gladness from you."*

He goes out. Sera waits a count of one hundred, then another hundred, then takes her seat in the box. The household servants have remembered nothing because there is nothing to remember.

## Scene 4 — The lists and Cassian's first match, 15th First-Frost, third hour

The court fills. Daron arrives first and asks low, *"What did you do, sister?"* Does not press when she gives him only the public face. Theodric and Eira come together — Theodric not slept, Eira's eyelids faintly raw, but the two of them sitting *closer than yesterday morning.* Something has happened in their apartments overnight. Sera does not, for the first time in a year, want to know what. Lysandra comes last in slate-grey with the small public crown; her eyes find Sera's, ask one question.

The first bouts run. Aerwin Vornlin is at the rail in the lower stands in a plain brown cloak, watching the royal pavilion — not the bouts — and *especially* watching the King.

Cassian rides out at the third hour. **The handkerchief — pale, lavender-scented — is tied at his left wrist above the gauntlet.** The crowd notices. Daron's head turns by one degree; he sees the wrist, sees Sera, sees the wrist again. Closes his mouth. Eira sees the favour and looks at Sera with an unreadable expression. Theodric does not turn his head — a decision. Lysandra sees and her hand on the cup is perfectly steady. Caelum sees and puts one hand on the rail.

The first pass: Cassian takes Ardenmark on the centre of the shield, broken lance, good Verrent strike. Marrick takes Cassian on the gorget. First pass to Cassian on points.

Between passes Marrick lifts his visor and looks across the field for two full seconds — not at the royal pavilion. **At the favour on Cassian's wrist.**

The second pass: Marrick aims for the wrist. Not legal exactly, not illegal exactly. **Cassian sees it coming.** In the last twenty yards, the bay's head drops by an inch; Cassian's body shifts by perhaps two — away from Marrick; lance up a hand's-breadth — and finds Marrick under the shield instead of on it. Marrick's lance scrapes the gauntlet (metal on metal, brief) and reaches air. Marrick's seat goes; he comes off the black on the far side, lies on the grass for a count that is too long, sits up at last with a hand at the hip.

**The match to Verrent.** The crowd makes the noise.

**Memorable beat, Daron (low, into her ear):** *"He aimed under the shield, Sera. — Who taught him that. That is not a Verrent strike. That is one of ours."*

Behind Sera, Lysandra makes the smallest sound in her throat — the sound of a woman who watched a thing she had calculated for and saw that the calculation was, in one respect, wrong. Caelum's hand white on the rail.

## Scene 5 — Closing wine of day 1 of the tourney, 15th First-Frost, evening

Wine on the field at the royal pavilion. Aerinhal contingent up the steps led by Ser Yarrick; the King receives them himself, the closing ceremony of a good day. Cassian crosses the box to Sera with two cups of hot wine — one taken from the trestle for her on the way past, with a quick word to the pouring boy. He bows publicly: *"With my compliments. — And with the thanks of the Verrent contingent for the kindness of the Caldwyn court today."* The favour is now under his coat, against the breastbone.

She stands and takes the cup, brushing her gloved fingers over his. The four nearest ranks of courtiers notice. They will notice tomorrow at breakfast.

**Memorable beat, Cassian to Daron (charming the brother):** *"I read the one with your name on it twice, Your Highness. My father has been told that no Verrent prince should ever read what foreign sons of kings write about their own strikes. I have not always listened to my father."* The court laughs; Daron grins, first real grin of the day. Theodric drinks instead of speaking.

**Memorable line, Cassian, barely above a breath, under Daron's laugh:** *"Your mother is going to take Ser Yarrick into her rooms at the tenth bell. — Be where I can find you at the ninth."*

He turns and goes to greet Theodric. Does that piece himself, in the open, in front of the court, while the Crown Prince's wine is still cold in his cup.

## Scene 6 — The corridor at the ninth bell, 15th First-Frost

Sera in dark slate wool, no circlet, hair down, small fine cream wrap. She has chosen to be in this corridor what she would be when no one is watching.

Cassian comes up the back stair in the plain dark Verrent court coat. *(Implied: Caelum has cleared the night-woman at the alcove. Caelum's silent kindness, in his master-of-arms register, the small Caldros word for an evening neither of them is going to speak about until later.)*

Cassian's long answer to *"Do you want this marriage?"*:
- *"Yes."*
- The history: his father told him about her when he was sixteen; he made a wrong picture in his head; his father showed him the Master Hallen portrait three years ago and he asked his father to do nothing until he had met her; his father sent the drafts anyway.
- *"I came here intending to be honourable about a thing I had not yet chosen. I had not, by any plan I made in Aerinhal, intended to give Hallin the look I gave him this morning."*
- **Memorable line:** *"I want this marriage. Yes. I have wanted it since the moment in the corridor last night when you said* very well *and meant it. I want the years after the marriage. I want to be the man who knows what wool you wear when no one is watching and what you look like with your hair down at the second of the night-watch's rounds."*
- The ask: stay close until the tenth bell, or leave now. Her choice.

Sera, on her past suitors: *"What I did to the last 3 proposals. I accepted, then pushed them, challenged them until they withdrew."*

Cassian's answer:
- *"I read the dispatches. My father has been keeping them. A small green leather book. Ten dispatches: three Norvik, four Allendar, three Volstrand. The Volstrand reports, I think, in Lord Halvorsen's hand."*
- *"What you did to them was the work of a young woman being asked to marry a man she had been allowed to know for less than a fortnight, and who had decided that if she was going to be married out of her country at her mother's hand, she was at least going to find out which of the suitors had a self underneath the bow. You did. None had one."*
- **Memorable line:** *"The three were men who needed you to be the woman the court told them you would be. I do not have a script for you. — Whatever you do to me, I have nothing for it to fail. The only thing it can do is show me you."*
- *"Sera, push me. I am not going to withdraw. I will not even flinch in the way you are afraid of. I will flinch in some other way, and you will know me a little better because of it. — Until at some quiet hour twenty years from now you push me again and I will not flinch at all, and you will think: I did that."*

Sera bites her lip: *"I wouldn't mind if you did touch me."*

**The kiss(es).** Cassian lifts his right hand to her temple, palm to the cheekbone. *"Tell me to stop, Sera. If you decide to."* He kisses her slowly. Three breaths. Pulls back half an inch, his eyes wet at the corner. *"I have wanted to do that since the corridor last night."* Kisses her a second time, shorter and more itself. Steps back three inches.

Sera: *"I will marry you."*

He closes his eyes for one breath. Takes her right hand and lifts it palm-up, then closes her fingers into a small loose fist and presses his lips to the backs of her knuckles for the count of three breaths — not a kiss, a holding.

**The endearment, used once and carefully:** *"After. — After, my love, we can stand in this corridor in the open, with the whole Spire watching, if you like."*

He goes down the back stair without turning at the corner — a small kindness Sera names only afterward. The chapel tower counts the quarter past the ninth.

## Scene 7 — Sera's chambers, the night of 15th First-Frost

Anwen banks the fire, undresses her, brushes out the hair, slips into the outer room and closes the inner door by a finger's width. The chapel tower counts the tenth — Lysandra is, somewhere two floors above, hearing Yarrick's two-thirds-of-an-hour of careful Caldros about mercer Holsbrand and Marchwarden purses and the late autumn weather, and then the last third in inner-court Aerinic, and the small question, and the answering yes.

Sera lies on her back. She thinks about her mother's face. She thinks about her father in the morning. Her lips tingle. She decides the tingling is allowed for tonight; by morning it will have to be put away. She rests her fingers at her own temple where Cassian's palm had been, leaves them there, decides she is not afraid. Asleep before the eleventh bell.

## Scene 8 — The family parlour, 16th First-Frost, half past the seventh

Sera is slow to get up. Anwen sets a slow pace. The silver gown with silver embroidery; the small plain fillet (not the public circlet); the hair down with one thin plait pinned behind the right ear. Anwen has hung **the new cloak from Holsbrand on the peg** — old-wine-red Solinhar wool, silver-grey Caldwyn-wool border at hem and hood, a plain hammered silver leaf-pin at the throat (Holsbrand's house mark, *I made this and no other man*). Made in twenty hours. Anwen settles it on Sera's shoulders at the parlour door, not before.

Family parlour breakfast — not the small one, the longer table the family takes when there is something to be marked in public-but-private. The Queen is in slate-blue for the family table, no crown. Theodric at her left; Eira beside Theodric, sitting *closer* than yesterday; Daron at the foot.

When Sera comes through in the cloak — Daron sets the bread back on his plate. Theodric looks once, slowly, at the gown, then at the cloak. Eira's face does a quick, almost-relieved thing, gone before fully arrived. The Queen's chin lifts a fraction. *"My love. Come and sit. The cardamom milk is in the small pot. Daron has been waiting for you before he eats anything, which is unlike him."*

**The exchange about the cloak.** Sera, sitting calmly and helping herself to food: *"Prince Cassian had it made."*
- Daron: *"Made, sister. Not bought. — Made. — In how many hours?"* / Sera: *"Twenty, perhaps."*
- Theodric, quiet, into the cup: *"Holsbrand cuts in his own house. He does not cut in twenty hours for anyone. Cassian Verrent paid in something that was not money."*
- Lysandra, mildly: *"He paid in the colours, Theodric. Holsbrand cuts in twenty hours for the colours."*

**Theodric's turn.** Looks at Sera, finally, says cleanly and not angrily:
- *"He rode well, sister. He aimed under the shield. That is one of mine."*
- *"Daron tells me he has read me twice. I would have liked to have been told that by a man before I was told it by his second pass at a strike."*
- *"I will speak to him today. Properly. In the open. — I should have done it last night at the wine."*

**Eira:** *"Theodric. I should like to come with you when you do."* Something passes between them that Sera cannot quite read. Theodric, after a beat: *"Yes."*

The Queen lays out the morning to come: King will read the letter between the eighth and ninth, will send for her first (before the ninth has finished striking), will send for Sera after that, will send for Theodric after that. Daron to be on the upper gallery between the ninth and the tenth in case the King changes his mind. Eira to the small audience room with the Queen and Theodric if she wishes. **Eira: *"I should like to be there. If you will have me, Mama."*** Lysandra, Aerinic, softly: *"Of course, my love."* First Aerinic at this table.

To Sera, Aerinic: *"The silver becomes you. The fillet was Anwen's idea, and Anwen was right. When you go to your father, wear the cloak. He will not need you to wear it. But wear it."*

## Scene 9 — The audience chamber, 16th First-Frost, ~quarter past the ninth

Aelric receives Sera formally in the long audience chamber. He is in dark blue, the working state crown at his brow; Lysandra is at his right in slate-blue *without crown* — by her dress, deliberately, Sera's mother today and not the Queen. The chamber otherwise empty. Sera walks the thirty paces of red carpet in the cloak Holsbrand made and the silver gown and the small fillet.

**The King's decision.** He has read the Aerinhal letter twice. He accepts the proposal. Cassian and Sera will be betrothed at the closing feast of the tourney. **He has added a condition:** at the close of the tourney, in nine days, Sera will travel south with the Aerinhal delegation, reside in her future father-in-law's house for one month from the day of arrival (that month is her engagement), and be married in the Aerinhal cathedral by Aerinhal rite, with Caldros rites added, at the Feast of the Lesser Lights. She will reside in Aerinhal as Cassian's wife thereafter.

**His stated reason:** the Aerinhal household is *patient,* will hold her well, will not give her the small daily occasions of provocation a Caldros engagement provides. The marriage will *take* on its own ground.

**His real reason, given because she is twenty and he has not been in the habit of lying to her:** *"I do not want to lose you a fourth time, Sera-min. — Three is enough."*

Sera accepts the decision. She asks three things and one named permission:
- **Anwen** comes south with her, into her household in Aerinhal. Granted (*"I would have given the leave whether you asked or not, but I am glad you asked"*).
- One day in the orchard with Daron in the nine days before she goes.
- One ride out to the hawking field with Theodric and Eira.
- One evening with her mother in the small parlour.

Lysandra, for the first time in the audience, turns her head and looks at her daughter — the small lift of the chin Sera has now twice seen this morning. *I have been shown a thing, and I find it good.* Nods to her husband. Aelric: *"Yes, my child. All three. The household will be told to make the time."*

Aelric calls Sera to the dais; she puts her hand in his; he holds it for one breath without pulling her closer. Releases.

**Memorable line, Aelric (at parting, his face unmoving):** *"The cloak is well cut. — Tell the prince, when you next see him, that I noticed it."*

The Aerinhal delegation is received in the audience chamber at the third hour the same day to ratify. Sera is not required at the reception; she is told that evening what was said.

## Scenes 10–18 — The remaining eight days of the tourney (16th–23rd First-Frost)

Compressed. The shape of the days, day-on-day:

- **Sera visits Cassian at his gear tent every morning at half past the eighth.** Twenty-seven half-hours over the nine mornings, at the brazier, with Hallin at the canopy as a chaperone-by-courtesy whom the Caldros court politely pretends he is. *No kissing in that window — held by mutual restraint until after the public announcement.* Topics covered across the nine: Aerinhal in winter; the books on each other's shelves; Sera's grandmother in the dower house at the western lake (ninety this Frost); Cassian's sister at sixteen, who already knows Sera by Cassian's letters; the household Sera will keep when she is south; the Verrent librarian's bad scholarship; what to do about Master Hallen's three-year-old portrait of Sera (Cassian: *"keep it. I want to see how badly he got you wrong"*).
- **The Aelthyn letter goes to Eira at the third bell on the 15th, in her own apartments, taken by the Queen and Sera together.** Eira signs it under the Queen's. The letter goes out the morning of the 16th by the Queen's courier to Lord Aelthyn at the Frostmarch. **Riannon Aelthyn confirmed by return courier on the 21st — will travel south to Caldemere within the month.**
- **Theodric speaks to Cassian on the 16th in the open, on the field, with Eira at his side.** What is said is private. The court reads only the outcome: Theodric, after, claps Cassian's shoulder publicly and does not let go for a count of two, which is the Crown Prince giving the prince his face. Eira, throughout, at her husband's shoulder, her hand once briefly on his elbow. **By the closing days of the tourney, Theodric and Eira are sitting visibly closer.** What changed in their apartments the night of the 14th has not been explained to anyone, but by the closing feast Eira is the woman who walks beside her husband, not the woman who follows him.
- **Aerwin Vornlin's Ostvar claim advances.** Formally received by Aelric in private audience on the 17th. His case is *interesting,* which is the worst thing the Rime Court can say about a foreign claimant — interesting enough not to dismiss, not solid enough to back. The King is holding. The matter will outlive the tourney.
- **Daron behaves.** Says nothing improper about Cassian to anyone. Hauls Sera into the orchard on the 19th for the promised day; they walk the cold paths and he tells her he is glad and that if Cassian ever makes her cry he, Daron, will go south by night and burn the Verrent third bedroom down. (She believes him.)
- **The hawking field with Theodric and Eira on the 21st.** A long ride out in clear cold weather. Theodric flies his short-winged falcon; Eira her grandmother's small Aelthyn hawk. They eat at a hunting cottage and ride back at dusk. Eira, on the path: *"I will miss you, Sera. — I had not realised, when this all began, that I had not yet started missing you."*
- **The Queen's small parlour, the 22nd evening.** Lysandra and Sera alone, no business on the table. Aerinic the whole evening. Lysandra gives Sera the small silver locket her own mother gave her on the morning she left Aerinhal at nineteen — a peace-bride's locket, with a single grain of cardamom inside. *"This goes south with you. I should have given it to you four years ago. I have wanted to keep it close to me. I am glad to be wrong about that, my love."*
- **Caelum.** Public face all nine days. Two private exchanges with Sera. The first in the upper gallery after the audience chamber on the 16th — *"He told me about the orchard. I told him about the year you were thirteen. He has my face."* The second on the 22nd evening, at her chamber door, after the Queen. They embrace for the second time, longer. *"Sera-min. — Go well."* No more is said.

Cassian wins three bouts after the first and is in the final at the third hour of the closing day. **Wins the joust outright** against the Whitewater Hills champion Sir Aelin in five passes — a thing many did not expect. Aelric presents the **Hoarfrost chain** on the field in person: a silver chain of small linked frost-roses, the centrepiece a single open Caldwyn rose in dark steel and silver, the year cut into the back. Puts it over Cassian's head himself, in the open, with the closing-day crowd watching. The Aerinhal contingent has not stopped smiling.

## Scene 19 — The closing feast, Long Hall of the Spire, 23rd First-Frost evening

The Long Hall opened for a feast for the first time since before the King's father died. Three hundred and fifty present: Caldros lords and wives, foreign delegations grouped at the cross-table, Lower City guilds at the door table. Four banners — Caldwyn rose, Aerinhal phoenix, Aelthyn tower, Whitewater stag. Twelve heralds in dress livery.

Dais: Aelric in the great crown, Lysandra in slate-blue with the small state crown, Theodric and Eira at the second seats (Eira in pale green with the small Aelthyn pearl, sitting close), Daron at the third, **Caelum at the rail in working steel as master of arms.**

Sera is on the floor at the cross-table, **between Cassian and Lord Vesper (the Aerinhal Ambassador, an older Verrent uncle of Cassian's father).** She is in Caldwyn deep blue with **a narrow band of old-wine-red at the bodice and at the hem** — Caldros silver embroidery; the two reds half a shade apart; *Holsbrand has been telling people.* The silver circlet at her brow; hair up in the public knot, three small silver pins.

**Cassian:** dark Verrent court dress, the Hoarfrost chain at his collar tonight, the Verrent rose at the inner collar in dull gold. Stands close. Her right hand has been in the crook of his left arm for a quarter-hour. Tonight she may.

**Memorable line, Cassian under the noise:** *"My father's letter was the third draft. I have been told what was in it. I will tell you tomorrow on the road. — But know now: it was a good letter, Sera. My father did well by you."*

Lord Vesper crosses the floor to the dais, opens the letter, reads the Aerinhal formal in the inner-court tongue: *"By the hand of his Most Serene Majesty Roderic the Fifth, King of Aerinhal… to his royal brother of Caldros, Aelric the Third… the House of Verrent does, by this letter and by the seal which follows, formally request the consent of the King of Caldros to the betrothal of his daughter, the Princess Seraphina, to the Prince Cassian Verrent, with a view to a marriage to be celebrated, in the Aerinhal rite, in the cathedral of Verrentine, on the Feast of the Lesser Lights, of this current year of Our Lord."*

Aelric rises. Speaks the consent from the dais himself, no paper:

> *"The King of Caldros, having received the request of his royal brother of Aerinhal, and having considered it in the company of his Queen, and in the company of his daughter, and in the company of his counsellors — gives, gladly, his consent."*

Calls Sera and Cassian to the dais. They walk the long carpet. Sera puts her hand in her father's. He turns her by it to face the room.

> *"The Princess Seraphina of Caldros and the Prince Cassian Verrent of Aerinhal are betrothed, by my hand, in the sight of this Hall, and the sight of God, and the sight of our two houses, on this the closing of the Hoarfrost Tourney of the Year of the Frost-Rose. — God keep them both. God keep both their countries. — Hall: lift your cups."*

The Hall lifts. The cheer carries to the rafters and back. **Memorable line, Aelric (very quietly, under it, in Caldros, so only the dais and Cassian hear):** *"My child. My son."* — *son* said while looking at his daughter, not at Cassian, but Cassian hears it.

Lysandra's tear at the corner of one eye. Theodric stands and lifts his cup to his sister without speaking. Daron grinning, both hands on his cup. Eira stands, cup at her chest, public smile that is not public. Caelum's hand white on the rail; he allows himself one direct look at Sera — *Sera-min. Sera-min.* — and looks away.

**Memorable line, Cassian under the cheer:** *"My love. — Breathe."* His hand at the small of her back. Lightly. Once.
