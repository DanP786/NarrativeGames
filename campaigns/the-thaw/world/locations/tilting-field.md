# The Tilting Field

## Description
The tourney grounds north of the Spire, on a low rise of meadow above Lake Caldemere. Wide enough for the full tilt-list (two long lanes with the central barrier between them), the mêlée yard south of the lists, and the spectator stands wrapping the long sides. In autumn the grass has been cut short and the ground frosted hard underfoot — good footing for horses, hard on a man who falls.

The **royal pavilion** sits at the east end of the lists, raised on a wooden platform so the royal seats look out across the full length of the field. Roofed in dark green canvas worked with the Caldwyn rose-and-rime in silver. Three rows of seating: the front row for the immediate royal family, the second for the great houses of Caldros and the foreign ambassadors of rank, the third for senior court officials and the favoured.

The **competitors' yard** is at the west end, behind a high screen of painted canvas. Tents, grooms, armorers, the smell of horses and oiled steel and the sweetened wine the squires drink to stay warm. Suitors of marriageable age expected to compete are quartered here in proximity to one another — a deliberate choice of the Queen's, who likes to see the temperaments of the men her daughter might marry rubbing up against one another before any of them gets to put on a charming face for the princess.

The **spectator stands** along the long sides are open to the gentry and the wealthier merchants of Caldemere. The Lower City watches from the slope of meadow below the stands, where the grass has gone to mud already from the morning's foot traffic.

## Today (the opening ceremony)
- Convening at noon.
- Procession from the Spire down the processional road, royal family on horseback, household guard behind.
- King Aelric opens the tourney with the traditional invocation at the lists' edge. (Brief. He does not waste words.)
- Suitors and competitors present themselves in turn at the royal pavilion. Each gives a short courtesy speech, swears not to draw blood in the lists by intent, and receives a ribbon from a lady of the court — a formal honor.
- Sera will be one of the ribbon-giving ladies. The Queen has chosen her seat carefully. Several of the men presenting themselves today are men the Queen would have her marry.
- Opening melee at second hour after noon; first joust at third hour.

## Mood
Cold, bright, the kind of late-autumn day where the lake reflects the sky and everything stands out in clean lines. Wind off the water. The crowd is thick. The smell is horses, woodsmoke, mulled wine, and the heavy perfumes the foreign delegations wear.

The court is restless. Everyone knows what this tourney is, even though no one will say it aloud.
