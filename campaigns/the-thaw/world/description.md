# The world

## The Kingdoms of the Hollow Sea

Four crowned kingdoms ring the Hollow Sea — a vast, cold inland sea that has been the centre of the known world for a thousand years. The sea is too rough for war fleets most of the year and too vital to leave unwatched ever; her shores have been the centre of trade, marriage-treaty, and rivalry for as long as crowns have sat on heads here. Smaller holdings sit inland and along the periphery — the Marches, the Long Coast, the holdings of the south. Beyond, the world thins into rumour: a far-southern empire said to have once held tributaries this far north, now quiet for a century. What is over the western horizon, no map agrees.

### The four crowns

- **Caldros** — north shore. The cold kingdom. Sera's home. House **Caldwyn** has ruled from the lake-city of **Caldemere** for nine generations. The land's magic is cold: hoarfrost, fog, killing winter. The Caldwyn line is bred for it. King Aelric Caldwyn holds the **Rime Throne**.
- **Aerinhal** — south shore. Heat-aligned. House **Verrent** rules from the gold city of **Solinhar**. Traditional rival of Caldros; the two kingdoms have fought four wars in two centuries. The current peace is twenty-eight years old and was sealed by the marriage of Queen Lysandra Verrent to King Aelric — Sera's parents.
- **Therond** — east shore. Mercantile. House **Ledranne** rules from the port-city of **Vaerost**. Their magic is winds and weather at sea; their wealth is the trade routes that cross the Hollow Sea. Cool relations with both Caldros and Aerinhal, who they sell to and bargain against in equal measure.
- **Ostvar** — west shore. Stone and earth, but currently in succession crisis: King Alren Vornlin died eighteen months ago without a clear heir, and three claimants are still working it out. The kingdom is functionally divided. None of the four crowns has yet recognised any claimant; the question is open and dangerous.

### Inland and elsewhere
- **The Marches** — fractured duchies along the southern inland border between Caldros and Aerinhal. No central king. Ruled by warlord-counts who change loyalty with the season.
- **The Long Coast** — south-eastern strip below Therond. Independent city-states, each smaller than a kingdom, often clients of Therond or Aerinhal.
- **The far south** — the rumoured empire. Once a power; quiet now. Anyone claiming to have been there is either a liar or rich enough to be worth listening to.

## Magic — what is known

Magic in this world is rare and bloodline-bound. The great houses have been marrying for it for centuries, consolidating gifts the way other lineages consolidate land. A commoner with magic is a once-in-a-generation curiosity; a noble with magic is expected; a king with magic is the standard.

Each of the four crowned bloodlines carries a distinct magical inheritance, tuned to the magic of its land:
- **Caldwyn (Caldros)** — cold. Frost, fog, blizzard, the slow killing breath of winter.
- **Verrent (Aerinhal)** — fire. Heat, flame, drought.
- **Ledranne (Therond)** — wind and weather. Sea-storm, fair wind, the working of clouds.
- **Vornlin (Ostvar)** — stone and earth. Tremor, root, the still strength of the mountain.

Magic is not infinite. Even a strongly gifted noble has limits — fatigue, illness, the slow toll of overuse. Most noble magics are useful but bounded. What separates royalty from nobility is the **land-binding** at coronation: the reigning monarch becomes the channel through which the land's magic moves at scale. A coronated King Aelric can call winter across a battlefield; his sons cannot. The toll is severe; old kings of Caldros are said to die with ice in their bones.

### The Rime Throne and the Hoarfrost Pact

The land-binding of Caldros is called the **Hoarfrost Pact**. Its full mechanism is the carefully guarded knowledge of the Caldwyn line and the priesthood of the lake. What is publicly known: a Caldwyn of the direct line is crowned upon the **Rime Throne** in the cathedral at the lake's edge; something happens; the new monarch can do things they could not do the day before. The bond can be broken — by death, by abdication, by usurpation that the land refuses to bless — but doing so is reputed to be a slow and ugly business.

### Rare gifts

A small handful of magics fall outside the bloodline economy and outside the land-bindings:
- **Mind-touching** — the ability to perceive another person's thoughts directly. One or two practitioners may be alive in the known world; the last confirmed one died forty years ago. The crown of Caldros has historically suppressed knowledge of any active mind-toucher. Most courtiers treat the gift as ghost-story.
- **Divining / dreamwalking / prophecy** — exists in the same register as mind-touching. Hedge-witches sell some version of these to credulous customers; *real* prophecy, if it exists, is suspect, expensive, and never gives a clean answer.

These rare gifts are not part of the noble lineage system. They appear where they appear. Crowns watch for them.

## Religion and the priesthood of the lake

The cult of the lake at Caldemere is the official religion of Caldros, though "religion" is a loose word for what is in practice a custodial priesthood that maintains the cathedral, witnesses coronations, and tends certain rituals around birth, marriage, and death. The other three kingdoms have their own cults — the Sun-temples of Aerinhal, the Eight Winds of Therond, the Stone-kept of Ostvar — and traffic with them is correct but cool. There is no shared faith across the Hollow Sea.

## What a royal knows about right now

- The **Ostvar succession crisis** is the live issue. Three claimants. None recognised yet by any other crown. Whichever way Caldros tips will tilt the balance.
- **Therond** has been quietly cultivating Caldros and Aerinhal alike. Trade. They want a Caldwyn match for someone. The Queen would consider it.
- **Aerinhal** is, despite the peace, the kingdom the Queen would most like to send Sera to — a second peace-marriage to consolidate her own family's hold and bury the last embers of war. There is a Verrent prince of suitable age. Sera dislikes him for reasons that may or may not be reasons.
- The **Marches** are quiet this season, which means something is being plotted there that no one has heard about yet.
