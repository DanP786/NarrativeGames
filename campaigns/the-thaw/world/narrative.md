# Narrative

Princess Seraphina Caldwyn, third in line to the Rime Throne of Caldros, has refused suitors for four years. Her mother has set a deadline. The Hoarfrost Tourney opens at the capital, staged as a marriage market in everything but name. Sera is twenty. She has seven months.

## The first day of the Hoarfrost Tourney (14th of First-Frost)
On the morning of the opening procession, a small girl of the bakers' guild was pushed forward to give Sera a wreath of bay-and-berry with three winter roses bound in red thread — the same shape the wreath had taken when it was given to her mother on her own first procession as a peace-bride twenty-eight years before. Sera took the wreath, plucked one rose, returned it to the child, and hung the rest on her saddle's pommel for the realm to see. The crowd's noise broke for it. The wreath survived the day on Whitewater's saddle.

At the opening ceremony of the lists, the Queen contrived that her daughter would tie the green-and-silver of Caldros around the wrist of Prince Cassian Verrent of Aerinhal — the marriage-match Lysandra has been quietly preferring since spring. Sera tied it a fraction too tight. Cassian did not flinch. The matter was, after that, no longer privately discussed by anyone who had been in the pavilion. In the same hour, the personal envoy of **Aerwin Vornlin** — third claimant to the Ostvar succession, son of a younger Vornlin prince thought long dead in exile — was formally received in the lists. The Rime Court had not previously acknowledged his existence. Aelric did not close his face on the question; he opened it, in his way.

Sera crossed two seats to sit with her brother's wife Eira, for the first time in three years of marriage, and found in her sister-in-law a woman trained at the long-knife and the short-spear in the Frostmarch yards until she was twelve, who has not lifted a blade in three years and misses it, and who is privately lonely in the chamber her marriage has given her. The two women have agreed, in everything but speech, to know each other now. A Frostmarch friend will be summoned north.

That evening, in her mother's small sitting room, Sera confirmed for Lysandra what Lysandra had separately confirmed for herself: that the Crown Prince of Caldros loves Ser Caelum Vesper, and has for almost four years; that the Queen has known three of those four; that the not-doing has been her policy and is now Sera's also. Lysandra promised her daughter that nothing would be done to Caelum without Sera hearing it from the Queen herself, in person. She also said she would ask, before the tourney closed, whether Theodric can stop — and that the answer to that question is the question of what is to be done next.

Returning to her own rooms late, Sera went instead toward the east garret to warn Caelum. She was intercepted in the gallery by Cassian Verrent, who showed her a kind of patience the court has not had with her in four years — clean offers, refused implications, a precisely judged cover story when a footfall came up the cross-corridor. The footfall was Caelum. He walked past them where they stood against the wall and made one small dry sound to say *I have seen and I am not in this room.* He did not stop. At her door Sera laughed, for the first time in months, and named the colour of a cloak she will accept from Aerinhal — a red the colour of old wine, with silver-grey wool inside, no Verrent crest. She called Cassian by his first name in the corridor. He has not, this night, asked her any question whose answer he does not deserve.

She is, perhaps, not entirely the woman she was this morning.
