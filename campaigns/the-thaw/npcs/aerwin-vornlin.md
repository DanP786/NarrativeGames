# Aerwin Vornlin

## Status
Alive. ~35–36. Third claimant to the Ostvar succession. Marchwarden's-purse knight of the eastern marches of Caldros. Son of the small fort's commander at **Ostvar pass** (eastern Caldros), who held the fort thirty-one years and has been four winters dead.

## Description
A working face. Thirty-five or thirty-six, the small lines that come from squinting at distance. Plain working wool in dark colours; the small **Marchwarden pin** at the collar his only ornament. Brown working cloak. The face of a man who has spent more of his life outside than in. Not handsome; not unhandsome; the kind of face that does not announce itself across a room and rests in the memory afterward.

## Voice & mannerisms
Caldros tongue, the eastern-marches accent — a slightly slower vowel, a faintly older grammar than the Rime Court. Working register; sparing with words. Speaks in **discrete sentences** numbered to themselves: the man clearly composes before he opens his mouth and does not improvise. Bows correctly; speaks with his eyes lowered to a listener's throat or collar until invited up, then meets the gaze for the duration of the work and not a second more.

When he asks for something he places himself so the person he is asking can refuse without effort. He does not block; he positions. The first courtesy of the eastern marches: *make the no easy, and the yes earned.*

## Goals & pressures
- **Be acknowledged on the Ostvar question.** Three claimants exist; he is the third in formal precedence and the only one currently in the kingdom. He has spent the autumn at Caldemere on his own purse to be seen by the King. He has been seen for nine days at the rail and has not been received.
- **Reopen the Ostvar pass** — the eastern Caldros / western Aerinhal pass closed seven years ago. The two kingdoms each have reasons not to be the one who acts first; nobody at either court has been the woman who wanted it open. He thinks Sera could be.
- **His father's name.** Thirty-one years' service at Ostvar. The barley sown on the slope where his father walked the lower wall. He carries this without ornament; it is his work, not his grievance.

## Relationship to Sera
**Neutral.** Has placed himself on her ledger by his own choice. Asks for nothing she must carry; gave her three sentences in a corridor at the eleventh bell and went. **She has committed to nothing.**

## History with Sera
- **23rd First-Frost, 1142, half past the eleventh bell — the upper corridor of the family wing, third bay window.** Waited there an hour and a half since the third quarter past the tenth, in the small hope she would come back to her apartments that way after the closing feast. Bowed properly; spoke three sentences; bowed again; went. Did not ask for letters or memory. Asked only that, when in five or seven years she sits at a table where closed passes are being discussed and someone asks *what is at Ostvar* — she not say *nothing.* Parted with *"Safe travel south, Your Highness. The Westmark road is hard going in First-Frost. Wear the cloak."* Cloak detail placed him: he had noticed which cloak and what it meant.
- Sera, before he spoke, masked herself back into the older face and said only *speak then.* Did not give him a single inch of her face throughout. Did not say yes; did not say no; did not thank him; did not have him removed. Heard him out. **Let him go.**

## The three sentences in their compressed form

1. **The standing.** Marchwarden's purse, no banner, two men, no sponsor. Council on Ostvar at Caldemere in eleven days. Marchwarden, Lord of the eastern lakes, Master of the Border Roads in it. *He* will not be.
2. **The geography.** Westmark road within forty miles of Ostvar. Aerinhal keeps it open at their cost for the grain. Sera will, within a year, hold a Verrent inner-court name, an Aerinhal court's ear, and the ear of an Aerinhal queen-mother who has fifteen years of writing letters about closed mountain passes. *"The pass has been closed seven years because no one at either court has been the woman who wanted it open."*
3. **The ask.** *Not memory. Not letters.* When the question *what is at Ostvar* comes to her one day, do not say *nothing.* The barley on the slope is going to be the only thing off Ostvar in ten years if the pass stays closed.

## Knowledge
- Ostvar — every inch of the pass, every stone of his father's fort, every name on the lower-wall garrison's roll.
- The eastern Caldros marches at the working level.
- The Westmark road — the Aerinhal grain road; the cost of keeping it open through First-Frost and Second-Frost; the cattle-tracks that meet it from the Caldros side at three places.
- The council scheduled at Caldemere in eleven days and the three men who will attend it.
- Lord Vesper by face, having seen him at the rail. *Has not introduced himself.*
- That Cassian Verrent's father has, on the Aerinhal side, been writing about closed passes for years. *(Not yet verified by Sera.)*
- *Does not know* what the Verrent inner court is actually saying about him in private — only that he has not been received.
- *Does not know* that Sera has now placed herself in Cassian's carriage by her own choice, or what that means about how far she has chosen the marriage.

## Relationship-shift triggers
- **Up (toward Friendly):** Sera writes him from Verrentine, or asks her mother to write him, or names Ostvar at a Verrent table within the year.
- **Side (toward something quieter):** Sera does nothing for six months and the pass stays closed. He will, on his side, do nothing. He has said so.
- **Down (toward Wary):** Sera puts his three sentences in the wrong hand and the wrong court hears them. He will not act against her — he will withdraw and the relationship will close on his side, civilly.
- **Hostile / Nemesis:** Not on the cards by his character. He has not asked for anything; he cannot be betrayed by the lack of a thing he did not ask for.

## What he will do if she does nothing
He will stay at Caldemere through Second-Frost and into the spring on his Marchwarden's purse, asking once more to be received. If not received, will ride east to Ostvar and winter there. If the council in eleven days decides against the pass — which it probably will, by his own reading — he will live with it. He has done so before.

## On-screen first scene
Already on-screen — the corridor at half past the eleventh bell on the 23rd of First-Frost. Next plausible on-screen: by letter from Caldemere; by report through Aelric's councils; or, far ahead, at a table at Verrentine where the closed pass comes up.
