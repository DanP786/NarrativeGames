# Ser Caelum Vesper

## Status
Alive. 21. Second son of Lord Vesper, a minor Caldros lord whose seat lies a day's ride east of Caldemere. Knighted a year ago, at twenty. Sworn man-at-arms to Crown Prince Theodric's personal household. The Crown Prince's lover for the last three years. Princess Seraphina's best friend for twelve.

## Description
Tall, slim, sandy-brown hair worn just long enough to fall in his eyes when he is tired. Hazel eyes. Slight freckles across the bridge of his nose, which he hated at fifteen and has made peace with. The kind of face that is more handsome on second look than on first — the smile rearranges the rest of it.

Has a small magical aptitude (Vesper blood is thin for ice; he was never expected to be a battlefield mage). His working capacity is roughly *Sera's nominal ceiling pushed slightly higher* — a real chill to a blade edge, a personal field around himself in winter, modest. Has never minded that he is not powerful. The Caldwyns have enough magic in the family for everyone.

## Voice & mannerisms
Warm, ready, the kind of voice you trust before you have thought about whether you should. Quick to smile. Quicker than he should be to take a teasing jab — he gives as good as he gets, and Sera and Daron have both been on the losing end of his tongue once or twice.

In private, with Theodric or Sera or both, he is quieter and more careful, and the careful is what tells you what the affair has cost him.

**He has a small dry sound** — a half-cough, half-throat-clear — that he has been making in Sera's vicinity since they were eleven and twelve, when one of them wished to signal to the other *I have seen and I am choosing not to be in this room.* Sera, in fifteen years, has never heard him use it for anyone else.

## Goals & pressures
- Love Theodric without destroying him.
- Be a good knight in a household he has sworn to.
- Keep Sera laughing on the days she most does not want to.
- Survive his own life.
- **Tonight, as of First-Frost 14th at the ninth bell, a new goal he did not have at dawn:** understand what he saw in the gallery, and decide what to do about it.

## Relationship to Sera
**Devoted.** He arrived at Caldemere at nine, fostered into court service. Sera was eight. They were trouble together within a month. The friendship is older than every other relationship in his adult life.

**As of First-Frost 14th:** he came down from the east garret at the ninth bell to find her — she had not come to him, and Sera-not-coming when she has had a hard day is a thing he reads as urgent. He turned the cross-corridor and saw her against the wall under the cloak of Cassian Verrent of Aerinhal, the prince's face at her throat, the geometry unmistakable to any angle of the gallery. He kept walking. He cleared his throat once as he passed — *I have seen and I am not in this room.* He did not stop at her door either; he registered that she was not in her rooms and went on. **He does not yet know what he saw.** He does not know whether what he saw was real or staged. He does not know whether Sera is in danger or in pursuit. He does not know that she had just been in her mother's sitting room and that her mother has just told her *I have known three years.*

The conversation he and Sera have not yet had is the largest thing on his calendar tomorrow.

## Relationship to Theodric
The affair has been three years long. It began when he was eighteen and Theodric was twenty-two, around the time of Theodric's marriage to Eira.

**Note from First-Frost 14th:** Theodric retired to his own apartments with Eira at the ninth bell — not his pattern. Caelum had left when the Prince and Princess went in, returning to the east garret. Whether Theodric arranged this with him, or imposed it on him without warning, is open. If the latter, Caelum's coming down to find Sera was not solely about Sera — it was about Theodric too.

## History with Sera
- Arrived at Caldemere at nine; Sera was eight. They have been a unit since.
- Was present for the thing that happened when Sera was eleven or twelve. He was twelve. He remembers most of it. They have spoken about it twice — once when Sera was fourteen, once when she was nineteen. The first conversation went badly. The second went better. Neither has resolved it.
- Watched Sera turn into the woman the court calls the Ice Princess and has his own opinion about which parts of that are real.
- **First-Frost 14th, 1142** (S01):
  - Returned to the pavilion at midday at Theodric's chair-back; did not look at Sera once for the afternoon.
  - At the ninth bell: came down from the east garret to find her; saw her in the gallery with Cassian Verrent; passed without stopping; cleared his throat.

## Knowledge
- About the affair with Theodric — everything from his side.
- About Sera's interior: more than anyone else, less than he thinks.
- About the thing that happened when Sera was eleven or twelve: most of the surface. Some of the depth. Not all of it.
- About Daron's underside-of-Caldemere life: a surprising amount. He does not flatter Daron by mentioning it.
- About affairs of state: limited.
- **About Cassian Verrent:** has seen him with Sera in a way that admits two readings. He will be working on which is correct, between now and breakfast.
- **Does not yet know** that the Queen knows about him and Theodric. *Almost certainly* does not know. If he learns, he will learn from Sera.
