# Prince Aldwin Verrent of Aerinhal

## Status
Alive. ~30. **Heir to the Aerinhal throne.** Eldest son of King Roderic V of Aerinhal and the Queen of Aerinhal (name TBD). Second cousin to Cassian Verrent — Aldwin's father Roderic and Cassian's father Halric are first cousins through the shared great-great-grandfather king, Roderic by the senior royal line, Halric by the cadet branch. Aldwin is also second cousin to Sera through the same shared ancestor on the Lysandra side.

**The campaign's antagonist of record as of opening of session 04.** *(Player canon, set out of character.)*

## Standing rule — Aldwin's intent
**Aldwin has decided he wants Sera for himself.** What he intends is for her **to choose him** — the way she chose Cassian. He believes he can win that choice if he can be the man Cassian is not, in rooms Cassian does not get to be in.

The method is **subtle.** Aldwin does not plot openly; he does not engineer scandal; he does not move politically against the match in the rooms where his father can name what he is doing. He is the heir of Aerinhal, and the heir has finer tools than that. What he plans is **not known** — not to Sera, not to Cassian, not to Halric, not to the engine in advance. What he does will surface in play.

What is known about his shape:

- He wants to **tame her.** Three years ago he passed because he was told she could not be. He has decided he was wrong — or that he had not yet been the man who could. *He intends to be that man now.*
- He wants her **own yes.** Cassian got it in a corridor at the ninth bell. Aldwin believes the corridor was an accident of timing and a junior cousin's luck; he intends to put himself in front of Sera in rooms that are his, at hours that are his, and let her find the yes a second time.
- He wants to **force himself in her way.** Not coarsely. By presence, by attention, by the heir's prerogative of being where the bride-elect is. By being the room she has to walk past, the conversation she has to take, the courtesy she cannot refuse without rudeness.

He believes, with the heir's certainty, that he can do this. He has the homecourt, the magic, six years over Cassian in social-mature presence, and the patience of a man who has been governing himself since eighteen. *He may, of course, be wrong. The story does not yet know.*

## Description
**Not yet on-screen.** Working assumption: warm Verrent colouring (Roderic line — golden hair the same shade as Cassian's but paler, hazel eyes a degree colder than his cousin's, the family face on the senior line), tall, kept fit because the heir of Aerinhal trains for the coronation rite he expects to live to take. Dresses to be looked at; the heir's chain inside his own house, even at private dinner. Reads as Cassian's elder brother on first sight before the distinction surfaces.

## Marital status
**Unmarried. Currently betrothed.** Match settled within the past year. His fiancée is a **fourteen-year-old foreign princess** (name and kingdom TBD). Marriage cannot be celebrated until the bride turns sixteen — Aldwin is **two years from his wedding** as of First-Frost.

The betrothal is a dynastic match that holds the western alliance. Aldwin has met his fiancée formally once, when she was thirteen, on a state visit. She is at her own court, not at Verrentine.

The fourteen-year-old is a complication on Aldwin's board, not a barrier. He has not decided what to do about her. He does not yet need to. *Whether and when she becomes a cost-piece for the Sera campaign is a question for later play.*

## Voice & mannerisms
**TBD on first scene.** Working register: Aerinic inner-court polish; the heir's grammar; sentences that are a fraction more declarative than they need to be. The kind of man who does not, in his father's house, finish his own sentences when an inferior is in the room. (Sera will not be an inferior.) When he wants something, he does not ask twice; he arranges the second asking to come from someone else's mouth. When he is denied, he does not press; he revises and returns at a different hour. He has been trained to govern. He governs himself, mostly.

His charm is not Cassian's. Cassian's charm is patience and listening; Aldwin's is **attention** — the precise application of the heir's regard to a person who has not had the heir's regard before. The discomfort of being looked at by a man who has decided you matter to him is the working tool.

## Relationship to Sera

### Backstory — the survey three years ago
The Aerinhal crown's quiet survey of options for the heir, conducted about three years back, included **Princess Seraphina of Caldros.** She fit the formal criteria — Verrent-blooded through her mother, a princess of the lake kingdoms, beautiful by every report and by Master Hallen's portrait. The crown's inner-court committee passed her along to Aldwin for the heir's own opinion.

**Aldwin passed.** The reason, given in the small Verrent inner-court rooms where such things are said (and recorded by at least three of those who heard it): *she is hard to tame, despite being beautiful.* He preferred to wait for a match he could shape from the start — and a year and a half later, the fourteen-year-old's betrothal was agreed.

That conversation was confined to the heir's inner circle and his father. **It did not reach Caldemere — neither Aelric nor Lysandra know the option was on Aldwin's desk and was declined.** Cassian's father Halric **does** know; he was on the survey committee.

### Why he has changed his mind
Lord Vesper's letters out of Caldemere describe a woman who does not fit the *hard to tame* report. *The woman who decided her own marriage in a corridor at the ninth bell on the second night of a tourney; who walked alone into a Verrent prince's gear tent at the eighth bell; who placed herself in the Aerinhal carriage at first light of her leaving in front of the inner court.* That woman did not exist in Aldwin's three-year-old file.

His private reading of the change: she was *not* untameable. She had not been approached by a man who knew how. The Caldros suitors who came to her at sixteen, seventeen, eighteen were boys who needed her to be the woman the court had told them she would be. Cassian — by the heir's reading — got lucky: a junior cousin without preconceptions, who arrived at the right week, in the right corridor, with no script. *Aldwin could have done that, had he tried. He intends to try now.*

He does not yet know that this reading is wrong about both Sera and Cassian — Cassian did the work Aldwin would not have done, and Sera chose because of the work, not in spite of it. The arrogance in Aldwin's mistake is the lever in the campaign.

### Aldwin's posture in the inner court since the 17th First-Frost
*(Visible to the inner court; not yet visible to Sera or Cassian.)*

His public posture has moved, week-on-week, from *arrogant pity* to something cooler — quieter, more measured. The cousins who were laughing about *the difficult one* in the small rooms have noticed Aldwin no longer laughs. Some of them have begun, on their own initiative, to ask whether the match is firm. Aldwin does not answer either way. The not-answering is itself the shift.

He paid Halric a courtesy in front of a clerk at midday in the senior cadet line's offices: congratulated his uncle on Cassian's success at the lists and the Hoarfrost chain. **Halric heard, in the precision of the courtesy, the older question.** Halric has not yet written to Cassian. Whether he will, and when, is open.

## Goals & pressures
- **Make Sera choose him.** Standing rule; see above.
- **Be the man, in his own house, that the report did not name.** This is Aldwin's interior frame; he is correcting an error of three years ago rather than poaching his cousin's wife. The frame is partly self-deceiving and useful to him as a frame.
- **Inherit cleanly when his father dies.** Take the coronation rite. Acquire the Aerinhal land-binding fire.
- **Resolve, or set aside without breaking, his own betrothal** to the fourteen-year-old foreign princess. He will think about it when he has to.
- **Get through the next eight to twelve weeks** without an incident he cannot recover from publicly. Subtle is the discipline.

## Magic
**Strong Verrent fire — the strongest of his generation.** Will inherit the full Aerinhal land-binding fire at coronation. Until then, his personal fire is at the top of the individual register — can lay flame across a battlefield length, can heat a man's blade until it cannot be held, can set a hearth from across a room. He does not display this casually; it would be vulgar in his father's house. *He may, however, display it for a particular witness, at a particular hour, in a particular room, for a reason that is not vulgar at all.*

**Cassian's fire is *moderate-Verrent.* Aldwin's fire is *high-Verrent.*** The asymmetry is structural. Cassian knows this. Aldwin knows Cassian knows.

## Asymmetries

**Favouring Aldwin:**
- Heir of Aerinhal. His father is the King. The house is his.
- Homecourt — the rooms, the corridors, the small access an heir has to a bride-elect under his father's roof.
- Magic outpowers Cassian's.
- Six years older than Cassian; longer in the social-mature register; longer in inner-court conversation; longer in governing himself.
- The novelty: Sera has only ever been around Cassian. The heir is a man of a kind she has not been near.
- Subtlety — his method is the kind Cassian is least practised at countering.

**Against Aldwin:**
- The match is written, witnessed, and blessed in open Hall by the King of Caldros.
- Sera has chosen Cassian. Her own *yes* is on record. She has resisted manipulation for four years; her instinct for being shaped is sharp.
- Cassian's actual virtues — patience, listening, the long answer — are not the heir's currency. *Cassian won her on ground Aldwin has not stood on.* Aldwin does not know this and will, possibly, learn.
- Halric has heard the older question and is positioned to defend his son. Lord Vesper has been at Caldemere a month and has met Sera; his voice in the council weighs.
- Time. Seven to eight weeks to the Feast of the Lesser Lights. The campaign window closes at the cathedral.
- The fourteen-year-old fiancée. Aldwin cannot move publicly without addressing her.

## What Sera and Cassian know about Aldwin's intent
**Neither knows.** Sera knows that Cassian has an elder cousin Aldwin who is the heir, that the cousin is unmarried, that the cousin has a fourteen-year-old foreign betrothed waiting. She does not know the *hard to tame* judgement was Aldwin's. She does not know what he has decided. Cassian knows the *hard to tame* judgement was his cousin's and that the inner-court rooms have been laughing about the pity. Cassian **does not yet know** the laughter has stopped and what has replaced it. Halric is positioned to be the first to tell him; has not yet written.

*This is the asymmetry of information at session 04's opening, and the engine should preserve it.*

## On the campaign as engine-direction
The engine **does not telegraph Aldwin's plans.** What he plans is not in this file because the world does not know it. What is in this file is his disposition, his goals, his shape. When he comes on-screen the engine will play him: subtle, watchful, the heir's attention precisely applied; the small social calibration that makes Cassian look like a junior cousin without anyone being able to name the slight; the offer of a book, a ride out, a kindness to Anwen, a question about Sera's grandmother; the display of fire at a hearth that Cassian's moderate fire could not have lit; the conversation in a room where Sera ends up alone with him because the heir has arranged for it. None of it large enough to be objected to. None of it accidental.

What Aldwin does *not* do, by character: confront Cassian publicly; coerce Sera; engineer scandal; move politically against the betrothal in the rooms where his father can name the move. He has finer tools. The discipline is part of what makes him dangerous.
