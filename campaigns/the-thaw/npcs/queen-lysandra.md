# Queen Lysandra Caldwyn née Verrent

## Status
Alive. 47. Queen of Caldros, married to King Aelric for twenty-eight years. Originally Princess Lysandra of Aerinhal, the youngest daughter of the previous Verrent king, sent north as a peace-bride at nineteen. **Sister to the reigning King of Aerinhal, Roderic V; cousin to Prince Halric Verrent (Cassian's father).** Sera's betrothal makes Cassian her cousin-once-removed-and-now-son-in-law.

## Description
Honey-skinned, golden-haired, brown-eyed — Aerinhal coloring, never quite adjusted to twenty-eight winters of Caldemere light. Strikingly still beautiful in her late forties, in the warm way that her daughter is beautiful in the cold way. Dresses in Caldros colors but in Aerinhal cuts — a small, deliberate signal that the court learned to read about ten years in.

## Voice & mannerisms
Soft, lightly accented Caldros tongue (she has worked at the accent and almost gotten rid of it; the last trace of Aerinic shows under stress or affection). Speaks slowly. Touches the people she is talking to — a hand on a sleeve, a brush at the elbow — in a way Caldros nobles do not, which is part of the reason she has always seemed slightly foreign and part of the reason she has been so disarmingly effective. She is the politest woman in any room, and the most dangerous, often at the same time.

**Turns a cup a quarter at a time on its saucer when working through a difficult matter** — a tell Sera now recognises. The cup completes the full revolution when the matter does. *(Across the small parlour at the seventh bell of the 15th, the four quarters of her cup marked the four pivots of the conversation.)*

In her own apartments she wears her hair down. The Aerinic accent comes closer to the surface. She is, in the small private room, a different woman than the one the court sees.

## Goals & pressures
- See her children well — *well* a word she has thought hard about for twenty-eight years.
- **Sera now formally settled to Cassian Verrent — the marriage of Sera done.** The goal that has dominated her last four years has resolved. The Queen is, as of session 02 close, the more shaken of the two parents by the actual going; she has spent the closing days speaking Aerinic to her daughter and giving her the peace-bride's locket.
- Maintain the Caldros-Aerinhal peace her own marriage purchased. Twenty-eight years and counting — now doubled by Sera's marriage.
- **Protect Theodric and Caelum without losing either of them** — still her project, now Sera's only by inheritance rather than presence. Has agreed with herself, by silent compact with Caelum, to not move on the question through the tourney. Has not yet decided what she will do after.
- **See Eira whole.** Riannon Aelthyn coming south within the month. The Aelthyn letter went out on the 16th, signed under the Queen's by Eira's hand. Returned acceptance from Lord Aelthyn at the Frostmarch on the 21st.
- *Newly articulated, possibly for the first time at the small parlour on the evening of the 22nd:* she is afraid she has been asking her daughter to walk into a version of her own room, and she has spent that evening in Aerinic giving Sera what she did not have at nineteen.

## Relationship to Sera
**Devoted (transformed).** A two-sided relationship has resolved into one. Lysandra contrived the introduction to Cassian; Sera did the rest. The conflict between them on *the marriage of Sera* has been overtaken by Sera's own choice. The conflict on *the marriage of Theodric* is still shared, and unresolved, and held over by silent agreement through the tourney.

**As of the evening of the 22nd First-Frost**, Lysandra spent an entire evening in Aerinic alone with her daughter in the small parlour, and gave her the small silver locket her own mother had given her on the morning she left Aerinhal at nineteen. *"This goes south with you. I should have given it to you four years ago. I have wanted to keep it close to me. I am glad to be wrong about that, my love."*

She will be at the gate at first light. She will not break in front of the court.

## History with Sera (running record)
- Birthed her, raised her, walked her through court protocol at nine.
- After the thing that happened when Sera was eleven or twelve — Lysandra was present for some of it, absent for the rest. She has never spoken to Sera about it directly.
- Offered the choice-of-husband privilege at sixteen, against her own better judgement, because the King wanted it for her. The deadline was her one condition.
- Three conversations about marriage in the six months before the tourney. None went well. None went badly.
- **14th First-Frost** (S01): the wreath, the ribbon-tying, the sitting-room conversation that put the Theodric/Caelum question on the table between mother and daughter for the first time in three years. Promised not to act against Caelum without Sera being told first.
- **15th First-Frost** (S02):
  - The small parlour at the seventh bell. *"You have dressed to be a princess in your mother's small parlour at the seventh bell. I should be flattered or worried. I have not yet decided."* Did not press the deflection on the circlet. Wrote the Aelthyn letter as a joint piece of work; revised it from *on Eira's behalf* to *on the Queen's own* when Sera asserted Eira should be told first; took it to Eira at the third bell. Named the three things of the morning: the letter, *what Sera thought of him,* and the Aerwin Vornlin / Ser Yarrick matter. Was told the cloak colours by Sera unprompted; crossed to her daughter and rested fingers on the circlet, then on the cheekbone, then on the crown of the head, then leaned down and kissed the crown of Sera's head. Warned about Cassian's long fuse: *"He will not break in front of you. He will break in front of someone else."*
  - Tenth bell that night: received Ser Yarrick in her private rooms. Was the carefully Caldros first two-thirds of an hour about Marchwarden purses and Holsbrand's deliveries; in the last third, in inner-court Aerinic, the question about *receiving a letter from my brother before the tourney closes.* Said *yes.*
- **16th First-Frost** (S02): At family parlour breakfast in slate-blue, no crown, the family-table version of herself. Laid out the morning's protocol for the household. *"Eat, my love. The quince is very good this morning."* Sat at Aelric's right in the audience chamber when the King received Sera; was deliberately not the Queen but Sera's mother by dress. Asked Sera to wear the cloak to her father, and was right that he would notice.
- **17th–22nd First-Frost** (S02): The administrative work of a daughter's leaving. Wardrobe cut and packed; Anwen's leave granted and her papers prepared; letters of introduction written for the Verrent inner-court women Lysandra has not seen in twenty-eight years and now writes to with the slightly old vocabulary of someone fluent but out of practice.
- **22nd First-Frost evening** (S02): Small parlour with Sera, Aerinic the whole evening. The peace-bride's locket. The single grain of cardamom inside. *"I am glad to be wrong about that, my love."*
- **23rd First-Frost** (S02): The closing feast. In slate-blue and the small state crown. One tear at the corner of one eye during the announcement that she did not allow herself to wipe.

## Knowledge
- Aerinhal politics inside out. Verrent family inside out. Has, for nine days, been quietly reactivating contacts at the Verrent inner court she has not used in years.
- The Caldros court she has built her own map of, careful, separate from the King's.
- The names, ages, magical aptitudes, and rumoured personalities of every plausible suitor for Sera in all four kingdoms — most of which is now retired knowledge.
- About Sera's magic stopping at party tricks. Has her own theory, kept private. May share it after Sera reaches Verrentine; the Verrent court has its own scholarship on shared-blood gifts.
- **About Theodric and Caelum — has known three years.** Holds for now. Will likely move sometime in winter; whether before Sera's wedding or after has not been decided.
- *Does not yet know whether Aelric knows about Theodric and Caelum.* Has decided not to put it in his hands until she knows what she wants him to do with it.
- *Knows now* — by closing day — that Theodric and Eira are sitting closer than they have in three years. Whether what happened in their apartments on the night of the 14th is durable or borrowed for the tourney remains the open question. She is, privately, daring to hope.
