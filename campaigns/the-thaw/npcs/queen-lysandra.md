# Queen Lysandra Caldwyn née Verrent

## Status
Alive. 47. Queen of Caldros, married to King Aelric for twenty-eight years. Originally Princess Lysandra of Aerinhal, the youngest daughter of the previous Verrent king, sent north as a peace-bride at nineteen.

## Description
Honey-skinned, golden-haired, brown-eyed — Aerinhal coloring, never quite adjusted to twenty-eight winters of Caldemere light. Strikingly still beautiful in her late forties, in the warm way that her daughter is beautiful in the cold way. Dresses in Caldros colors but in Aerinhal cuts — a small, deliberate signal that the court learned to read about ten years in.

## Voice & mannerisms
Soft, lightly accented Caldros tongue (she has worked at the accent and almost gotten rid of it; the last trace of Aerinic shows under stress or affection). Speaks slowly. Touches the people she is talking to — a hand on a sleeve, a brush at the elbow — in a way Caldros nobles do not, which is part of the reason she has always seemed slightly foreign and part of the reason she has been so disarmingly effective. She is the politest woman in any room, and the most dangerous, often at the same time.

In her own apartments she wears her hair down. The Aerinic accent comes closer to the surface. She is, in the small private room, a different woman than the one the court sees.

## Goals & pressures
- See her children well. *Well* is a word she has thought hard about over twenty-eight years and has reached her own conclusions about.
- See Sera, specifically, in a position where she will not be politically expendable to a king who is not her father. She still believes a daughter of hers can survive and prosper in a peace-marriage with a *better* partner than her own. The deadline has not moved.
- Maintain the Caldros-Aerinhal peace her own marriage purchased. Twenty-eight years and counting.
- **Protect Theodric and Caelum without losing either of them** — a project she has been carrying privately for three years and that, as of the first night of the Hoarfrost Tourney, she is now carrying with her daughter. The plan, as articulated to Sera: nothing tonight, nothing this tourney; watch, think; talk to the King when she knows what she thinks; ask Sera, before the tourney closes, whether she thinks Theodric can stop. Then act.
- **Help Eira.** A new project, articulated at the same conversation. An Aelthyn-house friend will be summoned north.
- *Not yet articulated, possibly noticed for the first time at the fire:* she is beginning to be afraid that her own marriage was harder than she has let herself remember, and that she has been asking her daughter to walk into a version of the same room.

## Relationship to Sera
**Conspirator (on Theodric/Caelum), Antagonist (on Sera's marriage).** A two-sided relationship from the night of First-Frost 14th forward. The two women confronted, in the small sitting room, the fact that they had each been carrying the same secret for three years and not telling each other. The Queen has formally promised: nothing will be done to Caelum Vesper without Sera being told first, in person, by Lysandra herself. The Queen has also openly said *I love you and I love your brother and I love Caelum Vesper in the in-his-way.* She has invited Sera to be company in the looking.

On the marriage of Sera, the conflict is unchanged. Lysandra contrived the ribbon-tying with Cassian Verrent on the opening day specifically to let Sera see what wanting looked like on herself; she has not retreated from this position and likely will not. The Aerinhal match remains her preference. The deadline (Sera's twenty-first name-day) remains in force.

She is no longer a clean antagonist. She is harder.

## History with Sera
- Birthed her, raised her, walked her through court protocol at nine.
- After the thing that happened when Sera was eleven or twelve — Lysandra was present for some of it, absent for the rest. She has never spoken to Sera about it directly.
- Offered the choice-of-husband privilege at sixteen, against her own better judgement, because the King wanted it for her. The deadline was her one condition.
- Three conversations about marriage in the last six months. None has gone well. None has gone badly.
- **First-Frost 14th, 1142** (S01):
  - Procession: gave Sera one soft instruction on the courtyard stones; did not turn when Sera refused it; heard the baker's-child sound at her back and did not turn.
  - Pavilion: contrived that the steward bring the green-and-silver ribbon to Sera for Cassian Verrent's wrist. After: *"You wanted to see him. And I wanted you to see what wanting looks like on you, when you are not refusing it."* And: *"You wounded him. Cleanly."*
  - Pavilion (later): brushed the back of her gloved knuckle against Sera's for one breath when Sera requested the audience.
  - Sitting room: hair down, slate-grey, the Aerinic accent forward. Confronted by Sera with the Theodric-and-Caelum question. Plainly: *I have known three years.* Promised not to act against Caelum. Asked the *who is he* question and received an evasion; produced the three-year inventory of how she has read the relationship herself. Set the *can he stop* question as the one she will ask before the tourney closes. Invited Sera to breakfast at the seventh bell the next morning. Said *please* — first time in years.

## Knowledge
- Aerinhal politics inside out. Verrent family inside out.
- The Caldros court she has built her own map of, careful, separate from the King's.
- The names, ages, magical aptitudes, and rumoured personalities of every plausible suitor for Sera in all four kingdoms.
- About Sera's magic stopping at party tricks. Has her own theory, kept private.
- **About Theodric and Caelum — knows, has known three years, has been feigning not seeing.** Has likely had a private conversation with the Aerinhal senior knight (Ser Yarrick) during the afternoon of First-Frost 14th, though the content of this is not yet known to Sera.
- *Does not yet know whether the King knows.* Has decided not to put the information into Aelric's hands until she knows what she wants him to do with it.
- *Does not yet know whether Theodric has retired to his apartments with Eira at the ninth bell on First-Frost 14th* — but Sera knows she did not arrange it.
