# Prince Daron Caldwyn

## Status
Alive. 22. Second son of King Aelric. The Spare. Unmarried (and reliably scandalous about it). Holds the courtesy title Prince of the Inner Lake, which he uses when he needs it and forgets about the rest of the time.

## Description
Compact, wiry, faster than he looks. Dark hair worn longer than the court likes — past the collar; he is told to cut it twice a year and ignores the request. The Caldwyn eyes, the Caldwyn jaw. Bears a thin pale scar across his left cheekbone from a tavern fight at nineteen that he has never quite explained, and a missing fraction of his right earlobe from a fight he refused to explain even to Sera.

Dresses for the court when he must and for himself when he can. The court version is impeccable. The self version is closer to a sellsword's.

## Voice & mannerisms
Quick, sharp, charming, profane in private. Laughs easily. Can be cutting; usually saves it for people who can take it. His charm is genuine — he likes people — but he is not careful with them, and the men and women he has slept with discover this in their own time.

When he is angry he goes very still, which is the only warning he ever gives anyone.

## Goals & pressures
- Be no king and never be a king. This is bedrock. He would refuse the throne if it were offered tomorrow and the realm would have to find a Caldwyn cousin.
- Sleep with whoever he wants, drink what he wants, fight when he must.
- **Protect Sera from everything he cannot stop her from doing to herself.** *(As of the close of session 2: she has chosen her own marriage, and Daron has decided, on his own private reckoning, that Cassian Verrent will do. The threat-promise stands.)*
- Manage the slow weight of being a son the realm has decided is the disappointment without becoming the disappointment in any way that costs Sera.

## Relationship to Sera
**Devoted.** She is the soft place. Has been since she was small. He is three years older; he was nine when she was six and watching her brother do something brave, and the moral economy of the rest of his life was set then.

He can make her laugh in a way that breaks her public face. She can make him stop a thing he was about to do with a single look. The two of them have a language built of one-word sentences and shared glances. The court has noticed. The court does not entirely understand.

- **23rd First-Frost** (S02): The closing feast. When the cheer began he lifted the cup and let himself laugh — loud, three counts, into the noise of the Hall.

## Battlefield magic
Has the family ice properly. Less than Theodric in finesse, similar in raw power, dirtier in application — Daron will frost a knife edge in a fight, throw a sudden chill at a man's grip on a hilt, fog a room to escape it. The court trainer's polite words are "creative." The court trainer's private words are "the kind of fighter who does not lose, exactly, but you do not always want him on the field."

## History with Sera
- Older brother since her birth.
- Was thirteen when the thing happened that Sera was eleven or twelve for. He remembers more of it than Theodric does and less than the King and Queen. The two of them have spoken about it once, when she was fifteen, in the orchard at midnight. Nothing was resolved.
- Has fought, slept with, drunk with, befriended, betrayed, and reconciled with an unflattering number of people over the last four or five years. Sera knows about most of them and disapproves of half.
- **14th–23rd First-Frost** (S01–S02): see above.

## Knowledge
- More about the underside of Caldemere than any other Caldwyn — taverns, sellsword captains, the houses where contracts are written between men of bad reputation. Sera does not always know how he knows what he knows.
- About Sera's magic stopping at party tricks. Has wondered, briefly, whether her not-being-good-at-it has anything to do with the orchard conversation, and put the thought down because it scared him.
- About Theodric and Caelum: *does not know with certainty, suspects strongly.* Has not asked his brother and will not. He is not a man who tells secrets, but if pressed he would lie clumsily on this one. *(As of the close of session 2 — given how visibly Eira and Theodric are sitting close — he is, for the first time, hoping he is wrong.)*
- About Aerwin Vornlin: by the closing day of the tourney, Daron has somehow acquired more information about Vornlin's life in exile than the Rime Council has. He has not yet handed any of it to his father. He is reading it himself first. *(Open thread for future play.)*

## At the gate at first light
Will be there. Will ride with Sera to the city's south gate (the second gate beyond the Spire's south gate) — has, on his own initiative, arranged this with the master of horse; will then turn back. Wants the extra quarter-hour with her. Will say nothing of substance during it; the orchard day was the goodbye. Will, however, at the city's south gate, lean across from his horse and put his forehead against hers for one breath. The Aerinhal delegation will read this correctly.
