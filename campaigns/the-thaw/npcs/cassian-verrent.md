# Prince Cassian Verrent of Aerinhal

## Status
Alive. **26.** Of the **senior cadet line of the Verrents** — son of Prince Halric Verrent and Lady Maren of the Solinhar gold-houses. Halric is a cousin of Queen Lysandra (their grandfathers were brothers; one was the Verrent king, one was the king's younger sibling who founded the cadet branch). The reigning **King of Aerinhal is Roderic V, Lysandra's brother and Cassian's uncle by Halric's cousin-relation.** Cassian and Sera are second cousins once removed through the shared Verrent great-great-grandfather king — distant enough that the realm will not blink at the marriage, royal enough that the houses are pleased.

**As of the closing feast of the Hoarfrost Tourney, formally betrothed to Princess Seraphina Caldwyn of Caldros.** Wedding scheduled at the Aerinhal cathedral of Verrentine on the Feast of the Lesser Lights, by Aerinhal rite (with Caldros rites added).

Travelling with a small Aerinhal delegation including the senior knight **Ser Yarrick of the Sun-Gate**, the Ambassador **Lord Vesper** (a Verrent uncle of Cassian's father), the squire-master **Hallin**, and a working set of squires.

## Description
Taller than Sera remembered him from family gatherings when he was seventeen and eighteen and she was eleven and twelve — has grown into his face. **Dark golden hair**, the warm Verrent colouring beneath olive skin that has not paled in the northern light. **Hazel eyes**, slightly warmer than the gold of his hair. His uncle's nose and his father's jaw, and the two of them sit oddly on him in a way that has resolved itself, over the years, into a kind of grave handsomeness. Dresses in the long Aerinhal cut — red-and-gold, slashed sleeves at formal occasions, dark Verrent court robes in private corridors. Wears a sword-belt within the Spire, as foreign nobles of crown rank are permitted to. **As of the closing day of the tourney, also wears the Hoarfrost chain** — a worked silver chain of small linked frost-roses, the centrepiece a single open Caldwyn rose in dark steel and silver, presented on the field by Aelric.

The six-year age gap with Sera reads as it does: he is a man of twenty-six speaking to a woman of twenty, not a boy and a girl. He has had years longer to be still in his face. He has spent some of those years quietly being prepared for her by his father.

## Voice & mannerisms
Caldros tongue, clean, accented only lightly by the warm southern vowels of Aerinhal. The accent gets warmer at the long words and at moments of attention. Speaks slowly. Listens more than he speaks. Has an unusual eye-discipline for a young man — has been trained, or has trained himself, not to let his gaze drift to a woman's mouth or body in any chamber that contains witnesses. The discipline is the most striking thing about him in a public room. It costs him visibly when it is tested. *In private, with Sera, the discipline relaxes — slowly, deliberately, and with a small interior monitor he runs that is itself visible if Sera watches for it.*

A small cousin-of-a-smile at the corner of the mouth — never a full one in public, sometimes a half-degree of one in private. **The fuller smile — the one Sera saw in the corridor of the ninth bell on the 15th — exists.** She has seen it twice.

Carries a small gold-stamped ledger for note-taking; the habit of a young man who has been raised to be useful at councils.

### Private language with Sera *(established as of the road south)*
**Sera and Cassian speak Aerinic to each other in private from this point forward.** It was a thing both wanted; neither had to negotiate it. Sera has the language from her mother — the same inner-court Aerinic Lysandra grew up in, including the old high register Sera heard at the gate on the morning of leaving. She is fluent; the small old-Aerinhal her mother used to dismiss her at nineteen is Sera's also. In public, in Caldros, they continue to speak Caldros in the room's hearing. In private — the carriage, the inn rooms, the private corners of the Verrent house — they speak Aerinic. Once they cross the border the public register also shifts.

For the engine's narration: prose continues in English regardless; switches between Aerinic and Caldros are flagged in narration when they matter. *In Aerinic, the small parlour:* etc.

## Goals & pressures (post-betrothal)
1. **Marry Sera well.** Reach Verrentine, hold the month at his father's house cleanly, take her hand at the Feast of the Lesser Lights. Replaces the "win her hand" goal with the "be the husband she has accepted" goal.
2. **Honour his houses.** Both. The Verrent inner court and Cassian's father's senior cadet line on the one side; the Caldwyn-Caldros alliance his marriage now embodies, on the other. He has been raised to do this; it is the part he was trained for.
3. **Win her *trust***, as a separate piece of work from her hand. He has correctly understood that the hand was the easier part. He plans to spend the years.
4. **Not embarrass her** in his father's court. *Especially* at his father's court. His father will be reading her; Cassian wants the readings to be the ones she chooses to give. He is already managing the small expectations Verrentine will have of her — preparing the household, the wardrobe assistants, the language tutors-by-courtesy, the inner-court women who will sit with her on the days she will need someone to sit with.
5. **The Aldwin question.** Cassian's working belief is still that his elder cousin is expressing arrogant pity in the inner-court rooms and will need to be managed at Verrentine — that working belief is **out of date,** though Cassian does not yet know it. Aldwin has decided to want Sera for himself; the campaign Aldwin runs will be subtle, and what Cassian will see at Verrentine is not what he is preparing for. Halric is positioned to be the first to notice and to tell him; has not yet done so. *Cassian's instinct will probably catch the shift before any letter does, but he will catch it in pieces.*
6. Acquit himself respectably in the lists. *(Resolved across session 2 — won the joust outright; the Hoarfrost chain has changed his standing in his own court by a meaningful amount.)*

## Relationship to Sera
**Betrothed.** Devoted-leaning. He came north already disposed toward her: read four volumes of the Hollow Sea histories before the journey; read his father's small green leather book of the three failed proposal dispatches twice; arrived intending to be honourable about a thing he had not yet chosen. He was moved by the first day in three specific things (the ribbon a fraction too tight; the half-lidded look after; Sera crossing two seats to sit with Eira) and confirmed by the second (the corridor of the 14th, the gear tent of the 15th, the corridor of the 15th in the evening).

Three things have happened between them, in order:

1. **The handkerchief at the third hour of the 15th.** Her own — not a cut favour. He wore it at his left wrist against Marrick Ardenmark and unhorsed Ardenmark under the shield. The handkerchief has been on his person since — under the gorget, under the coat at the closing wine, in the inner pocket of every Verrent court coat he has worn through the rest of the tourney. As of session 2 close it is *not* hers any more; it is *his.*
2. **The corridor at the ninth bell of the 15th.** He gave the long answer to *do you want this marriage* — *yes,* with the long answer to mean it; spoke of wanting *the years after the marriage,* of *what wool you wear when no one is watching;* read her on the three previous failed suits; promised he would not flinch in the way she feared. Was kissed twice on Sera's request. Used the endearment *my love* once, carefully, in the corridor — the only time he has used it the way he means it. Closed her fist around itself and held his lips to her knuckles for three breaths. Went down the back stair without turning at the corner.
3. **Nine mornings at the gear-tent brazier.** Twenty-seven half-hours over the nine days of the tourney, with Hallin at the canopy as chaperone-by-courtesy. *No kissing in that window* — held by mutual restraint. Conversation: Aerinhal in winter; the books on each other's shelves; Sera's grandmother in the dower house at the western lake; Cassian's sister at sixteen; the household Sera will keep in Verrentine; the Verrent librarian's bad scholarship.

He has, at the close of session 2, used the endearment *my love* a second time — under the cheer at the dais, *"My love. — Breathe."* And, in the carriage on the morning of the 24th, a third — *"I am glad you asked, my love."*

He calls her *Sera* in private and *Highness* in public until tomorrow morning, when the protocols will shift slightly. After the betrothal she is *Princess Seraphina* in both courts formally and *Sera* to him personally. He has not had to negotiate this; the Aerinhal contingent has watched and reads it correctly.

## Elder cousin Aldwin — the antagonist of record
*(Updated at opening of session 04 — player canon escalation. See `npcs/aldwin-verrent.md` for the full file.)*

**Aldwin has decided to want Sera for himself.** What he wants is her *choice* — the way Cassian got it. His method is **subtle.** He will not move politically against the match. He will not engineer scandal. He will use the heir's tools: presence, attention, access, magic, the courtesy that is hard to refuse. He intends to be a man Sera has not been near and to let her decide, in his rooms, what she did not have a chance to decide three years ago.

**Cassian's working belief is two weeks out of date** — he still thinks his cousin is performing arrogant pity, the public posture Aldwin held in the rooms until the laughter stopped. He will likely catch the shift at Verrentine in pieces, by observation — the heir's regard for his bride-elect at dinner that is half a beat too long; the small social calibration that places Cassian as the junior cousin; the unfamiliar warmth in Sera's chamber on the third night that was not laid by Cassian's hand. His character will reach for the patient response. *Patience is not the tool the campaign needs now, and Cassian has not yet been trained for the tool it does.*

**Halric** is positioned to notice — has already heard the older question in Aldwin's congratulation at the senior cadet offices. Halric has not written to Cassian. Whether he writes, when, and what he says is open. *Halric may, by character, prefer to wait until he has watched a few more days before naming the thing in writing; the cadet-line discipline is to give the senior line the benefit of the doubt until it can no longer be given.*

**The asymmetries, as the engine holds them:**
- Aldwin has the homecourt, the magic, six years on Cassian in social-mature presence, the heir's prerogative, and a method Cassian is least practised at countering.
- Cassian has the written and witnessed match, the King of Caldros's consent, Halric's positioning, Lord Vesper's voice, Sera's own *yes,* and the fact that he won her on ground Aldwin has not stood on. *Aldwin does not yet know this last asymmetry exists.*

The story does not yet know what Cassian will do when he understands what is happening. Cassian does not yet know either. The campaign at Verrentine is the part the engine has not seen.

## Magic
Verrent fire at moderate strength. Can lay a heat-shimmer on the edge of a drawn blade, warm a closed room over half an hour, light a hearth without flint, lift a hot coal in his bare hand without burning. Cannot summon flame at battlefield scale. Cannot send heat across thirty paces. Distinct *individual* Verrent strength — none of the Aerinhal land-binding that comes only to a coronated Verrent monarch. He is bred for fire but is not the most powerful Verrent of his generation; that is Aldwin. The asymmetry was structural and Cassian accepted it; *the asymmetry has, as of session 04's opening, become material in a way Cassian has not yet seen.*
