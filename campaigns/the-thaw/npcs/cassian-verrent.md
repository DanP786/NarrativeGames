# Prince Cassian Verrent of Aerinhal

## Status
Alive. 20. Of the **senior cadet line of the Verrents** — son of Prince Halric Verrent and Lady Maren of the Solinhar gold-houses. Halric is a cousin of Queen Lysandra (their grandfathers were brothers; one was the Verrent king, one was the king's younger sibling who founded the cadet branch). The reigning **King of Aerinhal is Roderic V, Lysandra's brother and Cassian's uncle by Halric's cousin-relation.** Cassian and Sera are second cousins once removed through the shared Verrent great-great-grandfather king — distant enough that the realm will not blink at the marriage, royal enough that the houses are pleased.

**As of the closing feast of the Hoarfrost Tourney, formally betrothed to Princess Cassara (Seraphina) Caldwyn of Caldros.** Wedding scheduled at the Aerinhal cathedral of Verrentine on the Feast of the Lesser Lights, by Aerinhal rite (with Caldros rites added).

Travelling with a small Aerinhal delegation including the senior knight **Ser Yarrick of the Sun-Gate**, the Ambassador **Lord Vesper** (a Verrent uncle of Cassian's father), the squire-master **Hallin**, and a working set of squires.

## Description
Taller than Sera remembered him at seventeen and eighteen — has grown into his face. Dark gold hair, the warm Verrent colouring beneath olive skin that has not paled in the northern light. Hazel eyes, slightly warmer than the gold of his hair. His uncle's nose and his father's jaw, and the two of them sit oddly on him in a way that has resolved itself, over two years, into a kind of grave handsomeness. Dresses in the long Aerinhal cut — red-and-gold, slashed sleeves at formal occasions, dark Verrent court robes in private corridors. Wears a sword-belt within the Spire, as foreign nobles of crown rank are permitted to. **As of the closing day of the tourney, also wears the Hoarfrost chain** — a worked silver chain of small linked frost-roses, the centrepiece a single open Caldwyn rose in dark steel and silver, presented on the field by Aelric.

## Voice & mannerisms
Caldros tongue, clean, accented only lightly by the warm southern vowels of Aerinhal. The accent gets warmer at the long words and at moments of attention. Speaks slowly. Listens more than he speaks. Has an unusual eye-discipline for a young man — has been trained, or has trained himself, not to let his gaze drift to a woman's mouth or body in any chamber that contains witnesses. The discipline is the most striking thing about him in a public room. It costs him visibly when it is tested. *In private, with Sera, the discipline relaxes — slowly, deliberately, and with a small interior monitor he runs that is itself visible if Sera watches for it.*

A small cousin-of-a-smile at the corner of the mouth — never a full one in public, sometimes a half-degree of one in private. **The fuller smile — the one Sera saw in the corridor of the ninth bell on the 15th — exists.** She has seen it twice.

Carries a small gold-stamped ledger for note-taking; the habit of a young man who has been raised to be useful at councils.

## Goals & pressures (post-betrothal)
1. **Marry Sera well.** Reach Verrentine, hold the month at his father's house cleanly, take her hand at the Feast of the Lesser Lights. Replaces the "win her hand" goal with the "be the husband she has accepted" goal.
2. **Honour his houses.** Both. The Verrent inner court and Cassian's father's senior cadet line on the one side; the Caldwyn-Caldros alliance his marriage now embodies, on the other. He has been raised to do this; it is the part he was trained for.
3. **Win her *trust***, as a separate piece of work from her hand. He has correctly understood that the hand was the easier part. He plans to spend the years.
4. **Not embarrass her** in his father's court. *Especially* at his father's court. His father will be reading her; Cassian wants the readings to be the ones she chooses to give. He is already managing the small expectations Verrentine will have of her — preparing the household, the wardrobe assistants, the language tutors-by-courtesy, the inner-court women who will sit with her on the days she will need someone to sit with.
5. Acquit himself respectably in the lists. *(Resolved across session 2 — won the joust outright; he was not, privately, prepared for this. The Hoarfrost chain has changed his standing in his own court by a meaningful amount.)*

## Relationship to Sera
**Betrothed.** Devoted-leaning. He came north already disposed toward her: read four volumes of the Hollow Sea histories before the journey; read his father's small green leather book of the three failed proposal dispatches twice; arrived intending to be honourable about a thing he had not yet chosen. He was moved by the first day in three specific things (the ribbon a fraction too tight; the half-lidded look after; Sera crossing two seats to sit with Eira) and confirmed by the second (the corridor of the 14th, the gear tent of the 15th, the corridor of the 15th in the evening).

Three things have happened between them, in order:
1. **The handkerchief at the third hour of the 15th.** Her own — not a cut favour. He wore it at his left wrist against Marrick Ardenmark and unhorsed Ardenmark under the shield. The handkerchief has been on his person since — under the gorget, under the coat at the closing wine, in the inner pocket of every Verrent court coat he has worn through the rest of the tourney. As of session 2 close it is *not* hers any more; it is *his.*
2. **The corridor at the ninth bell of the 15th.** He gave the long answer to *do you want this marriage* — *yes,* with the long answer to mean it; spoke of wanting *the years after the marriage,* of *what wool you wear when no one is watching;* read her on the three previous failed suits; promised he would not flinch in the way she feared. Was kissed twice on Sera's request. Used the endearment *my love* once, carefully, in the corridor — the only time he has used it to anyone in his life. Closed her fist around itself and held his lips to her knuckles for three breaths. Went down the back stair without turning at the corner.
3. **Nine mornings at the gear-tent brazier.** Twenty-seven half-hours over the nine days of the tourney, with Hallin at the canopy as chaperone-by-courtesy. *No kissing in that window* — held by mutual restraint. Conversation: Aerinhal in winter; the books on each other's shelves; Sera's grandmother in the dower house at the western lake; Cassian's sister at sixteen; the household Sera will keep in Verrentine; the Verrent librarian's bad scholarship.

He has, at the close of session 2, used the endearment *my love* a second time — under the cheer at the dais, *"My love. — Breathe."*

He calls her *Sera* in private and *Highness* in public until tomorrow morning, when the protocols will shift slightly. After the betrothal she is *Princess Cassara* to the Aerinhal court formally and *Sera* to him personally. He has not had to negotiate this; the Aerinhal contingent has watched and reads it correctly.

## What he knows about her secrets

- **About Theodric and Caelum.** Does not know. Has not been told. Has registered that there is something between the Crown Prince and Sera's life that she will not put into corridor conversation; has been carefully not asking. *(Will probably hear, eventually, but only from Sera herself; the Verrent inner court does not gossip on Caldros secrets, by Halric's discipline.)*
- **About Sera's magic ceiling.** Does not know. Cassian's own fire-magic is moderate Verrent strength — see below. The question of whether she has the gift, or doesn't, or has it and can't reach it, has not come up between them. *(Probably will, within the month at the Verrent house; the household has Verrent fire-mages and will treat shared-blood magic as ordinary table conversation.)*
- **About the thing-that-happened at eleven-or-twelve.** Does not know. Has registered, twice, that there is a window in her childhood she does not speak of.
- **About Aerwin Vornlin's claim.** Knows what the pavilion announced; has been writing to his uncle about it through the tourney. Has his own working view that the Aerinhal crown would prefer the second claimant for trade reasons, but has not said so to Sera and will not in this campaign without invitation.

## Magic
Verrent fire at moderate strength. Can lay a heat-shimmer on the edge of a drawn blade, warm a closed room over half an hour, light a hearth without flint, lift a hot coal in his bare hand without burning. Cannot summon flame at battlefield scale. Cannot send heat across thirty paces. Distinct *individual* Verrent strength — none of the Aerinhal land-binding that comes only to a coronated Verrent monarch. He is bred for fire but is not the most powerful Verrent of his generation; that is his elder cousin Aldwin, who will inherit the Aerinhal throne, and whom Cassian does not resent.

## Relationship-shift triggers
- **Up (toward Devoted, fast):** plain candour from Sera. He is built to receive that and overweights it. *Already realised across the corridor of the 15th and the nine gear-tent mornings.*
- **Down (toward Wary):** discovering he is being used. He has discipline; he does not tolerate being made a fool of for long. *Not currently a concern — Sera's acceptance was visibly, audibly her own choice. Could come back as a risk if Sera at any point retreats into being managed by her mother in front of him.*
- **Side (toward Friendly-but-not-husband):** the betrothal collapsing on his end. *Almost not in the cards now; the only thing that would do this is a betrayal on her side or a death.*

## Knowledge
- Aerinhal politics inside out; his uncle's court intimately. He knows what trades his uncle has made and what trades he has not.
- The four volumes of the Hollow Sea wars, read slowly, in the past six months. Theodric's pamphlet on tilting commentaries, read twice. The three failed-suit dispatches from Caldemere, read in one sitting and (in the Volstrand reports) read a third time.
- The pairings for all the tourney bouts; the closing-day field as it played.
- That Sera is now formally betrothed to him and will reside at his father's house for one month before the wedding.
- The road south at the easy pace the delegation will travel: fourteen days. The household at Verrentine has been told, by courier, to be ready.
- *Does not* know about Theodric and Caelum.
- *Does not* know about Sera's parlour-trick magic ceiling.
- *Does not* know about the orchard / eleven-or-twelve.
- *Has* the gold-stamped ledger; his note-taking is good.

## On the road south at first light
Will ride beside her where the path permits and behind her where it does not. Has arranged the order of travel so the days end at three pre-arranged inns and one pre-arranged Verrent kinsman's house at the half-way point — the household has been preparing the rooms since the courier left Caldemere on the 16th. Has personally specified which corner of which kinsman's hall Sera and Anwen will sleep in (a private guest wing, separate from his) for the half-way night. The implications of this — that he has been thinking about her comfort in detail for nine days — Sera will discover incrementally.
