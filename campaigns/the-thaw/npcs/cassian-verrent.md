# Prince Cassian Verrent of Aerinhal

## Status
Alive. 20. Nephew to King Almeric Verrent of Aerinhal; son of Prince Halric Verrent (Almeric's younger brother) and his lady Maren of the Solinhar gold-houses. Currently a guest at Caldemere for the duration of the Hoarfrost Tourney, lodged in the south wing of the Spire. Travelling with a small Aerinhal delegation including the senior knight **Ser Yarrick of the Sun-Gate** and a younger courtier-lord (name TBD).

## Description
Taller than Sera remembered him at seventeen and eighteen — has grown into his face. Dark gold hair, the warm Verrent colouring beneath olive skin that has not paled in the northern light. Hazel eyes, slightly warmer than the gold of his hair. His uncle's nose and his father's jaw, and the two of them sit oddly on him in a way that has resolved itself, over two years, into a kind of grave handsomeness. Dresses in the long Aerinhal cut — red-and-gold, slashed sleeves at formal occasions, dark Verrent court robes in private corridors. Wears a sword-belt within the Spire, as foreign nobles of crown rank are permitted to.

## Voice & mannerisms
Caldros tongue, clean, accented only lightly by the warm southern vowels of Aerinhal. The accent gets warmer at the long words and at moments of attention. Speaks slowly. Listens more than he speaks. **Has an unusual eye-discipline for a young man** — has been trained, or has trained himself, not to let his gaze drift to a woman's mouth or body in any chamber that contains witnesses. The discipline is the most striking thing about him in a room. It costs him visibly when it is tested.

A small cousin-of-a-smile at the corner of the mouth — never a full one in public, sometimes a half-degree of one in private. Carries a small gold-stamped ledger for note-taking, the habit of a young man who has been raised to be useful at councils.

## Goals & pressures
1. **Win Sera's hand.** Primary. He came north for this. The match has been quietly arranged by his uncle and the Queen of Caldros across the past year of correspondence; Cassian was sent because he was the Verrent of his generation most likely to make it work. He knows this. He has agreed to it.
2. Not embarrass his house. Tied with 1 in his head. The Verrent of his generation does not arrive at a foreign court and leave behind a story that is told for a decade.
3. **Win her *interest*** — which he has correctly understood is a different thing from her hand and a prerequisite for it. He is playing the long game on this.
4. Not embarrass *her.* (This is new. It arrived in the corridor on the first night. He invented the dropped-handkerchief cover story for her sake, not his own.)
5. Acquit himself respectably in the lists. He is paired against Marrick Ardenmark in the third hour of day two and he is, privately, anxious about it; Ardenmark is the most-wanted-by-mothers bachelor knight in Caldros and Cassian is a prince, not a knight of the field. He has fire magic at moderate Verrent-blood strength and good footwork.

## Relationship to Sera
**Provisional Friendly-leaning-warmer.** He has no prior friendship with her — they had met twice before, briefly, at seventeen and eighteen, and Sera bruised him both times. He came north already disposed toward her: he read four volumes of the Hollow Sea histories before the journey, on his own initiative, and his tutor wrote to the Queen of Caldros to say so. The first day of the tourney moved him significantly. Three things in particular landed:
- The ribbon tied a fraction too tight at the pavilion. He felt what it meant.
- The half-lidded look she gave him after. He is twenty and not made of stone.
- **Sera crossing two seats to sit with Eira.** This is the one he will remember in three months. He told her so in the corridor.

He has not yet been told anything Sera does not want him to know. He has not been told about Theodric and Caelum. He has not been told about her magic. He has not been told about the orchard, the thing-at-eleven-or-twelve, or any of it.

**He has, however, heard her laugh.** First name in a corridor. He has been moved by all of this. He is, when alone, deliberately not letting himself overweight it. But he is overweighting it.

## What he knows about the corridor
- A man Sera knew came around the cross-corridor at the ninth bell, did not stop, did not speak, made one small sound, and walked on.
- The man did not stop at her door either.
- Sera's breath changed when the man's footfall was heard.
- He does not have a name. He has registered that the man's footfall was a sworn-knight's pace, soft boots, working tread. He has registered that the man was *trying not to be in this room.*

He has not asked. He will not ask. He is, however, *thinking* about it.

## Magic
Verrent fire at moderate strength. Can lay a heat-shimmer on the edge of a drawn blade, warm a closed room over half an hour, light a hearth without flint, lift a hot coal in his bare hand without burning. Cannot summon flame at battlefield scale. Cannot send heat across thirty paces. Distinct *individual* Verrent strength — none of the Aerinhal land-binding that comes only to a coronated Verrent monarch. He is bred for fire but is not the most powerful Verrent of his generation; the most powerful is his elder cousin Aldwin, who will inherit the Aerinhal throne and whom Cassian does not, by all accounts, resent.

## Goals & failure modes
- **Up (toward Devoted, fast):** plain candour from Sera. He is built to receive that and he will overweight it. One true sentence will carry years.
- **Down (toward Wary):** discovering he is being used. He has discipline; he does not tolerate being made a fool of for long. If Sera's interest reads to him retrospectively as transactional or staged, he will not confront her — he will withdraw cleanly and report to his uncle.
- **Sideways (toward Friendly-but-not-suitor):** if she tells him plainly she will not have him. He has, in his head, prepared for this. He will go home and write her letters, possibly for years.

## Knowledge
- Aerinhal politics inside out; his uncle's court intimately. He knows what trades his uncle has made and what trades he has not.
- The four volumes of the Hollow Sea wars, read slowly, in the past six months.
- The pairings for tomorrow's bouts (Ser Yarrick walked him through them on the first evening).
- That Sera is the most carefully observed woman in the four kingdoms this season.
- *Does not* know about Theodric and Caelum.
- *Does not* know about Sera's parlour-trick magic ceiling.
- *Does not* know about Aerwin Vornlin's claim except what was announced at the pavilion this morning; will likely write to his uncle about it tonight.
- Has the gold-stamped ledger; his note-taking is good; what he has written in it from the first day of the tourney is, presumably, careful and incomplete.
