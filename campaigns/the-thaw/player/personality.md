# How the world sees Sera

## Reputation in general
*The Ice Princess.* The nickname has been hers for four or five years and has hardened into a fact. The court has agreed on it; the people who travel to Caldemere have heard about it before they arrive; the bards have begun, recently, to put it into songs. The reading is doubled: the family magic is cold, and the woman is cold, and the two reinforce each other in the public mind whether or not either reading is fair.

Suitors who arrive expecting either a meek girl or a flirt have learned, sometimes publicly, that she is neither. Several have left convinced she is brilliant and unattainable; one or two have left convinced she is broken. None has left in possession of her hand or her interest.

**As of the first day of the Hoarfrost Tourney, the Lower City has begun to talk.** The bakers' wreath, the rose handed back to the child, the wreath on Whitewater's pommel through the day — a small story, but the kind a Lower City crowd carries home. By sundown there was already a song in a Lower City tavern about the princess and the baker's daughter; the song is sentimental and not entirely accurate.

## At court (Caldemere)
- **Her father, the King:** Loves her. Indulges her, within the limits of what indulging her costs him. Has so far backed her right to choose; will not back it past the deadline.
- **Her mother, the Queen:** Loves her. **Now also openly her conspirator on the question of her brother's marriage** — a position arrived at on the first night of the tourney, after a conversation in the Queen's sitting room in which both women confirmed, separately and together, what they have each been carrying in silence for three years. The conflict between them on the *marriage of Sera* is unchanged. The conflict between them on the *house of Caldwyn* is now a shared problem.
- **Theodric, the Crown Prince:** Older-brother fond. Knows she keeps his secret. Owes her in a way he has never named. *(Does not yet know that the Queen also knows. — Or — and this is the cold reading — has been told something by his mother and has not told Sera. On the first night of the tourney he retired to his own apartments with Eira at the ninth bell, which is not his pattern.)*
- **Eira, the Crown Princess:** Was correct, careful, slightly distant. **As of the first afternoon of the tourney: opened, plainly.** Eira has admitted loneliness to Sera. Eira has admitted she misses the practice yard. Eira has been promised a friend from the Frostmarch. The two of them have not yet had the conversation Eira may or may not need them to have, about why Sera was gentle with her in the first place.
- **Daron, the Spare:** Adores her. The only person in the world who can break her public face on demand. She is the only person whose disappointment moves him. **Currently slightly out of frame**: he disappeared for two hours of the tourney's first afternoon, returned with snow on his shoulders and a vague *the hawking field, with a friend*, and has not been pressed.
- **Caelum Vesper:** Best friend. **As of the first night of the tourney: saw her against a corridor wall under the cloak of Cassian Verrent and walked past without stopping.** Cleared his throat once as he passed. He did not knock at her door afterward. The conversation they are going to have is, by morning, the largest thing on her calendar.
- **The Rime Court (her father's council):** Respect her wit; do not entirely know what to do with her. After today, the Ostvar question has taken up most of their attention; they have, however, all seen the ribbon-tying, and several of them have already begun to count.
- **The household staff:** Like her. She remembers their names, including the children's. Hessen, the youngest stable-groom, has been entrusted with the wreath. He brought it back to the Spire stables in the dark.

## Among suitors and visiting foreign nobles
- **Aerinhal (House Verrent and clients):** **Cassian Verrent is now provisional Friendly-leaning-warmer.** The Aerinhal senior knight has had a private conversation with the Queen during the afternoon. The contingent at large will arrive at tomorrow's events with whatever instructions he has now received.
- **Therond (House Ledranne and the trade-port nobles):** Curious. Sera speaks their language without an accent. The Therondian envoys read this as flattering and have begun, in the last year, to suggest matches. Less pressure, more art. The Queen would consider it.
- **Ostvar (the three claimants):** Each of the three would-be Vornlins has reasons to want a Caldwyn princess and reasons to suspect they cannot afford one. **The third claimant, Aerwin Vornlin, was formally received at the pavilion today** — the Rime Court has not previously acknowledged him. His envoy stood at the rail every bout of the afternoon and watched.
- **The Marches (warlord-counts):** Several have sent gifts. None has sent a son. They know what they are.

## What people get wrong about her
- That the cold is the whole story. (It is not. After the first day, slightly fewer people believe it.)
- That she has no preference. (She does. She has not told anyone. — She has, however, *laughed* once at a foreign suitor, which is itself the closest thing to a preference she has shown the realm in four years.)
- That she will eventually fold. (The court is *roughly* divided on this; betting in private chambers runs about three-to-two that she will be sent abroad in the end. After today, the betting may shift.)
