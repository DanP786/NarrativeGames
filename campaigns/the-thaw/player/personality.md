# How the world sees Sera

## Reputation in general
*The Ice Princess.* The nickname had been hers for four or five years and hardened into a fact. The court agreed on it; visitors heard about it before they arrived; the bards had begun, recently, to put it into songs. The reading was doubled: the family magic is cold, and the woman is cold, and the two reinforce each other in the public mind whether or not either reading is fair.

**As of the closing feast of the Hoarfrost Tourney, the reputation is in active revision.** What three hundred and fifty pairs of eyes saw at the cross-table and on the dais of the Long Hall — the deep-blue Caldwyn formal with the band of old-wine-red at the bodice and hem; the hand in the crook of Cassian Verrent's arm for a quarter of an hour before the announcement; the walk down the long carpet at the prince's side; the *my child, my son* the King allowed the dais to hear — those are not the things a court tells about an Ice Princess. The court is rewriting. The bards will follow within a fortnight.

**The Lower City has been telling the bakers' wreath story for nine days.** By the closing feast there is more than one tavern song. The most popular is sentimental and not entirely accurate; the version sung in the bakers' guildhall is closer to what happened. Both versions end with the wreath on Whitewater's saddle and a princess who took a rose from a four-year-old without smiling.

## At court (Caldemere)

- **Her father, the King:** Loves her. Made the decision to send her south as one month's engagement at the Verrent house in Verrentine, married by Aerinhal rite at the Feast of the Lesser Lights. Did not lie to her about why: *"I do not want to lose you a fourth time, Sera-min. Three is enough."* Took the cloak as a small private compliment paid to him as well as to Cassian. *Noticed* it, and said so.
- **Her mother, the Queen:** **Now openly her conspirator on the question of her brother's marriage** *and* the woman who chose Cassian for her against four years of her refusals, and was, in the end, right to. Their conflict has not been resolved; it has been overtaken. By the closing days they were speaking only Aerinic to each other in the small parlour. Lysandra has given Sera the peace-bride's locket. Lysandra is the more shaken of the two parents by the going.
- **Theodric, the Crown Prince:** Older-brother fond. **Has begun to mend with Eira;** something changed in their apartments the night of 14th First-Frost. Spoke to Cassian on the field on the 16th, with Eira at his side. Recognised Cassian's under-shield strike as his own and said so to Sera at the family parlour the morning of the 16th. *"I would have liked to have been told that by a man before I was told it by his second pass at a strike."*
- **Eira, the Crown Princess:** Open. Sat beside Theodric noticeably closer through the closing days. Promised the Aelthyn friend (Riannon, of Otrim's three sisters) and signed the letter under the Queen's. Rode out to the hawking field with Sera and Theodric on the 21st. *"I had not realised, when this all began, that I had not yet started missing you."*
- **Daron, the Spare:** Adores her, has been visibly behaved through the tourney, charmed by Cassian's *I read the one with your name on it twice, Your Highness* at the closing wine of day 1, openly grinning at his sister at the family table the morning of the 16th, hauled Sera into the orchard on the 19th for the promised day. Threatens, only half in jest, to burn the Verrent third bedroom down if Cassian makes Sera cry.
- **Caelum Vesper:** Best friend. **Has held the public face through nine days.** Cleared the night-woman at the alcove for the corridor of the 15th and never named the kindness. Was held privately by Sera in the king's withdrawing of the royal pavilion on the morning of the 15th — *"You did not betray me. — Your mother has known three years."* Said his last private goodbye at her chamber door on the 22nd. *"Sera-min. Go well."*
- **The Rime Court:** Has watched a betrothal land in nine days and is, for once, not certain it has caught up. Several of them have already begun to count what it means for the Caldros–Aerinhal map. The Ostvar question has held their attention through the closing days; the Vornlin file is open.
- **The household staff:** Like her. Anwen has been given leave by the King to travel south. Hessen the youngest stable-groom has, on his own account, brushed Whitewater every morning of the nine days. He will not be at the gate at first light.

## Among the Aerinhal contingent (now her future court)

- **Cassian Verrent.** *Betrothed.* Plays the public game well. In private, used the endearment *my love* once, carefully, in the corridor at the ninth bell of the 15th. Won the joust outright on the closing day; received the Hoarfrost chain from the King. Will be at her side on the road south at first light.
- **Ser Yarrick of the Sun-Gate** — Cassian's senior knight. The man who made the approach to the Queen at the tenth bell of the 14th. Reads the Caldwyn court with care; will travel south with the delegation. Already addresses Sera, in formal moments, as *Highness* in the Aerinhal register.
- **Lord Vesper, the Aerinhal Ambassador.** A Verrent uncle of Cassian's father. Read the proposal in the Long Hall in formal Aerinic. Sat beside Sera at the cross-table for the closing feast. Travels south.
- **The wider Aerinhal contingent.** Has decided, in nine days, to like her. They have seen her in the cloak, in the silver, in the deep-blue with the old-wine-red trim; they have seen her brush a gloved fingertip across their prince's at a cup; they have seen her hand in his at the dais. They will arrive at Verrentine with their own report ahead of her.

## Among other suitors and visiting foreign nobles

- **Therond:** Lord Brennic Ledranne came forward to congratulate the King personally on the dais after the announcement. His face was correct; whether the senior Therondian envoys are pleased or merely civil is open.
- **Ostvar:** **Aerwin Vornlin** stood at the long tables for the closing feast and watched the King through the announcement. He did not turn his head to Sera. His claim continues at Caldemere.
- **The Marches:** Several of the Marcher counts have begun, since the second day, to court Theodric and the Crown directly rather than Sera. The realm has read the air.

## What people get wrong about her now

- That the cold was the whole story. (After today, almost no one believes it.)
- That she was chosen by her mother. (She was chosen by herself. The Queen contrived the introduction; Sera did the rest. Most courtiers, watching carefully, can tell the difference. A few cannot; those will be the ones to manage.)
- That the betrothal cost her nothing. (It cost her Caldemere. The cost was paid clear-eyed.)
- That she will be a *Verrent* princess. (She will be a Caldwyn princess at the Verrent court, by her own decision and her father's, and the Aerinhal will discover what that means in their own time.)
