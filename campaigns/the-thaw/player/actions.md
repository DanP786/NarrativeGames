# Significant deeds

*The reputation log. Updated when Sera does something the world will remember.*

- Four years (so far) of refusing suitors without giving offence sharp enough to start a war. This is itself a kind of accomplishment, and the court has noticed. It is the source of the nickname's harder edge.

## The Hoarfrost Tourney (14th–23rd First-Frost, 1142)

- **14th of First-Frost, 1142.** *The bakers' wreath at the procession.* Took the wreath from a four-year-old in the bakers' colours, plucked a winter rose, and handed it back to the child without smiling. Hung the wreath on Whitewater's pommel for the rest of the day. The Lower City began to talk about it that evening; by the closing feast there were at least two tavern songs.
- **14th of First-Frost, 1142.** *The Verrent ribbon at the opening of the lists.* Tied the green-and-silver around Prince Cassian Verrent's offered wrist a fraction too tight; lifted her face to him; let the pavilion see what was on the board. The Aerinhal contingent read it correctly.
- **14th of First-Frost, 1142.** *Sat with Eira.* Crossed two seats in the pavilion to sit with the Crown Princess Eira, alone, for the first time in three years of marriage. The pavilion saw. The Queen saw. The friendship has been the foundation of the Aelthyn letter and what has begun to mend between Theodric and Eira.
- **14th of First-Frost, 1142.** *The conversation in the Queen's sitting room.* Confirmed for her mother what her mother had separately confirmed for herself — that Theodric and Caelum have been lovers for three years. Extracted the Queen's formal promise that nothing will be done to Caelum without Sera being told first. The two of them now openly share the problem.
- **15th of First-Frost, 1142.** *Named the cloak colours.* In her mother's small parlour at the seventh bell, confessed in a child's flop-down posture that she had asked Cassian for a cloak in old-wine-red and silver-grey wool with no Verrent crest. The colours became, by the next morning, the realm's most-discussed piece of cloth.
- **15th of First-Frost, 1142.** *Walked alone into Cassian Verrent's gear tent at the eighth bell.* Pulled the flap closed. Lowered her hood. Asked him what he wanted; received the long answer. Gave him her own handkerchief as a favour, by name, for the third-hour bout against Marrick Ardenmark.
- **15th of First-Frost, 1142.** *The favour at the third hour.* The handkerchief was tied at Cassian's left wrist when he rode out against Ardenmark. The Caldwyn court watched a Verrent prince ride with a Caldwyn princess's named favour against the most-wanted bachelor knight in Caldros, and the court watched Cassian use Theodric's under-shield strike to unhorse Ardenmark on the second pass. The favour came off the wrist unbroken.
- **15th of First-Frost, 1142.** *The corridor at the ninth bell.* Met Cassian by appointment in her own corridor, hair down, in slate wool, on her own ground. Accepted his proposal in the private register, and was kissed twice on her own request. *"I will marry you."* No witness in the corridor itself except the night-woman at the alcove, who has not seen anything; Caelum, who had cleared the night-woman, also did not see and did not need to.
- **16th of First-Frost, 1142.** *The audience chamber.* Walked the thirty paces of red carpet in the silver gown and the Holsbrand cloak. Accepted her father's decision — the month at the Verrent house, the Aerinhal-rite wedding at the Feast of the Lesser Lights, the residency south thereafter — without pushing back. Asked, instead, for Anwen, for a day with Daron, for a ride with Theodric and Eira, for an evening with her mother. All four granted.
- **15th–23rd of First-Frost, 1142.** *Nine mornings at the brazier.* Visited Cassian in his gear tent every morning at half past the eighth for the duration of the tourney. Hallin chaperoned by courtesy. The Aerinhal contingent decided, in nine days, to like her.
- **16th of First-Frost, 1142.** *The cloak at the family parlour.* Wore the Holsbrand cloak into the family parlour at the half past the seventh and named it as Cassian's. Theodric, at that table, conceded the under-shield strike was his and that he would speak to Cassian publicly; Eira asked to come with him.
- **23rd of First-Frost, 1142.** *The closing feast.* Walked the long carpet to the dais at Cassian's side. The King of Caldros's consent was spoken from the dais in open Hall, to a court of three hundred and fifty. *"My child. My son."* Aelric, very quietly, under the cheer, looking at his daughter.

## The night after the feast and the morning of the leaving

- **23rd of First-Frost, 1142, half past the eleventh bell.** *Heard Aerwin Vornlin's three sentences alone in the upper corridor of the family wing.* Masked herself back into the older face — not the woman of the dais but the woman of the suitors' rail — and said only *speak then.* Let him speak; let him bow; let him go. Committed to nothing. No promise was given; no door was closed either. Carries his three sentences south. Anwen heard them and will not pass them on without Sera's word. *(Reputation-shaping only if she ever acts on what she heard. Until then: she is on no ledger of his.)*
- **24th of First-Frost, 1142, at first light.** *Refused Wren the falcon at the gate.* Daron offered Sera's own kestrel as a parting gift, in front of the family on the steps. Sera received the bird onto her glove and returned her: *Wren is yours, Daron. Keep her. Fly her. Write to me about her. — When I have a household at Verrentine I will send for her. Not before.* The gift declined was the kindness preserved. The family register read it correctly. Daron did not make his second joke.
- **24th of First-Frost, 1142, at first light.** *Placed herself in Cassian's carriage in front of the inner court, by her own request.* Crossed to her betrothed at the door of the Aerinhal carriage and asked, in Caldros, low but not private, that they ride south together. The Queen confirmed the arrangement in family register as if it had been the plan. Lord Vesper's order of march revised on the courtyard cobbles. The Aerinhal contingent registered the seal; the Caldros household will be talking about it. *The realm now reads: she is going south her way, not the King's.*
- **24th of First-Frost, 1142, at first light.** *Answered her mother in the old high Aerinhal.* Lysandra stepped down off the step in front of both courts, took Sera by the upper arms, and spoke the three words a mother of the Verrent inner house says to a daughter at the door on the morning of riding to a marriage. Sera answered correctly. The Caldros court, on the courtyard cobbles, witnessed something in a tongue older than Caldros itself. *The deed marks Sera as a woman the inner-court Aerinhal language belongs to — a thing said about her, not by her, in the rooms that decide such things.*

## Departing
- **24th of First-Frost, 1142 (from first light).** Travelling south with the Aerinhal delegation by the Westmark road. Caelum at the head of the Royal Guard escort as far as the marches' border. The road is fourteen days in winter.
