# Significant deeds

*The reputation log. Updated when Sera does something the world will remember.*

- Four years (so far) of refusing suitors without giving offence sharp enough to start a war. This is itself a kind of accomplishment, and the court has noticed. It is the source of the nickname's harder edge.
- **14th of First-Frost, 1142.** The bakers' wreath at the procession. Took the wreath from a four-year-old in the bakers' colours, plucked a winter rose, and handed it back to the child without smiling. Hung the wreath on Whitewater's pommel for the rest of the day. The Lower City has begun to talk about it. The bakers' guild will not forget it for a generation.
- **14th of First-Frost, 1142.** The Verrent ribbon at the opening of the Hoarfrost Tourney. Tied the green-and-silver around Prince Cassian Verrent's offered wrist a fraction too tight; lifted her face to him; let the pavilion see what is now on the board. The Aerinhal contingent has read it as encouragement. Whether they are right is open.
- **14th of First-Frost, 1142.** Crossed two seats in the pavilion to sit with the Crown Princess Eira, who was alone. The first time the two women have sat alone together in three years of marriage. The pavilion saw. The Queen saw.
