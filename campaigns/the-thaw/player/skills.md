# Skills

## Adept
- **Courtly Persuasion & Repartee** (20 ticks) — Her defining competence and the place she lives. Languages (fluent: the Caldros tongue, Aerinic, and Therondian; conversational: Ostvarn and three minor dialects), etiquette across all four crowns, table arrangements, precedence, the unspoken rules of a chamber. Reads a room in a glance. Cuts without raising her voice. Can deploy silence as a weapon. Years of refusing suitors have honed this from training into instinct.
  - *Session 1 ticks (5): ribbon to Cassian (charged); the level-something through the procession (sustained); the request for audience to the Queen in her own register (charged); the corridor with Cassian twice (climactic, doubled).*
  - *Session 2 ticks (7): the small parlour with Lysandra — Riannon recommendation and the "you are not going to commit any of this to paper without telling Eira first" (charged); the deflection-and-reveal on the cloak colours (charged); Cassian's gear tent and the handkerchief-as-favour (climactic, doubled — and the "call me Sera" beat is its own piece of work); the corridor of the ninth bell of the 15th, the long answers and the acceptance (climactic, doubled); the family parlour entrance in the cloak that landed Theodric's concession (charged); the audience-chamber request for Anwen, the orchard day, the ride, and the evening (charged); the closing-feast walk to the dais and the cross-table cup-handover (charged).*
  - **Approaching Master.** At 20 of the suggested 20 ticks for the Adept→Master threshold, with the *narratively earned moment* arguably banked at the closing feast — the Caldros court watched her, and a Verrent prince and the Aerinhal contingent received her, on her own terms. Per the cap (§7), advancement to Master is the player's option; she has at most 2 Master skills in her lifetime. **Holding at Adept until the player declares; promotion noted as available.**

## Novice
- **Reading People** (7 ticks) — Knows when she's being lied to, mostly. Picks up tells. Not infallible — strong personalities can roll over her read, and the family she grew up with she is *worse* at reading than strangers, because love distorts the signal.
  - *Session 1 ticks (2): read her mother's chair-arrangement at the pavilion; read Eira's working practice from one sentence about Otrim.*
  - *Session 2 ticks (3): correctly chose Riannon over Soren as the right Aelthyn sister for Eira on the basis of one wine-loosened sentence from Eira three months ago (charged); read Theodric's silence at the family parlour on the 16th and understood it was a concession, not a cold (charged); read Marrick Ardenmark's plan to aim under the shield from the way he lifted his visor between passes, before the bay even moved (climactic — though it was Cassian's body that acted on the read, the read itself was Sera's first).*
- **Singing** (2 ticks) — Trained, skilled, private. She has performed publicly only at the formal occasions she could not refuse and has been careful to be merely good on those occasions, not memorable. What she actually sounds like, three people in the world have heard. A public performance at full voice would be a different magnitude of risk.
- **Ice Magic — Party Tricks** (0 ticks) — Has the family gift. Can frost a glass, dim a candle by chilling the wick, leave a thin rime on the surface she touches. That is the ceiling and no amount of will has yet moved it. Why this is, no one in the family or the priesthood of the lake has yet worked out. Sera has stopped trying to push it; the trying has begun to feel like its own humiliation. *(Session 2 note: She did not, across nine days of high-stakes court work, reach for the gift once. The ceiling is, in her head, untested again.)*
- **Horsemanship — Display** (2 ticks, new entry as of session 2) — She has been formally a passable rider in a procession for years and Whitewater is well-trained, but she rode down to the field two hours early on the morning of the 15th, dismounted in a cold field, walked the perimeter alone, and remounted at her own stirrup with Hessen's help in a way that did not embarrass her. Twice more across the nine days she rode out — once to the orchard with Daron, once to the hawking field with Theodric and Eira — and once she came back tired but not unhorsed. Not a *real* horsewoman; not the same princess who would have refused to consider the ride a fortnight ago.

## Notable Untrained
- **Combat of any kind.** Sword, knife, bow, unarmed. A blade in her hand is a prop. She has not been taught and her body has not taught itself. The household guard would not let her near anything sharp in a real fight.
- **Battlefield ice magic.** What her brothers do — what the lowest scullion in the family's bloodline could do at her age — is closed to her. Whether this is a permanent ceiling, a frozen door, or something else is, for now, open.
- **Horsemanship beyond formal display.** She can ride in a procession; she can, as of session 2, ride out on a cold morning and back. She would not survive a hard ride across country. The Verrent road south takes fourteen days at the easy pace the delegation will travel; she will be tired by the end of it.
- **Aerinhal court politics (inner court).** She knows the *outline* through her mother and through the volumes; she has not yet lived in the Aerinhal inner court. The month at the Verrent house will be her education or her exposure.
