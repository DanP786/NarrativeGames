# Inventory

## Worn (closing feast of the Hoarfrost Tourney, 23rd First-Frost evening)
- Gown of deep Caldwyn blue, fitted bodice and full skirt, long close sleeves, Caldros silver-thread embroidery at cuff and hem in the Caldros pattern
- A narrow band of **old-wine-red** at the bodice and at the hem (half a shade off Aerinhal red — half the foreign envoys noticed, half did not)
- Silver Caldwyn circlet at the brow (the public one, not the small fillet)
- Hair up in the public knot, three small silver pins
- Small steel pendant at her throat (Caldwyn heirloom, plain — worn always)
- Grandmother's silver ring on the third finger of the right hand (worn always)
- The peace-bride's locket on a fine chain inside the bodice (a single grain of cardamom inside; given to her by Lysandra on the evening of the 22nd, the one Lysandra was given by her own mother on the morning Lysandra left Aerinhal)

## Carried (closing feast)
- A pair of fine kidskin gloves
- A small embroidered purse with a few silver coins and a folded handkerchief

## In her chambers at the Spire (packed by Anwen, travelling south at first light on the 24th)
- **The Holsbrand cloak** (Cassian's commission, made in twenty hours): old-wine-red Solinhar wool, silver-grey Caldwyn-wool border at hem and the hood-cuff, hammered silver leaf-pin at the throat (Holsbrand's house mark, used only by him), no Verrent crest, no Caldwyn rose. Made for her, by half-secret arrangement, named in colours she set; the realm knows.
- The silver gown of the morning of the 16th — pale silver wool, silver embroidery at cuff and hem, the Caldwyn rose worked tiny in three places. (Wear this for the wedding-eve at the Verrent house, possibly. Anwen's instinct.)
- The small plain silver fillet (the not-circlet) from the morning of the 16th — better for the long road than the circlet.
- Wardrobe of court dress, riding habits, two plain dresses for days she wishes to disappear in. **Three new winter formals** Anwen and the Queen have had cut in the last nine days for a Verrent winter — heavier wools, longer sleeves, Caldros silvers shading into Aerinhal warm-grey.
- The heavy slate-grey fox-lined cloak (the older one — for the road; the Holsbrand cloak is for arrivals).
- Her singing-master's lute, packed in its case with a wool wrap (Anwen's idea).
- A small case of books in three languages — the Aerinic poetry volume from her mother at twelve; the Therondian Hollow Sea history; the treatise on hawking; **and a new addition: a Caldwyn pamphlet of Theodric's tilting commentaries** in which his strikes are catalogued, Sera asked her brother for a copy on the morning of the orchard day and he sent it that evening with no comment.
- Her writing-desk's contents: paper, ink, sealing wax, the Caldwyn personal seal she is permitted to use on letters that do not bind the crown.
- The locked box at the bottom of her wardrobe (contents private; Anwen will see it goes with her).

## On her person at all times (jewellery worn always)
- Small steel pendant at the throat (Caldwyn heirloom)
- Grandmother's silver ring on the right hand
- The peace-bride's locket (cardamom grain inside) — *new as of 22nd First-Frost*

## Significant items she does NOT take south
- Her sword-belt-style riding belt (left at Caldemere by choice — she will buy or be given a Verrent one)
- The dried rosemary stem in her old purse (left in her writing-desk; her grandmother's habit, kept at home)
- The Hessen wreath (the bakers' wreath from the procession of 14th First-Frost — kept by Hessen in the Spire stables; he has dried it and hung it in his quarters; he is not at the gate at first light because he cannot watch her go)

## Significant items she has given away

- **The handkerchief.** Her own, lavender-and-the-darker-scent-at-her-wrists, given to Cassian as a favour at the third hour of the 15th. She has not seen it since he tucked it into the inner pocket of his shirt that morning. He has carried it on his person — under the gorget for the second pass, under the dark coat at the closing wine and the corridor of the ninth, then in the inner pocket of his Verrent court coats through the rest of the tourney. It is, as of the close of session 2, *not* hers any more. It is *his.*

## Items now associated with Cassian, by gift or by association

- **The Hoarfrost chain.** A worked silver chain of small linked frost-roses, the centrepiece a single open Caldwyn rose in dark steel and silver, with the year cut into the back. Presented by Aelric on the field at the close of the tourney; worn by Cassian at the closing feast. The chain is Cassian's — it is the closing-day winner's, by Caldros custom — but it is now also, by the public reading, a thing the King of Caldros gave to his future son-in-law. The court has filed it.
