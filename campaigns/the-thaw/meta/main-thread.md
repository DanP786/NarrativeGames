# Main thread

**Status:** converging *(as of opening of session 04 — the central question has been answered by play; the marriage of Sera is formally betrothed and Sera is on the Westmark road south by her own placement. A new convergence point has been added by player canon: Prince Aldwin Verrent, heir to the Aerinhal throne, has decided he wants Sera for himself, and intends — subtly — to make her choose him. The campaign now converges on the Feast of the Lesser Lights against a quiet antagonist who shares the house she is travelling to.)*

## The question
*What does it cost to stay cold? What does it cost to thaw?*

A second framing of the same question: *Can a woman who has refused to be moved be moved by anything she chooses, rather than anything chosen for her?*

A third framing: *In a court of trapped people, is choosing for oneself even possible — or is every move a choice for or against someone else one loves?*

A **fourth framing**, added at the opening of session 04: *Can a woman who has chosen continue to choose, when a more powerful man positions himself in her way and asks her, quietly, to choose again?*

## What play has answered

- **Yes,** she can be moved by something she chooses. The thawing has happened. It happened in a corridor at the ninth bell on the first night of the tourney; it was confirmed in a gear tent at half past the eighth on the second morning; it was sealed at the closing feast of the tourney by a betrothal declared in open Hall; and it was **publicly affirmed in front of the inner court at first light of the 24th** when Sera placed herself in Cassian's carriage by her own request.
- **The thaw was a choice,** not a surrender. The Aerinhal match was her mother's preferred candidate, and Sera went into the second day of the tourney determined to *torture Cassian first with impropriety to see what he was made of.* He held. He chose her back. The corridor at the ninth bell on the 15th was Sera's choice, not the Queen's. The carriage on the 24th was Sera's choice, not the King's. The Queen knows this and has been visibly working to keep the choice the daughter's, not her own.
- **The choice cost.** Sera is being sent south against her stated preference (her father's condition: one month at the Verrent house, married in Aerinhal). She is leaving Caldemere, Caldemere's people, Caelum, Daron, Eira, the Spire, the lake. The cost has been accepted clear-eyed.

## What Sera still wants

- Six months of Verrent court without losing her own face.
- To know what kind of wife she is going to be before the cathedral on the Feast of the Lesser Lights.
- To remain herself, in a country where the language her interior most easily speaks is the one her mother gave up at nineteen.
- Quietly — to be loved well by Cassian, in a way she could not, at twenty, have asked for in the open even with him.

## What stands in her way now

- **Prince Aldwin Verrent of Aerinhal — the antagonist of record.** *(Player canon, set at opening of session 04.)* The heir to the Aerinhal throne, ~30, unmarried, with a fourteen-year-old foreign princess waiting two years out — has decided he wants Sera for himself. What he wants is her **choice** — the way Cassian got it. His method is **subtle.** He will not move politically against the match in rooms his father can name; he will not engineer scandal; he will not coerce. He will use the heir's tools: presence, attention, the magic Cassian cannot match, the small access of a host in his own house. He intends to be a man Sera has not been near and to let her decide, in his rooms, what she did not have a chance to decide three years ago — when he passed on her as *hard to tame, despite being beautiful,* and now believes he was wrong. **Neither Sera nor Cassian knows yet.** What he plans is not known to the world; what he does will surface in play. The story does not yet know whether the choice she made on Caldros ground holds on his. See `npcs/aldwin-verrent.md`.
- **Aerinhal court she has never lived in.** A Verrent household for a month before the wedding. A father-in-law (Prince Halric) she has not met. A future mother-in-law (Lady Maren of the Solinhar gold-houses) she has not met. A sister-in-law-to-be (Cassian's sister, sixteen) who knows her only by Cassian's letters. The household will be patient (her father's reading) and watchful (her own).
- **The road south itself.** Fourteen days. Cassian beside her every day. The first nights they are not in their parents' courts. *Now also: fourteen days in which Cassian's working belief about his cousin is two weeks out of date, and the campaign Sera is travelling toward is one she does not know exists.*
- **Her own untested everything.** She has been kissed twice in her life. Both kisses were Cassian's. She has consented, on her own terms, to consent to him in everything; that is not a problem of his asking, it is a question of her own discovery. The campaign now opens that door — and a quieter door behind it, that Aldwin will be on the other side of.
- **Aerwin Vornlin's three sentences.** Carried south alone in her head. She has been asked to be a woman at a table five years from now who does not say *nothing* about Ostvar. Whether she accepts the question, refuses it, or forgets it is the kind of slow-arc test the campaign will return to.

## Threads continuing in absentia

- **Theodric and Eira.** Something changed in their apartments the night of 14th First-Frost. By the closing feast they were sitting close; at the morning departure Eira did not release her husband's arm to embrace Sera. Whether what changed is durable, partial, or a courtesy borrowed for Sera's departure remains to be played. The "can he stop" question has, by silent agreement of mother and sister, been set aside for now. **Caelum's status has not been settled — only suspended.**
- **Caelum.** Rides Sera south with the Royal Guard as far as the marches' border. Names himself *Captain* in front of the court — a title he had not used to her since she was eleven — because the title is the one he can be seen using. Their last private goodbye has already been said. The country between them becomes a country in fact at the marches' border.
- **Aerwin Vornlin / the Ostvar question.** **No longer entirely in absentia.** Sera has heard his three sentences directly, in her own corridor, at half past the eleventh bell of the 23rd. The council on Ostvar sits at Caldemere in eleven days without him. Aelric is still holding. The Westmark road Sera now rides passes within forty miles of Ostvar at its closest. The thread is in Sera's hand; what she does with it is in her hand. See `npcs/aerwin-vornlin.md`.
- **Riannon Aelthyn coming south to Caldemere for Eira.** Will not have arrived before Sera left. The friendship is set up but the seeds will sprout offstage.
- **Lysandra and Aelric.** Have, between them, given their daughter to a foreign court for the second generation in a row. Lysandra is the more shaken; Aelric the more reconciled. At the gate Lysandra spoke to Sera in the old high Aerinhal her own mother used at her own leaving twenty-eight years ago; Sera answered correctly. The marriage of the King and Queen of Caldros is the third marriage this campaign has lit, and play has only glimpsed its outline so far.
- **The fourteen-year-old foreign princess offstage.** Aldwin's current betrothed. A complication on his board; not a barrier. When and whether she becomes a cost-piece for the Sera campaign is for later play.

## What the campaign converges on

The Feast of the Lesser Lights in the Aerinhal cathedral, ~seven to eight weeks from the close of session 02. Between here and there: the road south (14 days); the month of engagement at the Verrent house under Halric's roof and the heir's regard; the discovery of who Sera is in a country that has not yet decided who she is; and the quiet contest of an antagonist who shares the dinner table and whose method is the kind Cassian's character is least practised at countering.

The campaign has shifted, by the player's standing rules, into a phase where consent with Cassian is no longer in question and where the Aldwin question is active. What is in question is whether the choice Sera made on Caldros ground holds on Aerinhal ground, and what it costs each of the three of them either way.

The Aerinic standing rule, set at the opening of session 03, has not yet surfaced in narration. The first private exchange between Sera and Cassian in Aerinic on the road south will be its own small beat — pickable by either of them, at the time the moment fits.
