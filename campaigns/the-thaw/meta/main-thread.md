# Main thread

**Status:** active *(hardened across the establishing arc of session 1 — the antagonist appeared on-screen, the want was made concrete by Sera's choices, and the central question was framed by the day's beats)*

## The question
*What does it cost to stay cold? What does it cost to thaw?*

A second framing of the same question: *Can a woman who has refused to be moved be moved by anything she chooses, rather than anything chosen for her?*

A third framing, the one play has begun to surface: *In a court of trapped people, is choosing for oneself even possible — or is every move a choice for or against someone else one loves?*

## What Sera wants
- Not to be sold.
- To remain herself.
- (Now articulated, in the small private dark of her own bedchamber at the end of session 1) — to be able to *choose,* and to be allowed to discover what she would choose if no one had a deadline.
- (Unspoken, still) — to matter on her own terms.

## What stands in her way
- **Her mother, Queen Lysandra.** *Status as of session 1: complicated.* Lysandra has revealed that she is not what Sera had been treating her as — a force-of-nature pressure for the foreign marriage. She is also Sera's only adult ally on the matter of Theodric and Caelum, has known for three years, and has formally promised that nothing will be done to Caelum without Sera being told first. The Queen is no longer a clean antagonist; she is a woman with whom Sera now shares two competing problems — the marriage of Sera (where they are opposed) and the marriage of Theodric (where they are aligned). Whether Lysandra can hold both at once is open. The deadline has not moved.
- **The clock.** Seven months until Sera's name-day.
- **Herself.** What she has frozen — the reason she has never kissed a man, the reason her magic stops at parlour tricks, the reason the court reads her as ice. She laughed for the first time in months tonight, at a foreign suitor in a corridor. The thing she has frozen is not as solid as it was this morning.

## Adjacent threads (live, shaping the spine)
- **The Crown Prince's marriage.** Theodric does not love Eira; he loves Caelum. As of tonight: *the Queen knows.* The triangle is now visibly observed by an additional party. The question *can Theodric stop* is being held by both his mother and his sister. He has not been asked.
- **Eira.** Has opened to Sera as of this afternoon. Will receive an Aelthyn-house friend from the Frostmarch. May or may not be moving toward suspicion of her husband; she did not seem to be at the pavilion this afternoon. *Theodric retired to his own apartments with her at the ninth bell tonight — which is not his pattern.* Open whether something has changed there.
- **Cassian Verrent.** Has heard her laugh. Will send a cloak. Tomorrow third hour in the lists against Ardenmark. Provisional Friendly-leaning-warmer. He is now an actual contender for the marriage, in a way he was not when the morning began.
- **Caelum.** Saw the corridor. Walked past. The conversation he and Sera have not yet had is the largest thing on tomorrow's calendar. He may or may not understand that the Queen knows. He almost certainly does not yet know that Sera has told her tonight.
- **The wider map.** Aerwin Vornlin, the third Ostvar claimant, was formally received this morning. The Rime Court has new information about a man no kingdom has yet recognised as a son of the Vornlin line.
