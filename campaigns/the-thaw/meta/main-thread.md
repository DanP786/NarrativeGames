# Main thread

**Status:** converging *(as of end of session 02 — the central question has been answered by play; the marriage of Sera is formally betrothed and on the road south. The Theodric/Caelum/Eira thread has shifted from acute to chronic; the wider-map thread (Aerwin Vornlin / Ostvar) remains open and will outlive Sera's presence at Caldemere.)*

## The question
*What does it cost to stay cold? What does it cost to thaw?*

A second framing of the same question: *Can a woman who has refused to be moved be moved by anything she chooses, rather than anything chosen for her?*

A third framing: *In a court of trapped people, is choosing for oneself even possible — or is every move a choice for or against someone else one loves?*

## What play has answered

- **Yes,** she can be moved by something she chooses. The thawing has happened. It happened in a corridor at the ninth bell on the first night of the tourney; it was confirmed in a gear tent at half past the eighth on the second morning; it was sealed at the closing feast of the tourney by a betrothal declared in open Hall.
- **The thaw was a choice,** not a surrender. The Aerinhal match was her mother's preferred candidate, and Sera went into the second day of the tourney determined to *torture Cassian first with impropriety to see what he was made of.* He held. He chose her back. The corridor at the ninth bell on the 15th was Sera's choice, not the Queen's. The Queen knows this and has been visibly working to keep the choice the daughter's, not her own.
- **The choice cost.** Sera is being sent south against her stated preference (her father's condition: one month at the Verrent house, married in Aerinhal). She is leaving Caldemere, Caldemere's people, Caelum, Daron, Eira, the Spire, the lake. The cost has been accepted clear-eyed.

## What Sera still wants

- Six months of Verrent court without losing her own face.
- To know what kind of wife she is going to be before the cathedral on the Feast of the Lesser Lights.
- To remain herself, in a country where the language her interior most easily speaks is the one her mother gave up at nineteen.
- Quietly — to be loved well by Cassian, in a way she could not, at twenty, have asked for in the open even with him.

## What stands in her way now

- **Aerinhal court she has never lived in.** A Verrent household for a month before the wedding. A father-in-law (Prince Halric) she has not met. A future mother-in-law (Lady Maren of the Solinhar gold-houses) she has not met. A sister-in-law-to-be (Cassian's sister, sixteen) who knows her only by Cassian's letters. The household will be patient (her father's reading) and watchful (her own).
- **The Verrent inner court.** Aerinhal's politics around the King her uncle, the senior cadet line, the elder Verrent cousin Aldwin (who will inherit), the Verrent fire-magic culture, the things her mother left behind at nineteen.
- **The road south itself.** Fourteen days. Cassian beside her every day. The first nights they are not in their parents' courts.
- **Her own untested everything.** She has been kissed twice in her life. Both kisses were Cassian's. She has consented, on her own terms, to consent to him in everything; that is not a problem of his asking, it is a question of her own discovery. The campaign now opens that door.

## Threads continuing in absentia

- **Theodric and Eira.** Something changed in their apartments the night of 14th First-Frost. By the closing feast they are sitting close, walking together, Eira at his shoulder. Whether what changed is durable, partial, or a courtesy borrowed for Sera's departure remains to be played. The "can he stop" question has, by silent agreement of mother and sister, been set aside for now. **Caelum's status has not been settled — only suspended.**
- **Caelum.** Stays at Caldemere as master of arms to the royal house. Sera has said her goodbye. The friendship will continue by letter; the country between them is now a country in fact.
- **Aerwin Vornlin / the Ostvar question.** Still open. Aelric is holding. The matter will outlive Sera's presence and may pull a campaign thread west in time.
- **Riannon Aelthyn coming south to Caldemere for Eira.** Will not have arrived before Sera leaves. The friendship is set up but the seeds will sprout offstage.
- **Lysandra and Aelric.** Have, between them, given their daughter to a foreign court for the second generation in a row. Lysandra is the more shaken; Aelric the more reconciled. The marriage of the King and Queen of Caldros is the third marriage this campaign has lit, and play has only glimpsed its outline so far.

## What the campaign converges on

The Feast of the Lesser Lights in the Aerinhal cathedral, ~seven to eight weeks from the close of session 02. Between here and there: the road south, the month of engagement at the Verrent house, the discovery of who Sera is in a country that has not yet decided who she is. The campaign has shifted, by the player's standing rule, into a phase where consent with Cassian is no longer in question — what is in question is what Sera does with everything else.
