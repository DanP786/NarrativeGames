# Main thread

## Central question
What does it cost Scarlet to keep doing this work, in a galaxy this dark, while telling herself she is just passing through?

## What she wants
To see the galaxy. Different planets, different ports, different people. Freedom of movement. The horizon, always.

## What's in the way
- The work itself. Bounty hunting in the Old Republic galaxy is not a clean trade. The easy jobs aren't always easy and the line she draws — *alive only* — costs her credits, time, and occasionally skin.
- Her own past. A skirmish she does not understand killed her family. She has not asked who or why. She might, eventually, have to.
- The galaxy's gravity. People who keep moving meet people who want them to stop, or stay, or come work for someone bigger. The longer she works, the harder it gets to keep passing through.

## The line
**She brings them in alive.** This is the spine of the question. The grimdark of the setting will lean on this rule. It is the difference between her and what she does. Whether it holds — and what it costs when it bends or breaks — is what the campaign will quietly be about.

## Status
**active — the question has now paid out three times, in three different registers.** S2 tested the line against the people who paid her last invoice clean. S3 tested it in private against herself. **S4 tested it against people who pulled lethal fire on her with intent to use it — and then closed by asking whether the line survives a contract whose terms make the line itself the cost.**

- **Antagonist surfaced fully (S2).** *The spiked spiral is a major political-shadow faction.* The party that bought the spring Coruscant trade-vote, owns the Aargau private vault, runs the Mortuda safehouse system, contracts through the Phantom Voice broker, and maintains a working "list of names" — the same faction that paid Scarlet 20k for Avash now paid her 35k for Pyx's death. Scarlet has worked for them twice. Vel is their in-person handler at Worlport. Distinct from and at war with the **Vesper** (the plain-coat killers from S1).
- **The line was tested and held (S2).** *Alive, always.* When her own employer wanted the man dead, Scarlet renegotiated the contract face-to-face: traded the box and the dossier (her better leverage) for the bounty and the man's life and parole. **First job in which her line survived contact with the people who paid for the bringing-in.**
- **The cost was named (S2).** *Eighteen months of someone else's grief on her ship. Risa's bracelet handed back to the people who killed her, without knowing. The intimate register pulled back to the off-world drop. Better leverage liquidated for one man's life. The faction is now sharper about who she is, and not in her hand any more.*
- **Theron is on the ship.** Operating under his real name from Bonadan onward, the *Pyx* run ended. **As of S4 close: committed to Scarlet in private register, mutually declared, exclusive on both sides, break-clause built in. Operating as asset-next-to-her-trade.**
- **The personal cost has a face now (S3).** The first night of transit became its own scene. Scarlet and Theron consummated the intimate register she had pulled back on the suborbital; in the quiet after, both halves of the cell-line kiss disclosure were spoken aloud; Theron lifted the bracelet trade off Scarlet in the open, **naming the bunk as the place he made peace with it.** *"Just passing through"* has not survived this contact intact.
- **The line was tested in combat and held (S4).** *Alive, always* against Berran's freight-snatch crew at Henn's Field. Returning lethal fire from her own captured pistol; she returned stun-only across the apron firefight, the cockpit confrontation, and the point-blank stun on Berran himself. **No kills. No collateral.** The discipline held under stim and under the personal weight of Korro having sold her to the boss.
- **The line is now under a new pressure (S4 close).** **Naia Tul.** Hutt-broker job from the Bonadan boards. Twi'lek female, late thirties, freight-line dispute with a junior Hutt called Klornakka, alive-only **with the *answer-the-question* clause** — the Hutt is going to torture her after delivery. Scarlet took it knowing the clause: *"I am going to deliver her. And the Hutt is going to do what the Hutt is going to do. I have done it before. It pays the bay-rent."* *The captain at twenty-five would not have taken this one.* **This is the first contract in the campaign that is itself the threat to the line, not a counter-party to it.** The line's survival into Mos Eisley is the next test.

The campaign continues. The thread updates reactively as Scarlet keeps moving. **Open-ended; no planned endpoint.**

Family name (the full name) still held back per his cell-line — *"the night you ask me a second time."* Scarlet has not asked. ## New threads opened in S4
- **Korro.** Pre-*Phoenix Claw* lover, five years ago. Carried Scarlet's name through every quarter and every port for those five years. Sold her to Berran when Berran asked who was at the *Phoenix Claw*. Took a stun-bolt to the chest, was dragged into hull-shadow, was carried off Vandros by the surviving bar-watchers. Whereabouts open. **Whether he comes back, and what he wants when he does, is a long-arc question.**
- **Berran.** Freight-snatch crew boss, three years running the Aukris route. Did not draw, did not fire a shot the entire engagement. Stunned in his own cockpit on a hardpan north of Vandros. Will wake, will lift, will disappear. Marked Scarlet as a working captain *he respects.* **Whether their paths cross again is a long-arc question.** Berran's crew is now reduced by three to four hands; the Aukris-run operation continues but at less weight.
- **The Phantom Voice channel ping.** Three pings in the week before Scarlet lifted from Bonadan. Channel dark since Mortuda. Origin unknown; fee not posted on the comm-header. **Queued for two days into Tatooine fold-cruise.** Will land in S5. *Whatever this is, it is from someone willing to pay through Vel's old broker, which means it is from the spiked spiral side of the galaxy, or from someone Vel had access to.*
- **The Naia Tul contract and the *answer-the-question* clause.** Already discussed in *Status* above. The Hutt at Mos Eisley is the immediate counter-party; the longer question is whether Scarlet's *alive, always* line survives being the one who walks the woman through the door knowing what is on the other side of it.
- **Theron's working theory about Risa's killers, still un-named.** S4 did not surface it. The committal in the cockpit was made without Risa's name spoken — deliberately. The theory remains 18-plus months unfalsified. **The bracelet thread sleeps but does not close.**
