# Narrative

Scarlet Kriov, 28, freelance bounty hunter, owner-operator of the light freighter *Phoenix Claw*. She works the easy boards across the galaxy — small bounties, small credits, just enough to keep flying. She brings them in alive. Always.

## Session 1 — The Brek'eevo job

The campaign opens on Nar Shaddaa. Three days into a routine tail on a Twi'lek slicer who skipped on a Hutt's gambling debt, Scarlet walks into the slicer's apartment to find him already dead and three professionals — a calm human, a Rodian with a wet knife, a Zabrak grappler — finishing a search of his rooms. She talks her way past the door playing a drunk girlfriend, breaks the bluff with a two-shot opening that puts the human and Rodian down, takes a hand at her throat from the Zabrak, and disables him with the lethal blaster — **the first time she has fired it on a job.** She does not kill any of them.

She takes the team's search target — a stack of flimsiplast — and runs. She walks back to her ship on foot through Nar Shaddaa's mid-level traffic, lifts off clean, jumps for Ord Mantell. In hyperspace she reads the papers and discovers a private 25,000-credit alive-only contract on a man named **Korel Avash** under a non-Hutt seal — a small spiked spiral in red wax. Brek'eevo had been doing the legwork. The killers were not Brek's pursuers; they were after the contract.

She arrives at Ord Mantell asleep, with a splinted wrist, an unpaid debt to a Hutt majordomo named Yelba, her face on a corridor cam she could not retrieve, and a question she chose not to answer in the bunk: was Brek'eevo more than he looked, was he given something to hold, or was he set up to be holding it when they came?

## Session 2 — The Avash extraction

She takes the Avash contract from a woman who calls herself Vel — sharp-faced, dark-eyed, the issuer of a 25,000-credit "alive only" bounty whose true nature is protective extraction under cover. Avash, Vel's "guest" for some time, is to be moved from one place to another while the **Vesper** (the same plain-coat professionals who killed Brek'eevo and, almost certainly, the first hunter Vel hired) are hunting him.

Scarlet picks Avash up from a Worlport service door, transports him in the holding cell of the *Phoenix Claw* under unforced cover, takes a route Vel had marked *do not use*, and delivers him nine days later to a woman in plain working clothes at a dead system called **Mortuda**. The half-moon tokens match. Avash nods once and goes. Twenty thousand credits clear to Scarlet's chit. She does not see the Vesper at any point.

She covers her tracks the way she has learned to cover tracks.

## Interim — three weeks, three small jobs

Two weeks pass. Three small alive-only jobs, easy boards, paying enough to keep flying. The wrist heals to light grip. The bruise at her neck fades. Her voice comes back. **Nobody comes for her face.**

The Avash contract is closed; Vel's dedicated channel has been dark since Mortuda; the spiked-spiral faction is, as far as Scarlet knows, finished with her. She is back to small jobs and the horizon — which is what she had wanted.

## A new hire arrives

A different port, a different ship-night, three weeks past the Iron Lung. Scarlet is out of the shower in a towel at the kitchenette when the *Phoenix Claw*'s main comm chimes — not Vel's channel, the registered freelance one. The message is encrypted in a signature she does not recognise. *Hire / retrieval / 35,000cr / 6 days. Reply within 12 hours or the offer rolls. — Identify yourself first.* Attached: a meet-point at an anonymous relay buoy in a system she has not been to.

The campaign continues from here.

## Session 2 — The Pyx contract; Tarvane's Quay; the salt-house trade; Bonadan-bound

She accepted the new hire from a contracting voice she has since identified as the spiked-spiral faction's broker. Target: **Pyx**, a never-captured vault-and-escape specialist; alive retrieval, 35,000 cr, six days. She tracked him to **Tarvane's Quay** — a Bothan-run smuggler station above the gas-giant **Sothen** — through a 30-credit packet from a teenage Bothan info-trader named Voya, beat a competing hunter named **Cael Soros** to the man, played the single-woman-at-a-bar at the Gold Hour, and walked Pyx out of the station under the silhouette of a drunk girlfriend cover after dropping a slipsleep tab into his second drink. She also stripped his flat. *Phoenix Claw* lifted on a generic vector and folded ten minutes after.

In the cell four hours later, in the carbonite-cuff and her work uniform, she sat opposite him and they spoke as two professionals who had also kissed at a transit-deck observation strip the previous evening. He confessed the kiss was the cover's gift to him, not the cover itself. He named himself *the open* on the locked Aargau box he had taken three weeks ago. From the paper notes she had stripped from his flat — sealed in a wax impression of a small spiked spiral, the same impression as the Hutt-format paper that had carried the **Brek'eevo** contract eight days earlier — she read the spring Coruscant trade-vote scandal and worked the fact-pattern. *The spiked spiral is a major political-shadow faction.* The party that had bought the vote and built **Korel Avash**'s death-cover was the party that had hired her for Avash's protective extraction and was now hiring her for Pyx's death. Her current contract was from her own previous client. *The bring-them-in-alive line was being tested against the woman who had paid her last invoice clean.* She named the binding choice: she would not deliver.

In the Worlport salt house's deck nine, Vel waiting at the table with a satchel pre-staged for either closing, Scarlet handed back what she had walked in with — the locked box, the spring trade-vote dossier — and asked for the bounty and **Pyx's life and parole** in exchange. Vel accepted without trying to take both at once. Scarlet uncuffed Pyx in front of two operatives. He worked the box open without looking inside, preserving his own deniability. Vel paid the 35,000 credits, sent the satchel through the back door, and told the door-man to let them out. Pyx is formally **off the spiked spiral's list of names** contingent on his staying off the boards — a list whose existence Scarlet now knows about and which is presumably long.

On the suborbital back to Kel Sahr, Pyx gave Scarlet pieces of himself she had not asked for the cost of. His real first name: **Theron**, family name still his. The dead partner whose name he had not said in 18 months: **Risa**, killed on an exit-route gone wrong after a vault job; her body moved; her silver mag-band bracelet — courier-chip in the underside, working credentials and a year of their shared job-tags — kept by the people who killed her. The bracelet had been in the Aargau box. **Scarlet had just traded Risa's bracelet back to the people Theron believes killed her, without knowing.** He let her, because he needed to make the choice himself — the first choice in eighteen months that put someone alive in front of someone dead. Scarlet pulled back. Called Risa *girlfriend* instead of the name. Closed the page she had let open on the transit strip. Theron accepted the closure without pushing on it: *"All right. Anywhere a long way from Ord Mantell will be enough. I will move under my own name from wherever you set me down. Thank you, Scarlet."*

The *Phoenix Claw* lifted from Kel Sahr on a five-day vector for Bonadan, two cooling mugs of caf side by side on the console. Scarlet's working line held — *alive, always.* Her financial position is the strongest of the campaign so far (~60,000 credits clear). She has now worked twice for the spiked spiral, both times paid clean. She has lost the spring trade-vote dossier as leverage on them. She has identified her own employer and chosen, once, not to deliver. The man she walked out of a room he should not have walked out of is on her ship for five days, operating from this day forward under his real name. **The campaign's central question paid out once. It will pay out again.**

## Session 3 — The first night of transit

Day 1 of the five-day vector, somewhere in the empty between Ord Mantell and the inner mid-rim. Scarlet locks the autopilot, rotates her chair, and asks Theron what they should do to pass the time. The closure she set on the suborbital — careful, mannered, the *"Thank you, Scarlet"* register closed — does not survive the cockpit. He meets her there by his own explicit hand-off. *"I am out of practice. You will have to lead."* He keeps the word the whole way down to her, and she gives him the lead back when she has worked her way up his body and seen what is in his face. *"My turn."* They sleep with each other for the first time. Risa's name is not spoken in the room.

In the quiet after, faces mirrored at each other's faces, Scarlet finally gives him the matching half of his cell-line disclosure: that the night they met, the attraction had been real on her side too. The *"Thank you, Scarlet"* register, closed on the suborbital, is reopened with full weight. *"Not the way he said it on the suborbital. The opposite."*

Then the harder thing. Scarlet — joke-as-disclosure — asks whether he resents her for trading the bracelet back to the people he believes killed Risa. He does not pull back. The dry returns in a different register and then drops entirely. *"You did not trade her bracelet. I traded it. You handed me the room and asked the price; I chose what we paid with. The bracelet was mine to pay with. I have been telling myself that for twelve hours, and I think on the bunk just now I finally believed it."* The first time the trade has been talked through. **The bunk has now been named as the place Theron made peace with it.** Scarlet's twelve hours of quiet carrying — lifted in the open by his framing.

Through the *Phoenix Claw*'s night-cycle dimming they go again — Theron taking the longer time he had promised, then a Scarlet-led shorter one, then a quiet third — and fall asleep entwined on the bunk Scarlet finally admits pulls out forty centimetres wider. *Not enough for two people who do not know each other; plenty for two who do.* Theron, who has not slept against another person in three years, gives in to sleep first. Scarlet, last awake, closes her eyes with one of her fingers in the crook of his. **Risa's name held out of the night by both. Mutual care without coordination.**

**The campaign's central question has paid out again, in a register the first payout did not reach.** Where Session 2 tested the *alive, always* line against the salt-house deal, Session 3 tested it in private — against the bracelet that had passed unknowingly through her hands twelve hours earlier, against the closure she had set on the shuttle, against the question of whether the trade could survive being asked about in plain language. It held; was acknowledged; was lifted off her by the man it had cost. The cost-of-the-work theme has acquired a new shape — not the cost of work itself, but the cost of carrying someone whose grief has now become hers to remember.

The morning of day 2 finds Scarlet at the kitchenette in Theron's shirt and her own boots, breakfast made for two, the bulkhead clock reading 06:18 ship-time and the Bonadan vector still four days and some away. Theron asleep in the cabin behind her. *The thing the morning is doing is enough.*
