# The galaxy — Old Republic era

## When this is
Roughly KOTOR-adjacent — millennia before the films. The Mandalorian Wars ended within living memory and left scars across the Outer Rim that have not closed. The Republic is over-extended, its fleet thin, its Senate slow, its Jedi Order quietly fracturing over what to do about a Sith resurgence that some on the Council still refuse to call by name.

The exact year is left loose. Galactic events will sharpen the timeline as Scarlet brushes against them.

## The Republic
Centred on Coruscant, vast and tired. Reaches its arms out across the galaxy through hyperspace lanes and Senate decree, but the Outer Rim has learned to govern itself. Republic law is what the local garrison can enforce that month. In the Core, life is comfortable for those born into it. On the frontier, the Republic is something that arrives in a warship every few years and asks who runs the spaceport now.

## The Jedi Order
Diminished from their height but still the Republic's first line. Their numbers were thinned by the Mandalorian Wars — many followed Revan and Malak to fight, against the Council's wishes, and few of those came back. The Order is internally divided and outwardly cautious. A Jedi on the Outer Rim is a rare sight and usually means something has gone wrong enough to merit one.

## The Sith
Officially extinguished. Practically — not. Cells, cults, schools, lone Dark-siders, ancient ruins that should not be entered. Sith Lords are a rumour in cantinas and a fact in classified Republic briefings. Anyone who has actually met one tends not to be available to talk about it.

## The Mandalorian wreckage
The Mandalorian Wars are over. The Mandalorians lost. The galaxy is full of broken Mandalorian veterans, salvage-fleets full of beskar, planets still glassed, refugee waves still resettling. Some Mandalorians have re-armed quietly. Others have hung up the helmet and become the meanest mercenaries in known space. The clans that remain do not all agree on what comes next.

## Hutt Space and the Smuggler's Moon
The Hutts run the underworld they've always run. Nar Shaddaa — the Smuggler's Moon, orbiting Nal Hutta — is the galaxy's largest port for things and people who do not want to be seen. Stacked vertically: gleaming spires at the top where the Hutt courts and the corporate offworlders live; layer after layer of bars, brothels, fight-pits, fences, slicers, swoop-gangs descending into a permanent twilight; at the bottom, the Undercity, where things live that nobody acknowledges.

Bounty hunters work Nar Shaddaa the way other people work in offices.

## Bounty hunting as a trade
Not yet the codified guild it will become in later eras. The Bounty Hunters' Guild exists in the Outer Rim but its writ is patchy. Most hunters work freelance, pick up bounties off public boards, take side-work from Hutts, syndicates, planetary governments, the occasional Republic warrant. Reputation is everything — for finding work, and for not getting killed by other hunters.

The galaxy's standing assumption is *dead or alive, doesn't matter, bring me proof.* Scarlet's *alive only* rule reads as either eccentric, principled, soft, or all three, depending on who you ask.
