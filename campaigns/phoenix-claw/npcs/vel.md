# Vel

## Status
Alive. Late 30s / 40, human female. Currently at the Iron Lung cantina, Worlport, Ord Mantell. Issuer (or senior representative of the issuer) of the Korel Avash contract.

## Description
Sharp-faced. Dark hair gathered back. A small scar through one eyebrow that she has chosen not to have removed. Dark eyes, "neither warm nor cold — the kind of eyes that are a tool she keeps clean." Lines at the corners of the eyes that have been earned. Carries herself low and unrushed.

## Clothing
A plain coat that reads as plain only from across the street. Up close: collar lined with a fabric that catches lamp-light wrong; inside cuffs edged with something darker. A working coat, made well, deliberately built to read as nothing.

The plain coat is **different in cut** from the plain coats worn by the Nar Shaddaa killers' team. Possibly related, possibly opposed, possibly unrelated. Scarlet has noted the difference.

## Voice & mannerisms
Quiet. No surplus. Reads people fast and is willing to show it as a tactical move — opened her conversation with Scarlet with three observations Scarlet had not realised were visible:
- *"You came in on the right phrase."*
- *"You sat at the wrong angle."*
- *"You walked over at the wrong time."*

Bluffs (or promises) confidently: *"Lying is fine. I will know."* — whether literally true or a senior operator's controlled projection, Scarlet must act as if it is true.

Reassures with touch: covered Avash's hand on the booth table for one second when he laid it palm-down between them.

Sits with the room. At ease in the Iron Lung. A regular, or trusted by the venue's owner (the Mon Calamari), or both.

## Goals & pressures
- The contract on **Korel Avash** — 25,000cr, alive only, 22-day deadline. **Delivery** rather than location: she has direct access to him, sitting with him in a booth thirty minutes before activating the contract relationship with Scarlet.
- Whatever she is reassuring Avash about — he is uneasy about the meeting / the destination / the cover. She is the one settling him.
- Issues contracts under a **spiked-spiral red wax sigil**, on Hutt-format flimsiplast. The spiral is a faction whose mark hunters mutually warn each other off — the Hunters' Roost board has scrub-resistant titles to that effect. Vel either is, represents, or works for that faction.
- The original hunter she gave the contract to (an unknown hunter who took the work and hired Brek'eevo) is presumably out of contact. Vel may or may not know.

## Relationship to Scarlet
**Wary (transactional).** No prior history. Recognises Scarlet only as the new hunter who walked in on the phrase. Has already clocked her timing and seating as off-protocol; is testing how truthful Scarlet will be.

She is willing to do business with a stranger — that is why the contract was posted at the Iron Lung in the first place — but the relationship begins on a tight rein.

## History with Scarlet
- 1st meeting (this scene). Scarlet activated the recognition phrase at the bar while Vel was finishing a meeting with Avash. Vel let Avash leave first, then summoned Scarlet to the booth.
- Vel knows Scarlet is armed, that the lethal carries the smell of recent firing (read off her own attention, or relayed from the Trandoshan), and that she has the discipline to wait without being told twice.

## Knowledge
- The full terms of the Avash contract.
- Avash personally — direct in-person contact. Possibly more than transactional with him; the hand-cover gesture was reassurance.
- The Iron Lung's working protocols (recognition phrase, Mon Calamari relay, side-door client handoff).
- Other hunters working contracts through the Iron Lung — at least one was in and out earlier today (seen by Scarlet from the Bent Hook).
- **The Vesper.** Diagnosed Scarlet's killers as Vesper instantly, no surprise. Prior operational history with them.
- **Renno** — the original hunter she gave the contract to. Had a Twi'lek wife on Nar Shaddaa. Hired Brek'eevo as his slicer. Out of contact for 8 days. Vel has now (post-Scarlet's truth) settled on "killed by the Vesper" as the likely explanation.
- (Probably) why the spiked-spiral faction has a hunter-network reputation bad enough to be warned-against on the Hunters' Roost.
- (Probably) what the destination is for the "alive delivery" of Avash.

## Disclosures to Scarlet this scene
- Named **the Vesper** as the killers' faction.
- Named **Renno** as the original hunter.
- Stated that the Vesper and the spiked spiral are in active conflict over Avash.
- Stated that the Vesper will come back for what Scarlet took.
- Acknowledged that Scarlet outperformed Renno against the same opposition — and that this counts for the contract.
- **Disclosed the contract's true nature: protective extraction under cover.** Avash is not a target to be captured — he is an asset to be relocated alive.
- Stated Avash has been her "guest" for some time and is now leaving because he cannot stay.
- **Handed over operational kit (post-acceptance):**
  - Encrypted dataspike (one-time-read, ship-only) with coordinates, time-window, route recommendations.
  - Half-moon bone token for recognition with the receiving party at the destination.
  - Coded credit-chit for 5,000cr signing.
- **Operational terms:** pickup window 14–36 hrs from now, location to be relayed via a dedicated comm channel; Scarlet comes alone; recognition at destination by matched half-moon; if no match, hold Avash and comm Vel.
- **Personal request:** *"He is a man, and he is afraid. If anything in the next forty-eight hours can be done gently, do it gently. Twenty-five thousand pays for the work. It does not pay for the rest."*
- Parted with *"Welcome to it. Be very, very careful."* — first real (small, professional) smile.
- Has **not** named her own faction (the spiked spiral remains unnamed).
- Has **not** disclosed her name (no first or last given). Did not ask for Scarlet's.

## Operational pattern (observed)
- Vel uses Iron Lung contract activations to recruit hunters with faces the Vesper do not know.
- She briefs her assets (Avash) on the type of hunter to expect, settling their anxiety before handoff.
- She runs operations under deniable cover — bounty contracts as transport tickets.
- She moves slowly through her decisions but, once decided, executes precisely.
