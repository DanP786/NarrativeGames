# Session 02 — 10 May 2026 (real-world, continuing)

## Scene 1 — *Phoenix Claw* approach → Ord Mantell, Kel Sahr → Worlport

Scarlet wakes on sublight approach to Ord Mantell with her wrist throbbing and the Avash contract still undecided in the floor-locker. She showers (ration-water; in the mirror she sees the Zabrak's fingerprints around her neck for the first time in good light), eats half a protein bar against pain-nausea, and rides Ord Mantell up under the canopy from a fourth-time-familiar angle.

She picks **Kel Sahr** — a small working port on the planet's quieter continent — over the main hub at Worlport. Routine dock-control. Pays the dockmaster's deputy *Dol Maza*, a Sullustan she has used twice before, and tips him for a name. Bone work, discreet. He gives her *"the wrench's wife,"* two streets east, yellow door.

**Dr. Mira Vesh** runs a clean back-clinic behind her husband Henrik's tool-and-die shop. A burn-scar on her forearm in the shape of beskar that was hot once — Mandalorian Wars field medic, almost certainly. She bone-knits the wrist (hairline of the radius at the styloid), wraps the throat as a bonus while the knit runs, takes 1,200 credits without asking what Scarlet does for a living, and tells her *"if anything else comes up while you're on Ord Mantell, Dol knows how to find me."* Parting line: *"You move careful. You'll be fine."*

Back on the *Phoenix Claw*, Scarlet runs four HoloNet queries through the local relay:

- **Korel Avash** has a thin public profile — a small import/export business, *Avash & Co.*, registered fifteen years ago on **Dolomar**, postal-drop only. Modest revenue; nothing else. Either he is quietly living his life, or he is very good at looking like he is.
- The **spiked spiral** sigil returns nothing on heraldic and faction databases. Image-search surfaces ONE hit: a Hunters' Roost board post title from eight months back — ***"Spiral-marked clients — wait, don't take it."*** Post body, author, all eleven replies: deleted. The scrub is recent and total. **Hunters know to refuse this faction's work, and someone with reach has gone back to suppress the warnings.**
- The **Iron Lung** is in **Worlport**, the planet's main city on the western continent. Six hours suborbital from Kel Sahr.
- **Brek's tracking ledger** plots three Avash sightings (Dolomar, Vella IV mining world, Trent's Reach station — most recent five weeks ago) and one fourth entry tagged with the spiked spiral at **Mortuda** — a dead system with no commercial activity for two centuries. **Brek was tracking the spiral's people to a place nobody goes.**

Synthesis: *Vel is paying 25,000cr to a stranger across the planet because hunters who know the spiral refuse the work. Scarlet is the stranger.*

She takes it. *"The decision arrives without ceremony — it has been sitting on the table since hyperspace, and all the research has done is name the price."*

She packs a small bag — the contract sheets, a copy of her tracking notes, two changes of clothes, bacta patches, datapad, comm, credstick, a spare comlink, paracord. Leaves the carbine, the job-pack, and the Rodian's knife on the ship. Locks the *Phoenix Claw* at Kel Sahr, pays Dol Maza an extra day's berthing, and takes a commercial suborbital to Worlport. ID and weapons declared at standard frontier-carrier check; she travels under her real name (she has no others).

She arrives in Worlport in late afternoon. Glass-and-steel terminal, river-cut city, rain thinking about starting. The docks district is east. She walks.

## Scene 4 — Tarvane's Quay: The Pyx job, the kiss, the capture, the revelation

After accepting the contract over the encrypted relay (female-voice broker — *The Phantom Voice* — who confirmed knowing the Avash work), Scarlet vectored to **Tarvane's Quay** (Bothan-run station above gas-giant Sothen). On-station she paid 30cr to a young Bothan info-trader, **Voya**, for working intel on Pyx — at the Gold Hour gambling hall twice already, sleeping above **Bres**'s tailor shop on deck six under the alias **"Mister Vahn,"** waiting on an off-station buyer, with one other hunter on him (Mirialan, **Cael Soros**, working from Voya's aunt's intel-packets, no sightline yet).

### Scene 4a — The Gold Hour bar
Scarlet dressed in the red dress kit (combat boots, knife in right boot, both blasters left on ship, slipsleep tab case concealed). Sat at the bar with her back to Pyx for eleven minutes. He came over. The cover-work was mutual, immediate, and unusually equal: *"The boots are giving you away,"* he opened. She read him back — calluses, glass-turning, second-drink-not-being-drunk, still-working posture. He gave his working name (*"Pyx"*) and watched her face when he did. **She passed it.** She offered her real first name. He drank her second drink in.

### Scene 4b — The transit-deck observation strip
Eight minutes through a service hatch he knew, onto a curved transit-deck walkway with **Sothen** filling the upper half of the transparisteel. Her working mask slipped for one count of breath at the window and her recovery was too clean. Pyx caught both: *"Both of us are allowed one."* When she deflected with *"You have to take in the beauty sometimes,"* he turned fully from the glass, stepped into her space and repointed the line at her face: *"Yes. We do."* She leaned in. He kissed her — slow, unhurried, hand at the angle of her jaw with the thumb at the cheek, third button still fastened. For one count of breath she stopped thinking about the job. For the next count she named, only to herself, *that she liked him.* He used her name afterwards: *"Hello, Scarlet."*

She turned the conversation operational — when did his contact arrive (two more days, three at the outside) and could he take her to his bed (*"Not my bed... the place I am sleeping has things in it I cannot have a stranger near, and until I have asked you a question I have been holding off asking, you are a stranger... I have not wanted to count yours."*). She pulled away with a dismissive tease. He released her without chasing: *"That was a working answer."* When she came back demanding he name what he suspected — *"A thief? A spy?"* — he replied *"Not a thief. Not a spy,"* and then, the offered off-ramp: *"I am sorrier if I have read you right and you make me find out the hard way."*

### Scene 4c — The drug and the walk
Scarlet's working-face emerged, third-face. She had palmed a **slipsleep** tab into Pyx's second drink at the bar, during the Devaronian-watch window — delayed-onset hunter's sedative, ~10–15 min lag. *"You are going to find out the easy way."* He clocked his own slowing voice — *"Scarlet,"* steady, no weight — and asked *"How long."* She walked him through the inner-ring concourse on a drunk-girlfriend silhouette, brushed off a Bothan concourse-aide with *"He owes me dinner, two months running. I'll get him home,"* exchanged *"Long night." / "Long night."* with the dockmaster's deputy at pad 7, and put him in her cell with the carbonite-cuff at his ankle, third button finally loosened.

### Scene 4d — Return to the flat above Bres's
Scarlet took Pyx's keys from his coat and went back to the deck-six flat. Bres saw her face twice and asked nothing — *Bres on station discretion.* She took everything from the flat (a half-packed duffel ready to move, spare comm, slim datapad, bypass-tool roll, blaster pistol, folding knife, credit-pouch, sealed envelope of paper notes, and — wrapped in oilcloth in the duffel's false-floor compartment — **a small ornate metal box, palm-sized, locked, heavy for its size**). *Phoenix Claw* lifted clean on a generic vector, folded into transit space ten minutes later. **No flight plan filed.**

### Scene 4e — The cell, in transit (~4 hrs out)
Working uniform on, dress dead on the cabin floor, Pyx's life laid out on the deck-plate around her, the box in her lap. She made caf for both of them. Keyed the bulkhead transparent. *"Promise I didn't spike that one."* Pyx drank, did his working-pro inventory of clothes/cuff/duffel/box/her face/mug, registered that **she had gone back to the flat,** and conceded her work: *"That was a good drink, Scarlet. The first one, I mean."*

What followed was an extended exchange of disclosures with the kiss in the air between them:
- Pyx named the two tells that gave her away (the patience at the bar, the recovery from the mask-slip) and gave her unsolicited hunter-craft advice: *"Civilians do not snap back. They wander."* The first warmth in his voice since the strip.
- Pyx confessed the run: *"I have been on the run for the better part of three years, Scarlet... I knew the shape of you before I came over. And I came over anyway. The kiss was not the cover, Scarlet. The cover was the cover. The kiss was the thing the cover let me have. If you are asking would I have done it knowing how it ended — yes."*
- Scarlet, internally attracted at a new level, held it off: *"If you weren't behind a cell I'd believe you."*
- Pyx confirmed the box was the Aargau prize, named himself as *the open,* and warned her against breaking it.
- Scarlet declared the contract was *the man, not the box.* Pyx recalibrated visibly (the small mouth-corner pull): her buyer was not the Aargau party. *"Whose hand on the comm hired you, Scarlet."*
- Scarlet told him the truth she had: encrypted relay buoy, female voice, the **same broker who handled her Avash work** — a protective extraction of a man called **Korel Avash** to Mortuda. Pyx placed Avash from a spring Coruscant trade-vote story *that did not survive the day,* and concluded: *"I do not, yet, know which side of the table I am sitting at."*

### Scene 4f — The box stashed, the duffel packed, the envelope opened
Scarlet stashed the locked box in the *Phoenix Claw*'s false-floor compartment under her bunk (empty since the Avash work). Packed Pyx's duffel in silence — clothes, comm, datapad, bypass-roll, his blaster (with the power-pack removed into her hip-pouch), knife, credits — with a working knot, set against the bulkhead visible to him, the way a thing is packed for the person who will lift it again. She did not pack the sealed envelope.

**She opened it.** Four sheets, careful small hand, dated four months gone. A drafted confession of the spring Coruscant trade-vote scandal: a bloc paid to break their vote-commitment through a back-channel via a relay buoy in Bothan space; the vote killed; names redacted to initials with one annotation reading **"A. killed July."** *(Scarlet had moved A. alive in May.)* The wax seal — broken once and re-pressed before her — reassembled into **a small spiked spiral.** The same impression as the wax on the Hutt-format paper that had started the Avash contract.

### Scene 4g — The fact-pattern resolves
Scarlet worked it through in silence on a corridor crate while Pyx watched her face change. The picture: **the spiked spiral is a major political-shadow faction** with reach through relay-buoy infrastructure in Bothan space, a private vault on Aargau, both protective-extraction and asset-recovery operations, and the buying of a Coruscant trade vote in the spring. Avash was their man (the *"killed July"* line is the cover they built for him). Pyx hit their Aargau vault three weeks ago — the box AND the dossier. The Phantom Voice broker is their contracting arm. **Her current contract is from the same faction she worked for on Avash — to recover their stolen man for torture and death.** The dossier in her hand is leverage on the faction itself.

She stood, paced, and named the binding choice:

> *"I don't kill people. Not that I haven't, in tight spots. But — what you've got yourself into is going to get you killed. Hear me out."*

**Scene closes mid-speech.** Day-3 delivery is ~1.7 days out. The decision implicit in her opening line is that **she will not deliver**.

## Scene 5 — *Phoenix Claw* mid-transit, the Worlport salt house, and the closing of the page

### Scene 5a — The plan, in hyperspace
Scarlet, mid-speech at the bulkhead, paced once and laid out the trade: she would deliver Pyx at the rendezvous, but offer Vel the box and the spiral-sealed dossier in exchange for the bounty AND Pyx's life. Pyx, listening from the cell, named the floor of the negotiation: he had a buyer for the box already lined up to pay more than 35k, but the buyer could not protect him from the spiked spiral's reach; if Scarlet's plan worked, he would owe her his life and lose the leverage at the same time. He accepted the plan. Scarlet plotted Kel Sahr (Ord Mantell, her home port), folded a second time on a vector she had not filed. The cell stayed unlocked-but-closed. They both slept.

### Scene 5b — The morning, before the meeting
Scarlet woke before her alarm. Changed (brown low-rise trousers, scuffed field boots, cream wrap-front X-cross vest, cropped brown leather jacket with fur collar, hair up tight; Pyx's blaster power-pack in her left hip pocket). 1hr 12min to Kel Sahr. The Phantom Voice had relayed during the silent hour: **Worlport, western continent, deck nine of the old salt house. One hour from your arrival. Alone.** Scarlet made breakfast — eggs, flatbread, cured meat, caf — not trying to be quiet. Pyx woke. Drank. Looked at her properly: *"You are landing."* Then: *"Sleep well? You did not... Talk to me. I have spent the night running the same calculation you have."* Scarlet, arms crossed: *"I don't want you dead..."* Pyx reframed it, mildly: *"If you can tell me what you do not want, we have a floor under the conversation."* She did not match him. Smirked off the bedroom-tease, packed up the breakfast plate.

### Scene 5c — Cuffed, off the ship, into Worlport
After two hours of silence in transit-fold, Scarlet cuffed Pyx (*"Hands. Front."*) and walked him off the *Phoenix Claw* past Dol Maza's small nod at the Kel Sahr gate. Suborbital 40min hop to Worlport. Through rain-runoff alleys to the salt-house district. **The old salt house deck nine** — four storeys, grey, rusted plate, tired loading-bay light, a fresh chalked spiked-spiral at the lintel. Two streets short, in the shadow of a freight gantry, Pyx broke silence: *"Talk to me. You have brought me to the meeting. You have brought the box and the dossier. Tell me what I am walking into."* Scarlet: *"I have a plan where we both live. Don't talk."* He nodded.

### Scene 5d — The salt house, the trade
Past a plain-coated lookout at the pedestrian door. Concrete bay, work-lights at quarter output, square table center, three chairs, two operatives (door + back wall), second pedestrian door far wall. **Vel at the table.** *"Welcome back, Scarlet. You moved very quickly. You always do."*

Scarlet steered Pyx to the bench, sat at the table opposite Vel, set the locked box on the wood, gestured at her left pocket where the dossier was, and made the offer:

> *"I'm asking for what the contract was. Take both. Pay me the bounty. Let me walk Pyx out. He never appears on your boards again."*

Vel took the offer in silence. Then:

> *"That is a smaller number than I would have offered you if you had asked. The trade is acceptable. One thing — before he walks out, he opens it. Here. In this room. With me looking. Contents into satchel through the back door. You and he walk out the front. Acceptable?"*

Scarlet raised an eyebrow at Pyx; he nodded yes. She uncuffed him in front of the room. Pyx walked to the table, worked the lock silently, did not look inside, did not lift the lid, stepped back half a pace. Scarlet placed the dossier on the table: *"We good?"* Vel produced a pre-staged satchel from beside her chair, slid the lid-down box and the dossier into it, tied a working knot — the back-wall man took it through the second pedestrian door. Vel produced a credstick from her coat, slid it to Scarlet: ***"We are good."***

To Pyx: *"You are off these boards from this hour. You understand that the boards include a list of names. The list is not large. You stay off the list, you stay off the boards. Yes?"* Pyx looked at Scarlet first, then: *"Yes."*

Vel, not standing: ***"Good. Walk."***

### Scene 5e — Worlport pavement, the drink offer
Outside, the rotated lookout did not look at them. Scarlet walked out without looking back. One block in: *"I can take you off world or you can just pick up your thing and go on your way."* Pyx, one block before answering: *"Off-world with you. And then I would like to buy you a drink somewhere neither of us has worked. If you will let me."* He walked at her right shoulder, sleeve brushing hers on corners, did not take her hand.

### Scene 5f — The suborbital back, the disclosure cascade
Half-empty shuttle. They sat side-by-side. Pyx stretched his legs out — *the small relaxed thing a man does when he is not, for the first time in three years, sitting in a public space watching the doors.* About 20 minutes in, quiet: *"I have not been alone with somebody who knew my working name in a long time. Not somebody who was not paid to be alone with me."*

Scarlet leaned closer: *"So what's your real name?"*

He thought. Looked at her properly. ***"Theron. Just Theron. The other half I have kept off the boards as long as the first half. You can have it the night you ask me a second time."*** Then: *"You are the first person who has had a name out of me in a year and a half. The last one is dead."*

Scarlet: *"How did they die?"*

The short version: ***"Her name was Risa.***" 18 months working together, *"some of that was working; some of it was the part of working that is not."* Wrong-call on routes after a job. He took his exit; she took hers. The people whose box they had taken were waiting on her route. Took her two hours after he was clear; an hour dead by the time he reached the meet; body moved. *"I have a working theory about who did it and have not, in eighteen months, found anything to disprove it."* Then, quietly: ***"The Aargau job was not entirely a job. I have not told that to anyone."***

Scarlet, shifting her angle toward him: *"What was it?"*

A bracelet. Plain silver, mag-band closure, working data-chip in the underside (courier-style ID). **Risa wore it on the job that killed her.** On her wrist when she went out her route, gone when they moved her body. The Aargau private vault was where the spiked spiral had been keeping it — the chip carried her credentials and job-tags from a year of their working partnership, *"all of which incidentally ties them to a year of our work. They could not afford to destroy it. So they kept it where they kept things they could not afford to destroy."* He wanted it back partly to ID the killers, *"most of it was because it was hers."* And, without weight: ***"You have just traded it back to them."***

Scarlet: *"Why didn't you tell me it meant something to you before I just traded it back?"*

Two reasons. **First (the trade-quality reason):** if he had told her in the gantry shadow, she would have tried to keep the bracelet, Vel would have read the seam, and the room would have escalated. **Second (the harder reason):** ***"I have been carrying Risa for eighteen months. The bracelet was part of carrying her. Trading it back was the first time in eighteen months I have been able to make a choice that put someone alive in front of someone dead. I needed to be the one to make that choice. I needed you not to make it for me by knowing in advance and writing me out of it."***

### Scene 5g — Scarlet pulls back; Theron accepts
Scarlet, turning away: *"I'm sorry about your girlfriend."* (Used *girlfriend* rather than the name he just gave her. Pulled back the lean.)

Theron heard the distancing. Did not reach for the space. ***"Thank you, Scarlet."*** Then offered her room: *"You do not have to know what to do with what I have told you. You have an hour to land and to think and to land a second time. I will be at your shoulder while you do. If you would prefer me not to be."*

Scarlet: *"I offered to take you off world, so that's what I'll do."*

Theron — single nod, the same one he gave at the salt-house door. *"All right. Anywhere a long way from Ord Mantell will be enough. I will move under my own name from wherever you set me down."* Then: ***"Thank you, Scarlet."*** — name used to close, not warm. Did not look at her again. Shuttle's wheels touched.

### Scene 5h — *Phoenix Claw*, resupply, the second smile
Through Dol Maza's gate. Pad 7. Scarlet handed Theron the run of her ship while she went to the Kel Sahr spacer market — 43cr at the Zabrak vendor's stall (eggs, bread, hard cheese, caf grounds, preserved fruit, fresh greens). Returned to find Theron in the galley, press on. *"Made yourself at home,"* she joked. He countered, dry: *"I have been on enough ships I do not own to know where the press lives. I hope you do not mind."* Set a mug on the counter on her side, didn't push it. She unpacked, took the mug, said *"thanks,"* walked to the cockpit. Over her shoulder: *"So where do you want to go?"*

Theron, voice carrying: ***"Bonadan."*** The eastern spaceport, the third quarter, *"a stretch of pawn-and-trade since I was younger than you are. The customs there are not friendly but they are not curious either. I can be a man with my own name and a small line of credit inside a week."* 5-6 days transit. Stopped at the cockpit doorway, leaned on the frame.

### Scene 5i — Universe-and-Pakuuni; the lift
Scarlet sat back in her seat: *"You know why I became a bounty hunter? Not because I love justice but because I want to see the universe. I can make Bonadan work."* Theron stepped in two paces, took the co-pilot seat, set his mug beside hers — not touching — looked at the same instrument array.

> *"I have seen a fair bit of it. Not all of it the way you want to. There is a stretch of coast on Pakuuni — not a port, just a coast — where the tide pulls back about three kilometres twice a day and the locals walk out on the wet floor to pick blue urchins. I went there once for a week between jobs. I did not work. I did not do anything. I ate urchins and watched the tide. If you ever find yourself in that quarter, set down at a place called Sirinno and ask for the south road. That is the piece of the universe I would offer back."*

***First smile she had seen on him.***

Scarlet, teasing, plotting vector: *"Bonadan is it, and 5 days of living with me in close quarters."* Theron, second small smile: *"I have lived in a single cubic metre of a Bonadan dockside hostel for three months at a time. Five days in your ship with a galley and a cot is a vacation by my measurement... Lift when you are ready."*

*Phoenix Claw* climbed off pad 7 in a clean line; Kel Sahr fell back to a small grey-and-brown patch on the curve of the world; two mugs cooled side by side on the console. Bonadan, five days ahead.
