# Session 01 — 10 May 2026 (real-world)

## Scene 1 — Nar Shaddaa, the Throat (mid-levels) → Dock 14-North → hyperspace (local night)

The Brek'eevo job. Three days into a tail on a Twi'lek slicer who skipped on a Hutt's gambling debt — a routine 2,000-credit Yelba bounty, posted alive. Scarlet tracks Brek'eevo to tenement 7-J in *the Throat*, a stack-tenement in Nar Shaddaa's mid-levels, and finds the door ajar, the smell of blood under the cooking smell, and two voices inside finishing a search.

She stows the stun pistol in her jacket pocket and walks in playing a drunk girlfriend — an act she has used in other ports. Brek'eevo is dead on the carpet. Two professionals are in the room: a calm human searching flimsiplast, and a Rodian with a wet knife. The human reads her, smiles the shape of a smile, and tells her to come in and close the door. *"Sweetheart. Brek didn't mention you. Come in. Close the door."* A third — a Zabrak grappler — closes the door behind her from the corridor.

The human tests her with a probing question while the Rodian goes for her satchel (which holds binders, a stun grenade, and a tenement-routed nav-slate — every cover-blow she has). *"Brek had bad people looking for him — you'd have known that. You did know that. Right, sweetheart?"*

Scarlet acts first. Bait-feint to the visible lethal, left-hand draw of the stowed stun, point-blank under the Rodian's jaw. Snap-shot over the falling body — the human takes a stun bolt across the chest and folds over the table. The Zabrak takes her from behind, choke-and-wrist hold, and her air starts to go.

Right-hand draw of the lethal blaster — **the first time she has fired it on a job** — and a disabling shot through the top of his boot. Elbow back-and-up to his face. She steps clear and holds him at gunpoint. He tells her, professional through the pain, that the team comms in every 20 minutes, that a second team is inbound, and that he left a corridor cam running in 7-I — *her face is on the tape.* His parting line, smiling with blood: *"Run, sweetheart."*

She stuns him, holsters both blasters, grabs as much of the flimsiplast as her injured left wrist allows, picks up the Rodian's knife on impulse, and runs. Down through the Throat, out onto the catwalk — where she sees a plain speeder descending on the tenement's roof and two more plain-coats watching the front exit. The second team is real.

She takes the long way back to the docks on foot, low profile, ~40 minutes through Nar Shaddaa mid-level traffic. Catches a watcher on a passing swoop and breaks the line of sight with a vendor cart and a protocol droid. Catches her own reflection in a closed shop window and does not, for one second, recognise herself. The papers in her jacket are damp where they touch her vest. She thinks, walking, about the Zabrak's foot — not the shot, but the sound.

She boards the *Phoenix Claw* clean (Dock 14-North, berth 7-left). Pre-flights, requests departure, lifts off, jumps to Ord Mantell — the truth, as filed. In hyperspace, alone, she finally lets her hands shake.

### What this scene did to her
- **The line bent.** Fired the lethal blaster on a job for the first time. Did not kill — disabled. She is intact, but the gun on her right thigh is no longer just metal.
- **The work changed.** She took the papers the killers were after. She does not know what they say. She has chosen, for the moment, to carry them.
- **The galaxy noticed.** Her face is on a tape. A team of disciplined professionals will, by the Zabrak's word, be hunting her. The "just passing through" self-image is in active threat.
- **Injuries:** left wrist (bone-bruised / possible hairline), throat (choke bruising), residual adrenaline crash.
- **Unfinished business:** the Hutt majordomo Yelba is owed an answer about Brek'eevo. She has not paid that debt and may not be able to safely.

## Scene 2 — *Phoenix Claw*, hyperspace transit, ~14 hours

Quiet decompression. Scarlet bacta-patches and splints her left wrist (probable hairline, kept tight-but-with-blood). Sorts the flimsiplast from 7-J into three piles — Brek'eevo's small life (cantina chits, a docking paycheck two systems back, a torn corner with a girlfriend's name), Brek's unreadable slicer shorthand, and the prize.

The prize: a private bounty contract on **Korel Avash** — human male, mid-fifties, alive only, 25,000 credits, 22-day deadline. Contact at *The Iron Lung* cantina on Ord Mantell, docks district. Drink: *"a Tatooine sour, no salt."* Recognition phrase: *"Vel sent me."* The seal is a small spiked spiral in red wax — a sigil Scarlet does not recognise. Not Hutt. Not anything she has seen on a job before. Brek's tracking ledger shows Avash moved through multiple systems in recent months, with the spiked-spiral mark recurring in the margin taxonomy. A third-hand single-line note on different stock, folded once: *"Avash will know if he is followed. Use a face he does not know."*

She stows the papers in a floor-locker false-underside she installed herself three years ago — "not because she had ever needed to hide anything. Because she had wanted to know she could." Locks the cockpit, sets proximity alerts at ten light-seconds, turns in. Stun pistol on the wall shelf above the bunk. The splint pulls when she lies down.

She lies a long time before she sleeps, thinking about Brek'eevo. He did not look like a man who held a 25,000-credit contract on someone who knew how to spot a tail. **He was more than he looked.** Or someone had given him something to hold. Or someone had set him up to be holding it when they came. She does not pick one. Picking one is a morning problem.

The hyperdrive hums. She sleeps.

### Character beats (scene 2)
- The false-bottom floor-locker — *"not because she had ever needed to hide anything. Because she had wanted to know she could."* (Quiet competence; the kind of paranoia that is a discipline.)
- The three hypotheses about Brek'eevo, not yet decided.
