# The post-war Wizarding World — September 1998

## The war
Voldemort fell at the Battle of Hogwarts on 2 May 1998, four months ago. The Ministry has spent the summer trying to look like it knows what it is doing.

## The Ministry
Kingsley Shacklebolt is Minister, having taken office uncontested in the immediate aftermath. He is genuinely a reformer; he is also a man trying to rebuild an institution while it is still on fire. Trials of captured Death Eaters are ongoing — overcrowded, slow, occasionally summary. Several known Death Eaters remain at large. Aurors are stretched thin.

## Pure-blood families
The old families are quiet. Some — Malfoys, Notts, Selwyns, Yaxleys, Rosiers — lost members to the war or to the trials that followed. The estates that survive have closed their gates. Old alliances are silent or broken. There is talk, in private rooms, of who paid and who didn't.

## Hogwarts
Rebuilt. McGonagall is Headmistress. Many staff are new — replacements for the dead, the disgraced, or the simply tired. The Slytherin dungeons feel emptier than usual; some families pulled their children, others lost them. The student body is jittery, watchful, divided. House lines have hardened.

## Public mood
The Daily Prophet is celebrating. The streets are quieter. Most people want to believe it is over. A growing minority — those who lost too much, those who suspect too much — are not so sure.

## Diagon Alley & Gringotts
Diagon Alley is rebuilt but thinner — fewer shops, more boarded windows. Gringotts is open but tense. After the Lestrange-vault break-in during the war, the goblins and the wizards are not on speaking terms; they conduct business and nothing else. Old-blood vault clients are received with the same icy correctness as everyone else, and watched a little harder.
