# Tone & rules

## Tone
Shadows-of-the-war. The official story is that the Wizarding World is healing. The truth is messier. Death Eaters are scattered, not destroyed. The Ministry under Kingsley Shacklebolt is reformist but compromised — old sympathies still walk the halls. Old-blood families have buried their dead and closed their doors. Hogwarts is rebuilt; the stones remember which corridors people died in.

## Content posture
Nothing off the table. Graphic violence, dark magic shown in detail, sexuality, torture, moral despair — all on the table when the fiction calls for it.

## Agency posture
Gritty realism. Cass succeeds at things she's good at, but everything has cost. Failure is frequent and consequential. The world does not bend to keep her safe.

## Death rule
**Hard death enabled.** Cass can die. Lethal failures in lethal stakes resolve as death. On her death, narrate it with weight; the player chooses to start a new campaign or continue with a successor character in the same world.

## Voice
Watchful. Restrained. Lean into silence and what is not said. The post-war world is full of people who have learned to keep their faces still. Match them.
