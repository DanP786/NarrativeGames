# Narrative

Cassiopeia Rosier returns to Hogwarts for her fifth year, four months after her parents died at the Battle of Hogwarts in service of the Dark Lord. She did not fight. She is the last of her line.

## The mother's bequest
On the morning of 1 September 1998, in the Rosier vault, Cass discovers a blood-sealed drawer her mother left for her. Inside: a brass key to an unknown lock, and a cipher-note in three words — the first two readable (**Hollowfield**, **Rookery**), the third sealed by a cipher key Cass was never taught. Her mother's letter, read on the train and burned, points Cass toward a place named in the third word, where a custodian — a woman Cass does not remember meeting — holds something her mother could not commit to paper. The recognition protocol is the rose-and-thorn seal and the speaking of her parents' names.

The third cipher key is held by Ambrose Brookhouse, the family solicitor, who has known of "the place" all his life and has had cause to use the key once before. Selene Rosier dated the bequest **28 April 1998** — four days before her death.

## Re-entry into school
The Rosier name moves ahead of her like weather. On the train back, Daphne Greengrass — eighth-year returnee, two years above Cass — extends her family's hand on her mother's behalf. The Greengrasses are positioning themselves as old-blood-but-not-Death-Eater, and Daphne's reading of Cass on the platform is sharp enough to see lies and old enough to leave them alone. Cass's actual dorm-mate Astoria Greengrass — Daphne's younger sister — has begun, gently, to declare a side. The wider school is divided: some afraid, some openly hostile (a Gryffindor boy in her year has already heckled her in the corridor), a few pitying. Pansy Parkinson, on the train, was prevented from showing what she actually thought; whoever's holding her leash is holding it tightly.

This is the story of what Cass does with what her mother left her, and who she becomes in doing it.
