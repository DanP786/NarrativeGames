# Session 01 — 9 May 2026 (real-world)

## Scene 1 — The Rosier Vault, Gringotts (1 Sep 1998, morning)
Cass meets Brookhouse, the family solicitor, in the deep Rosier vault on the morning of her return to Hogwarts. Three documents on the table: routine trust paperwork, a Ministry petition requesting consent to inventory the vault for "items of dark provenance," and a sealed letter from her mother bearing the rose-and-thorn seal, held by Brookhouse for four months. Cass signs the trust paperwork without comment and asks Brookhouse to attend the inventory; he agrees, having done two such inventories already this summer.

Walking the vault, she finds cabinets open and items missing since Brookhouse's last attended visit at Yule — the Crisanthemum Set, three genealogies, and the reliquary that sat on the back plinth. Footprints in the dust at the back: a woman's tread, light, walking in to the empty plinth and not returning. With the bone-handled family dagger she opens a blood-sealed drawer in the genealogy cabinet base. Inside a black lacquered box keyed to her blood: a plain brass key (unknown lock, no felt enchantment), a parchment in the Rosier cipher reading **Hollowfield / Rookery / [unreadable third word]**, and an enamel rose set into the lid dated **28 April 1998** — four days before her mother's death. She closes the drawer without taking anything, signs the petition with Brookhouse's added stipulation, and pockets her mother's letter unopened.

**Memorable line.** Brookhouse, parting: *"I am at the firm any night you need me. Owl. Floo. In person. Anything."*

## Scene 2 — Platform 9¾ and the Hogwarts Express (1 Sep 1998, ~10:50–14:00)
Cass arrives at King's Cross via Floo, alone, twenty-eight minutes before departure. On the platform she approaches Daphne Greengrass — eighth-year Slytherin returnee, two years above her — and stands silent. Daphne, after winning the silence game, calls her *"Cassie"* (the first time anyone outside Brookhouse has used the name since the funeral) and offers her a seat in the carriage. She sends Astoria — Cass's actual fifth-year dorm-mate — to fetch pumpkin pasties as a pretext, then delivers her mother's careful message: the Greengrasses did not attend the Rosier funeral because they were not invited, and she wants Cass to know that.

Cass deflects with a half-truth — *"I couldn't say, my parents left instructions in their will. You didn't miss much, the Ministry being there made it even more uncomfortable."* Daphne hears the seam, accepts the cover, and softens for one beat: *"I am sorry about your mother. She was kind to me once, when I was twelve."* The Greengrass position is now legible — neutral old-blood, hand quietly extended.

Walking to the carriage, Astoria offers Cass a pasty before her sister — a small, deliberate breach of protocol. In the corridor between platform and seat, the rest of the school's mood becomes plain: a third-year mixed-house compartment falls silent as Cass passes (the afraid); a Gryffindor fifth-year boy with a scar across his nose props the door with his foot and tells her *"Walk faster, Rosier — we've got OWLs on this train"* (the hostile); a packed compartment of parentless Slytherin first-years watches her pass like dogs that have been kicked once. One small girl with pale eyes nods. Cass does not return it.

In the carriage two-from-the-back: Pansy Parkinson and Millicent Bulstrode (also eighth-year returnees). Pansy's first look is openly hostile, then visibly bitten back — she has been told to manage herself this term. Cass takes the window seat. Astoria beside her. Tracey Davis joins after the trolley's third pass, loud and unrepentant, and she and Daphne carry the conversation.

Walking with Astoria toward the carriage, Cass asks low how her summer was. Astoria, unguarded: family kept behind the orchard wall all summer; her father took Daphne to two trials "to be seen sitting" but refused Astoria; she wrote Cass a letter in July, her mother said it was *"premature,"* she did not send it. *"I don't think it was premature. I'm sorry your summer was — "* Cass: *"Thanks."* Walks on.

After about an hour Cass excuses herself to the toilet. In the locked cubicle she opens her mother's letter. **Selene Rosier**, writing with knowledge of her own death, does not apologise for being a Death Eater; tells Cass to reach her own conclusions honestly; points her to the blood-sealed drawer (already opened); names the brass key and the cipher note as Cass's bequest; reveals that **Brookhouse holds the third Rosier cipher key** — he has used it once before — and will give Cass the third reading once he sees she has opened the drawer. The letter names a custodian: *"the one who keeps it has known you since you were a child, though you will not remember her."* The recognition protocol is the rose-and-thorn seal and the speaking of her parents' names.

**Memorable line.** Selene's closing: *"I have loved you with a pride that frightened me sometimes, and I leave you a name that is heavier than I meant it to be. Carry it well, or set it down. The choice was always going to be yours."* Cass burns the letter (Incendio, controlled), washes the ash, returns to the carriage. No physical record exists.
