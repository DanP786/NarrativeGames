# Current scene — The Sorting Feast, Slytherin fifth-year stretch (1 Sep 1998, ~20:00)

## Beats
- The Sorting ends. McGonagall — briefly, more briefly than Dumbledore would have — welcomes the school and signals the feast. Food appears.
- Cass takes a small portion of everything within reach and arranges it on her plate the way her mother taught her — careful as something to do with her hands. Eats slowly. Astoria does the same beside her, less practised.
- The fifth-year stretch is initially quiet. The girls — Davis, Roper, Selwyn, the Gamp cousin — talk in low voices about safe things (holidays, a horse, hampers). The boys are quieter still. Yaxley does not lift his eyes from his plate.
- A sixth-year boy named **Marcus** opens the assault three places down on Cass's right. Three escalating lines, all pitched to carry, in the Slytherin trick of musing-aloud-in-a-public-place:
  - *"— didn't think she'd have the nerve, honestly."*
  - *"It's the seat I find interesting. The seat is a *choice*, isn't it."*
  - Names his cousin **Theo Nott** ("in Azkaban. Pending, anyway.") and lists the gutted houses around the table — three Notts, two Selwyns, the Yaxleys, "and the *Rosiers*. Both. Both Rosiers. And here she is. In the gap. Eating her beef."
- **Selwyn**, the 5th-year girl, says *"Don't, Marcus"* — but in the way you say *don't* when you also want to hear what comes next. Spot of colour rises high on her cheekbone.
- A seventh-year prefect Cass does not know — **Hadley** — intervenes, mild but firm: *"That's enough. […] You weren't talking to her, you were talking *at* her. There's a difference. Eat your dinner."*
- Marcus pushes once more — *"I just wonder what it must be like. To walk in here. To sit in *that* seat. Knowing what your father and mother — "* — and Hadley cuts him off harder. Marcus subsides, but with eyes on Cass, waiting for her to give him something to work with.
- Cass sets her knife and fork down (quarter to three, neat together). Lifts and resets her water goblet. Holds the silence one beat past polite. Then looks at Marcus.
- **Cass's reply**, levelly, in the carrying voice her mother used at table:
  *"You wondered what it must be like. I can tell you. It's like sitting at a table with people who have been thinking about my dead parents all summer, and have only just worked up the nerve to ask. — Was there anything else, Marcus?"*
- Marcus opens his mouth, closes it. Hears in his own head exactly how a follow-up would sound. Looks at his plate. Hadley picks up his goblet without comment; his mouth is doing something it is trying not to do.
- **Selwyn** meets Cass's eye for the first time tonight. Something between reassessment and very small relief. (Possible warming — to be confirmed by future contact.)
- The **Gamp girl** breaks the silence pragmatically: *"Pass the potatoes, would you, Marcus."* Marcus passes the potatoes.
- Astoria releases a breath she had been holding. Four down the bench, the **pale-eyed Slytherin first-year** has lowered her goblet and is watching Cass with the absolute attention of a small animal that has just learned something.
- The exchange will reach Daphne (eighth-year end, far seat) before pudding, carried up the bench by people who want to be the first to bring her the news.
- Cass picks up her cutlery. Cuts another piece of beef. Eats it. The fifth-year stretch slowly remembers how to make the small sounds of people having dinner.
- Cass's private resolution: not a quiet year, but not a coward's one either.
