# Mari Quintero

## Status
Alive. Human, 23 — the youngest of the six. From the *Helia-Voss.* One of the last two found (S05), brought in off the trail by Drenn. **The dark BONDED her to Drenn at the cave** (the spore reached through her nape-implant; she hums back involuntarily) and **Drenn has claimed her.** At camp she is kept at the women's fires, watched, under Senna's eye — *watched, not yet taken,* by Thaal's order, pending the matriarch's examination.

## Description
Young, small, raw with it. Warm golden-brown skin, scratched and filthy from days lost in the canopy. Auburn curls matted dark with sweat and mud. Huge, wet, frightened eyes. Days of jungle have worn her grief down to a flat exhaustion that breaks open easily. Presses a flat hand to her own sternum when the involuntary hum starts up in her chest — as if she could hold it still. Has a neck-scar she didn't know about (Senna found it); doesn't know what it is.

## Voice & mannerisms
Speaks in breaks and whispers when she speaks at all; the youngest-sibling habit of looking to whoever seems to know. Understands Kael'tha (the chip) without knowing why she does. Her body betrays her — the hum, the eyes that drag toward Drenn and snap away in horror — which terrifies her more than the aliens do, because it is coming from *inside.*

## Relationship to Mira
**Looked to Mira as the one who understood — then felt betrayed by her.** Mira tended her, steadied her, told her the truth (*it's biology; it isn't you*), which Mari grabbed like a rail. But when Mari begged *make it stop* and Mira answered, in effect, *I can't — maybe Drenn can be that for you,* it landed as betrayal: *"You said it isn't me. You said the wanting isn't mine. And now you want me to—"* She heard Mira advocate the thing being done to her. The thread between them is wounded but not dead — Mira is still the only one who has tried to tell her the truth, and the only one who speaks gently to her.

## Relationship to Drenn
**Bonded to him by the spore, claimed by him, and terrified of him.** The pull is real and involuntary — by the third night on the trail she was leaning into his nape-hand in her sleep and waking hating herself for it. The horror is precisely that her own body wants a generation-older scarred male she did not choose. To Drenn it is the dark choosing; to Mari it is a violation coming from inside her skull. This is the campaign's consent-horror at its rawest — handle with weight.

## Goals & pressures
- Make it stop. (There is no way to make it stop; she doesn't know that yet, fully.)
- Not be alone with what's happening in her own body; she clings to whoever will hold the truth steady (Mira; now, warily, Ada).
- Survive the next hour, then the next.

## History with Mira
- Crewmates on the *Helia-Voss* (the ship; not close — Mari is the youngest).
- S05 (the caves): arrived with Drenn; the dark took her in front of everyone; hummed back without knowing how.
- S05 (the stream): tended by Mira; received the truth; begged *make it stop*; **felt betrayed** by Mira's *maybe Drenn can be that for you.*
- S05 (the return/terrace): brought home by the nape; Senna found her neck-scar; steered to the women's fires with one drowning look back at Mira.

## Knowledge
- Understands Kael'tha (the chip; doesn't know that's why).
- Has Mira's frame: it's biology, the cave does it, *it isn't you.* Now knows she has a neck-scar; doesn't know it's the route, or a device, or from her own people.
- Does **not** know the spore by name, the count of women, or anything Mira withheld.
