# Ada Okonkwo

## Status
Alive. Human, 31. Ship's engineer from the *Helia-Voss.* One of the last two women found (S05), brought in off the trail by Drenn. **As of the camp's return, CLAIMED by the chief Thaal** — a custom/power claim with his hand at her neck, **not** a spore-bond (Thaal never took her to the dark; she is unbonded). The only woman in the party the cave has not touched.

## Description
Tall — taller than Mira, taller than several of the women. Deep-brown skin gone ashen with days lost in the jungle. Close cornrows, half-undone by the wild. A pale coolant-burn scar climbing the right forearm. Filthy and scratched on arrival; the kind of straight-backed that exhaustion can't fully bend. Now knows there is a small ridge of scar at the back of her own neck she never noticed before — and does not yet know what it is.

## Voice & mannerisms
Clipped, fast, controlled — an engineer's economy. Rations even her drinking. Reads people and rooms the way she reads a failing system: hunting for the weak point, the way in, the door. Stops panicking the moment a problem becomes a *mechanism* (panic isn't load-bearing). Speaks to the other women low and fast in their shared human tongue — the channel the chip-less Kael'tha can't follow. Says hard things levelly: no heat, which is worse than heat.

## Relationship to Mira
**Was the kin-hand Mira finally took — now fractured.** On the trail and at the stream Ada was the steady one, the ally Mira tended and confided in; she pressed for *the rules and a way out.* But when Mira answered with surrender — *"I gave in to it. I like Korren. He makes me feel safe"* and *maybe Drenn can be that for you* to terrified Mari — Ada named it flatly: *"That's the thing talking. It gets in, and then it makes you its advocate."* She now reads Mira as **compromised** — a crewmate the bond has captured — and she watched Mira flee into Korren's arms in proof of it. There's no heat in the breach; it's worse: a level, final *you gave in; I'm not going to; watch me.* Also between them now: Ada has realised **Mira knew about the neck-scar and told them it was "the air."** The trust is broken, perhaps recoverable, perhaps not.

## Goals & pressures
- **Find a door.** She has been counting exits since the cave; she counts the camp, the cliff, the trail, the men's habits.
- **Not get bonded.** She has Mira's frame — it's biology, the cave is where it happens, the willingness is veneer — and she means to never go down into the dark. Being claimed by Thaal (who *can* walk her there) makes this the central danger of her life.
- Understand the mechanism — including, soon, the thing in her own neck, once she stops being too busy surviving to dig at it.
- Keep the other women alive and clear-eyed; she is the counter-pole to Mira's surrender.

## History with Mira
- Crewmates on the *Helia-Voss* — worked the same ship, were not close.
- S05 (the caves): recognised Mira across the chamber; half-shielded Mari by reflex; looked to Mira for answers.
- S05 (the stream): tended alongside Mira; received her frame; pressed for the rules and a way out; **broke with her** over Mira's counsel of surrender; named the bond as something that *makes you its advocate.*
- S05 (the terrace/return): watched Mira go silent and defer; said nothing more; when Thaal claimed her she gave Mira one level look — *watch me* — and did not look back as she was led away.

## Knowledge
- Understands Kael'tha (the chip; doesn't know that's why). Speaks privately to the women in their human tongue (men can't follow).
- Holds Mira's frame: the bond is biological, routed through the local flora/the cave; the willingness is ritual veneer. Does **NOT** yet know the route is the nape-implant — but now knows she *has* a neck-scar, and is the most likely of the women to connect the two.
- Knows she has been claimed by the chief and roughly what that means by custom.
- Does **not** know the spore by name, the true total of women (she is one of six; doesn't know the count is fixed), or that Mira's "guesses" were anything more than guesses.
