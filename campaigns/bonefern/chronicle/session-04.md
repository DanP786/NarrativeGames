## Scene 1 — The run, and the four-day crossing of the canopy (Days 2-night → 4)

The secret run for the forbidden caves became a four-day crossing, and the canopy charged interest on every step. Mira's body could not hold Korren's pace; she walked until she failed, was carried, was set down to walk again, learning the shape of being kept by a thing that does not tire. He gave her the truths she'd rather not have — the deep places were sleeps away, not hours; he would not carry her the whole of it; by now the camp had found the furs cold and **Drenn** was reading their trail — and chose to spend her in daylight rather than be caught resting, trading her exhaustion against the tracker and letting her watch him make the trade.

The relationship deepened in the walking. Korren held his pre-cave rule against rising pressure every night, the cost louder each time — hardest the dawn she woke sprawled half-bared across him and undid him without meaning to, and he held it anyway. Mira, for her part, stopped rationing what she gave him in sleep and said the quiet thing aloud — *"I'm not used to someone so dedicated. I like it"* — letting herself be, openly, a woman who wants to be wanted this completely. Asked why *her* and not a woman of his own people, he answered with an ugliness he wouldn't file off: a tribe woman is shared — mothers, brothers, old debts — and Mira came with no kin, no claim, *nowhere on the whole world to stand that is not against him,* and he wanted that more than he should. And she **hummed back**, which no tribe woman has ever done; he called it the dark's doing and did not know it was the thing in her neck.

**Memorable lines.**
- Korren (holding the rule): *"Not here. Not even you."*
- Mira: *"I'm not used to someone so dedicated. I like it."*

## Scene 2 — The high river (Day 4)

At a flood-swollen, fast, brown river the story handed Mira the one kind of problem she was built for, and she took it cleanly: she read the water like a failing life-support system, refused the loud deep throat of it, and **called the safe braided crossing herself** — float the gear, a hand-line and not a leash, caution over speed, because *being caught is better than dead.* The call cost an hour they could not spare and gave it to Drenn, and Korren paid it without complaint because her hands were right. The second time her competence answered to her on this world, and the first time Korren watched it *save* them rather than merely impress him — every such proof, in his mouth, making her *worse to lose.*

**Memorable line.** Korren: *"Your hands were right."*

## Scene 3 — The threshold of the deep places (Day 5)

The country went strange as they neared the rock: older forest, luminous moss breathing brighter as they passed, Mira's hum no longer an echo but a deep pull up out of the ground, and the surgical scar at her nape waking to a warmth that was almost pressure. Korren read it as the deep places beginning to *see* them — awe winning out over dread — and promised her the after: once the rock had judged and she was wholly his, the warm dark and a sleep as long as her body wanted, *like a kept thing.* At the threshold she asked how they would know if they were bonded; he told her the rock does not lie — a false pairing gets nothing and goes home unclaimed; a true one, the light rises and the two hums become one. He admitted he had walked **two women into this dark before** and the dark gave nothing back either time — *"I did not know then what the nothing meant"* — and her flat, jealous *"I will hear more of those women. After"* only pleased him. Then she stepped over the threshold of her own will.

**Memorable line.** Mira: *"I will hear more of those women. After."*

## Scene 4 — The claiming, and the activation (Day 5)

In the green dark the rule he had carried four days and nights finally broke, and Korren claimed her fully, and she met it unashamed and choosing — and at the height of it the thing the whole campaign had been walking toward arrived. The scar flooded; the moss-light surged white; and for one heartbeat the deep places were not a place but a **presence** — vast, patient, aware — reaching up through the dead machine in her skull and finding it awake. To Korren it was the simplest joy of his life: the rock had woken, the bond was true, there was no hand anywhere now that could take her back. To Mira, underneath the pleasure, a colder knowing she could not yet name — that was *more* than a man and a woman in a cave; something reached into her and **took**, and only she half-felt it.

The prohibition that walled the caves had been, all along, the one thing accidentally shielding her from this — and she had run to it gladly. The bond his people built a god out of is, in her and only in her, a thing with **spores**, and it is no longer ahead of them. It is done.
