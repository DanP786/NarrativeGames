# Session 05 — 26 May 2026 (real-world)

*(Day 5 → ~Day 9 on Vethaal. Resumes inside the deep places immediately after the S04 claiming; ends in Korren's lodge the night the party returns to camp.)*

## Scene 1 — The deep places, after the claiming → the cave mouth (Day 5, morning)

Korren wakes in the warm dark and draws Mira in, kissing the back of her neck — and at the implant site something answers his mouth that is *not him*: a deep, patient pull, one heartbeat and gone, the first waking sign of what the spore did during the claiming. Only Mira half-feels it; to Korren it is only the bond singing, and he lays it down as fact: *"There is no hand that takes you back. You are kept."* He rolls her beneath him — and the moment breaks. **Drenn** has tracked the forbidden run all the way into the sacred caves, and stands at the chamber mouth with the two missing women, **Ada Okonkwo and Mari Quintero**, gathered off the trail. All six women are now located. Korren kills his light and shields the naked Mira; Drenn, grimly vindicated, throws Mira her own Day-2 prophecy back: *"His arm is between you and me. I said it would not be enough."*

Then the dark takes the judge. Drenn's scarred traceries light unevenly and his hum slides off its flat note — the deep places bonding him, helplessly and mid-reprimand, to **Mari**, the youngest. Mari hums back without knowing how, hand to her own sternum, terrified; and Mira alone understands that she is watching, from the outside, the thing that happened to her hours before. Ada half-shields the girl, then looks across to Mira for an answer Mira only half-has. Into that, Mira finds her voice — quiet, chosen, *without* stepping in front of Korren: *"Wait. Let's go outside and talk. We'll join you."* Korren reissues the call as his own (*"We will go up"*) and covers her; Drenn relocates his reckoning to the chief's fire above rather than dropping it, the first grain of *mine* already settling under his wonder at Mari.

Alone with Korren once the others climb out, Mira drops the fur and bares herself to him — *I am still yours; I am asking you, not going around you* — and proposes the split: *"Those women are terrified. I'll help them while you talk to Drenn?"* Korren agrees, but on his terms, his hand at the unknowing nape: tend them where he can see her, never past the fires with any of them without him, and *"they will all of them understand, before this day is old, whose you are… That is the price of you having this."* Mira gives him the whole of the *yes* — dresses back into the recut wrap, then waits, hands at her sides, for his approval before she will move — unable to tell, even to herself, how much of the ease is hers and how much was grown in her neck, and choosing to let the question be. They climb out of the deep places into the canopy morning, bonefern ranked at the cave mouth, where Drenn fights the pull and loses by inches and Ada waits, fierce and filthy, for Mira to come.

## Scene 2 — The cave mouth / the stream (Day 5, morning)

In the canopy daylight Mira led Ada and Mari a few paces to a stream, within Korren's eyeline, and tended them — pacing their drinking, washing the worst off, feeding them from Korren's journey-store (sent over unasked; Drenn marked the provisioning). Mostly wordless, she finally gave them her hard-won frame in the human tongue the chip-less Kael'tha can't follow: the bond is the local **flora**, thickest in the cave where pairs are made; the *willingness* is ritual veneer over biology; *"That's why you keep looking at him. It isn't you."* She withheld the one thing she knows and they don't — that the women are chipped at the nape, and that the chip is the route.

Then Drenn's pull won. He came toward the stream; Mira stopped him with his name; the senior scout halted, openly at war with a body that had crossed the clearing without his leave, and admitted before everyone — *"I do not know what this is… The dark is meant to be asked. It does not ask"* — then flipped suspicion onto the women: *what did you bring onto this rock.* Mira looked to Korren, worried, and Korren took the field: he refused to be Drenn's explainer and instead weaponised the Kael'tha's own faith — *call it a sickness and you name yourself sick and the cave a liar; or call it the gift, hers, and stop fighting it like a boy.* It defused the contamination suspicion (and buried Mira's secret deeper) — but it handed Drenn permission, and the older man stopped resisting and settled into ownership of Mari.

Asked by Ada for the rules and a way out, Mira answered with the truth that frightened everyone, herself included: *"I gave in to it. I like Korren. He makes me feel safe… I have him,"* and — meaning it as kindness — told Mari maybe Drenn could be that for her. Mari recoiled (*"you said it isn't me"*); Ada went cold and named it — *that's the thing talking; it gets in and makes you its advocate* — and Mira could not prove her wrong: she meant every word, and still cannot find the line where the implant ends and she begins. She retreated into silence and into Korren's side, and let him close her in, in full view of the clearing — the exact picture Ada had just drawn. Korren set the next move: all of it goes before the chief and the matriarch. *"Senna knows things about these women that none of us do."* Drenn assented — *we go back* — and the four-day walk to camp began.

## Scene 3 — The return, and the reckoning at the chief's terrace (Day 5 → ~Day 9)

The four-day walk home held its established shapes: Mira withdrawn, walking where Korren's hand put her; Drenn bringing Mari by the nape, the girl by the third night leaning into that hand in her sleep and waking hating herself for it; Ada unclaimed, under no one's hand, counting the trail for doors. They came down off the ridge onto the river-terrace camp, and the camp *counted* — two more women, the sky still dropping them; the senior scout home lit like a young man and claiming a stranger; Korren bonded and unbowed. Eshen broke into a run for Mira and pulled up short at the man beside her; Suvi tracked them and tallied; Ren stopped chewing.

Then the two it parts for rose. **Thaal** named the defiance, the growing count, and Drenn's turning. **Korren answered** with the sacred frame, Drenn standing at his shoulder (the scout now *with* the boy he was sent to fetch): the dark is never fooled; it chose me, and chose Drenn who walked no path; punish the path you took, but you cannot call the end a theft unless you call the deep places liars. It was a good answer — and **Senna wasn't listening to the men.** She crossed to Mari, made Drenn let go, and found the **nape made-thing, twin of Mira's** (which she'd found and called *awake*). She reframed it to Thaal: every sky-woman carries the same made-thing, same place, same hand — *or the made-thing is how it chose,* put there on purpose by a hand that meant for them to do exactly this, *"the deep places' — or something wearing the deep places like a skin."* And she exposed Mira: *she has known since I found hers.* **Korren spoke for the silent, eyes-down Mira** — *she's mine, bonded; the made-thing didn't stop the rock choosing* — but his own certainty cracked, and he turned to her with a question he kept private. **Ada found the scar at her own nape**, ashen and betrayed (*Mira told us it was the air*).

Mira **deferred again, silent**. Senna read the silence not as guilt but as a woman *taught not to speak* — pity in it now, harder to bear than the accusation. **Korren ended it**, claiming Mira bodily out of the circle: *"She has answered. By being mine. You will not examine her. She is kept."* And **Thaal tabled the whole thing** — not at the gate, with dusk coming; the new women *watched, not yet taken; the matriarch examines first; "you and I are not done — only not done tonight."* As the terrace broke up, **Thaal crossed to Ada and claimed her with his own hand at her neck** — a chief's custom/power claim, no glow, no spore-bond (he never went to the dark); the most resistant woman handed to the most powerful man. Ada didn't flinch; she gave Mira one level look — *you gave in; I'm not going to; watch me* — and was walked off. Drenn took Mari's nape again; the girl's drowning look back.

- Senna: *"Or the made-thing is how it chose."* / *"…a hand that meant for you to do exactly what you have done — the deep places', or something wearing the deep places like a skin."* / *"She will not tell you. She has been taught not to. That tells you something too."*
- Korren: *"She has answered. By being mine. You will not examine her. She is kept."*

## Scene 4 — Korren's lodge, nightfall (~Day 9)

Korren walked the silent, clinging Mira in out of the dusk — and alone with her for the first time since the caves, he didn't reach for her body. He lifted her face gently and asked the question he'd carried from the terrace: *"What is in your neck, Mira. And what do you know that the old woman thinks you know?"* And Mira gave him **the first wholly unmanaged truth she has spoken to anyone since the moss:** *"I don't know what it is. I don't know what it does, or who put it there, or what it's for."* She confirmed the thing in her neck and that she's felt it, and told him plainly she is as far in the dark as he is — holding back only her *guesses* (it's biology; it isn't you; there's no making it stop): not a lie, just a door she didn't open. Korren read her face for the seam, found none, and let his shoulders come down. He made his choice again: *"Then we do not know. Neither of us… Let the rest be a thing we do not know together. I can carry not-knowing. I cannot carry you afraid in my own lodge."* He drew her in to hold, not to claim — and the terrible, simple relief of it was that she *isn't* afraid there, and cannot tell whether that is love or the implant. For one night, the reckoning waits.
