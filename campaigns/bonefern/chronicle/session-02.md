# Session 02 — 20 May 2026 (real-world)

*(Day 2 on Vethaal. This file was reconstructed at the S02 /save — earlier notes referenced a session-02.md that had never actually been written to disk; the Day-2 morning is synthesised here from canon, the late-morning lodge scene from play.)*

## Scene 1 — Korren's lodge, morning (Day 2, early)
Korren wakes Mira with intimacy (off-page), feeds her by hand, and refuses to let her wash his mark away: *"You wear what I have done. All of today."* He re-cuts Senna's clumsy wrap to her own body with his own hands. Mira gives him her account of where she came from — a ship beyond the sky, her people, the likelihood that the four women are from the same vessel — and he believes her, building his own model of other suns and a thing that travels between them. They make a private channel: she tells him; he decides what reaches the chief. She privately names the four women her *tribe* (the *we are*); he agrees not to carry that to Thaal unless she releases it.

## Scene 2 — Drenn's cross, then Thaal's lodge (Day 2, mid-morning)
Korren goes to the chief alone. In his absence the senior scout **Drenn** crosses to Korren's fire to test Mira — and, to wound, hands her the *deep places* as a weapon, letting the shape of the path show. Mira keeps Korren's rule and gives Drenn nothing; Drenn cedes the fire and walks back to his own. Korren returns, faces him down, and afterward owns a rare fault to Mira: *"I should not have left you alone at the fire… that was mine to get wrong, and I got it wrong."*

He walks her before **Thaal.** He gives a controlled account through their channel. There — in the chief's lodge, the same moment Mira learns it herself — **Senna names aloud the made thing sealed at the base of Mira's neck.** Thaal examines her, rules that *what she is owed has changed but what she is remains unknown,* and when Korren asks to take her to the deep places **soon,** refuses — the caves wait on Senna's verdict.

## Scene 3 — Senna's lodge, women's side (Day 2, mid-morning)
Senna maps the implant by hand — small, long, sealed clean, no scar — hears Mira's *translation-chip* theory, and reasons past it, woman to woman: *"A thing meant to help you would not need to be hidden from you."* Someone wanted Mira **understood** here, on purpose. She was **made easy to keep** — soft, no threat, a voice that works on the first creature to find her — which serves equally a woman meant to be *loved into a tribe* or a quiet unwitting *eye* set down in the middle of one; the part of Mira that would know was cut away. On Korren: a good man as men go, but possessive to the bone, who claimed her with his blood and not his head. *"Good is not the same as safe… You are the only one in this camp who will always put you first. Keep her. Even from him."* She offers herself as an ally — *"a friend on the women's side of the fires now, if you want one"* — and sends Mira back to Korren, telling Thaal only that there is a quiet thing in the neck and that she is not finished.

## Scene 4 — Korren's lodge (Day 2, late morning)
Korren ushers Mira back in, relieved she came out whole, and asks what Senna found, his hand near the sealed nape. Mira gives a **benign cover** — the thing only *bridges their language* — and says Senna found nothing more. He takes the bridge-theory as far as it goes (it answers how she speaks Vahn-rii) and keeps his own darker read silent: the implant was sealed in by *the same hand* that cut her memory. *"I am glad of it. It makes you easier to keep."* He hears the seam in her cover but does not chase it: *"Senna found that. And nothing more."*

Searching his face, Mira **cracks partway** and gives him the structural reveal: *"that I have your language so that we could speak. That we were left vulnerable so that you would take us."* He does not deny a true thing. He reframes it as his own claim against the maker — *"it has given me something to keep and a reason to never give it back"* — and the want pulls him toward the deep places, *soon,* out of fear she'll be lifted off the moss a second time. Then she reaches for him — a hand flat on his chest, the hum rising to meet it — and says she is glad she was taken, that she would not survive alone. He agrees she would not (no softening), and confesses the cold thing plainly: because she was *made* to be taken, he cannot know if her gladness is hers or grown in her by the maker, *and neither can she* — and it does not trouble him as it should. *"That is the kind of male you were left with, Mira."* He removes the wrap to see what he is glad to have claimed; she — who has hated being looked at her whole life — does not cover herself. He claims her again in the green light, the pre-cave rule intact.

In the after, he **decides it:** he will walk her to the deep places **at nightfall tonight** — *"I cannot wait any longer"* — against Thaal's refusal and before Senna's verdict. He keeps his promise to tell her the path himself: a days' journey, a night in the inner dark, *go in his to claim, come out his to keep,* no giving her back. Her first words are not for herself but for him — *"Won't you get into trouble?"* — and then, accepting: *"We will go to the deep place anyway won't we?"* He confirms the trouble is real and *all mine to carry,* lays the plan (slip the camp at dark, east then up, gone before the furs are cold), and cracks one narrow door: she may refuse, now, aloud, and he will *hear* it — not, pointedly, *obey* it. Mira takes the door toward going: *"I would like to see these caves,"* said **lightly.** He seals it — *"Then it is done… I will show you the dark myself. Mira."*

**Memorable lines.** Senna: *"Good is not the same as safe… Keep her. Even from him."* Korren: *"That is the kind of male you were left with, Mira."* Mira: *"I'm glad I was. I wouldn't survive alone."* / *"I would like to see these caves."*
