# Session 06 — 27 May 2026 (real-world)

## Scene 1 — Korren's lodge → the lower terrace (Day 9 night → Day 10 morning)

The night ran in Korren's lodge. Mira named the women's hatred and Korren reframed it through Vahn-rii faith — *to want her chosen is the kindest thing one woman says of another* — without ever reaching the real wound: Ada's accusation that the made-thing in her neck makes her its advocate, the door Mira keeps shut. He held her without claiming; then they came together slowly, mutually, **unlike the cave** (no light-surge, nothing taken), and Mira slept against him with the bond-hum one note.

In the morning Mira woke **before him for the first time** and pleasured him awake. Lingering after, draped over him naked, she said shyly *"You don't need to do anything to be thanked,"* and Korren — turning the strange thought over like the foreign word *evil* in the dark — was moved: *"A male does. It is what he is for… I think I would like to believe it of you."*

Korren rose, told her to **stay and not dress,** and returned with a Vahn-rii mated-woman's outfit — deep river-green hide skirt, shoulder-tied top, and a **woven river-stone bead cord** (the mate-mark worn by women *taken to the rock, brought back, and named in front of her people*). He dressed her himself, hands-first: skirt with the practised single knot at her left hip, top tied at the shoulders, **and the cord twice at her waist with the polished-green stone at the front and the knot at her right hip.** Mira watched his face the whole way through. *"Now you are dressed as what you are. Come walk with me."* She reached for his hand unprompted — *as if he had been expecting her always to do this and not as a woman of his people would.* He walked her down through the waking camp openly mate-marked. The woman at the rinse-trough straightened; the men at the smoke-rack went still; the camp registered *like wind around a stone.* On the way down Mira whispered worry about what Thaal would say, and Korren gave her his brief: *"Walk close to me. Eyes on my face when he speaks. Not on his. Mine."*

At the lower terrace Thaal received them at the central fire with three or four older males in the **half-circle that, here, is the shape a meeting takes.** Thaal opened to Korren only: *"Brother. You walk her in this morning with the cord at her hip and the green on her, and you do not send word to me first. So. You have decided what we are going to talk about before I am out of my furs. Sit, then."* He gestured Mira's place implied: behind, in the proper way; not beside. Mira took the wife's not-quite-distance — a half-step back from his right shoulder, hands clasped, eyes on Korren's profile. Korren sat with ease; one thumb-press at her hand before releasing her: *"I have decided nothing for you, brother. I have done a thing of my own lodge in its own time."*

Thaal went to what he came for. He had **claimed Ada by power, no bond,** and he intended to make it real this morning by a **saying** before the chief's lodge in place of the rock — *and* he wanted Korren standing at his shoulder for it. Korren refused the form: *"I will stand at your shoulder, brother, when there is a bond to stand for. The saying is the camp's witness. It is not the binding. The binding is the rock. If you have taken her, take her to the rock… Not for a saying without the rock. The camp will not seal that. And I will not put my mouth to it."* — first public sign of his privately cracked certainty. The half-circle's stillness sided with him. Thaal, flat: *"You give me the rock back. Twelve years I have been chief of this camp, and you give me the rock back. We will see."*

Into the held silence a young Vahn-rii woman named **Yelka** broke in — temple-tattoos, hair undone, **no cord at her waist** — threw Thaal's *twelve years* back in his mouth, and crossed at Mira in three strides: *"He was to be mine."* She snatched at the cord. **Korren intercepted her wrist before the pull did its work; the single knot at Mira's right hip held; the river-stones clicked and settled.** Mira put one hand on the cord to keep the beads from breaking and the other on the band of Korren's wrap. Korren pried Yelka off **finger by finger without breaking her** — *"You will let go of her"* — and let her take her own arm back when she could. Yelka, broken into the open: *"You let me down in front of the whole camp before the sun was up, and now you stand at his fire and tell him he must take **her** to the rock — her — and not me."* She lifted her chin, **looked at the cord and not at Mira's face,** and walked out of her own dignity. An older woman, **Vora,** met her at the edge of the lodges, put an arm round her shoulders, and walked her out of sight. The half-circle did not speak through any of it.

Mira withdrew the hand from Korren's wrap and wrapped it round her own ribs. The cord-hand stayed. The ostracization landed: *no one had come out for her the way Vora came out for Yelka.* Senna pities from a distance she has not closed; Suvi reached for her name once and Mira has not answered it; Eshen has done nothing one way or the other. *The bead cord at her waist is the one thing on her today no one is going to take.*

Thaal pivoted. He **accepted the rock for Ada** and attached a parallel obligation: Korren must publicly answer for Yelka, via Vora, before the smoke turns black — *a saying of acknowledgement, a gift of standing, a speaking to her elder that witnesses hear, whatever your mouth and your hand decide.* *"You give me the rock. I give you the dust. We are even, and the morning gets older."* Korren took it: *"I will speak to Vora before the smoke is black. What I do in front of her, you will see."*

Then Thaal dropped from the formal voice into the **working voice** — same setting, council still — and asked the question of the morning. *"How did you make her so."* He itemised Mira openly: *standing behind you, hands kept, eyes down, the cord on her, the words mine; she does not move unless you have moved first; she does not speak* — and named openly the intent to break Ada into the same pattern. *"What did you do that I did not."* Korren refused the technique-framing: *"There is no *what,* brother. There is the rock. There is the keeping. There is the speaking of her name. I did not make her *so.* **The rock made her mine. She has made the rest.** If you want what I have, take her to the rock. Keep her. Speak her name. After that, what she does or does not do — that is hers. There is no other answer I have for you. And I would have one if there were."*

Behind him Mira's silent answer ran inside her own ribs — *not angry; just true.* She named, only to herself, the moment he found her (lift off her feet, press into the moss, **the claim spoken with his hand at her throat that was not a hurt and was also not a question**); the first night in his lodge (her dirty work clothes peeled off her piece by piece without asking; his mouth and hands where he wanted; *made clear by the doing of it what she was here to be*); the alternative being nowhere. **The rock was *after.*** *He took me to a place I could not leave. He named what I was on the floor. He uncovered me by his own hand. He kept me, and I followed, because the alternative was nowhere. The rest was not me making it. It was me arranging myself around what had already been done.* Held privately. Her face did not change. Korren did not perceive it.

Thaal filed Korren's answer without committing to it: *"We will see, I think, when I have done the one and the second and the third with mine, whether that is all of it. The morning has gotten old enough."* He stood; the half-circle stood with him. *"Smoke is climbing. Go and find Vora. We will speak again before this fire is cold."* He acknowledged Mira at the close **by a small tilt of the head at the cord, not at her face** — the chief's neutral nod to a mated wife at the close of a council in front of his lodge — and turned away into the chief's lodge. The morning is no longer in formal session.

### Scene 1 close — state of play

  1. **Korren → Vora → Yelka:** a public acknowledgement before the smoke turns black. Form is Korren's choice.
  2. **Thaal → Ada → the rock:** Thaal must take her; *watch me* makes this lethal-real.

## Scene 2 — Vora's lodge → the cliff → the river → the return → home (Day 10, mid-morning to midday)

Korren walked Mira up to Vora's lodge on the upper terrace; on the slope a bone splinter went into her right foot and she made the first sound of her morning. Korren on one knee in the gravel drew it clean, wiped the blood on his own wrap, and offered the line that became the morning's small refrain: *"Lean. Most of the way is yours. The last is mine."*

At Vora's door — older, set back, hide weather-darkened — Vora was waiting. Iron-grey hair in a bone-and-stone clasp; **mate-cord wound three times at her waist** (the older wear); face set, *not hostile, not warm.* Voice **low, level — the voice of a woman whose silences sit weight.** Korren opened the form: *"Vora. I have come to you the way a male comes who has set down a daughter of this camp without saying. I have come before the smoke turns black. Will you hear me?"* — *"I will hear you, Korren of the river. Speak."* He named Yelka by her father's line, named what was understood since she was young and he had taken the chief's-second's cord, named what he did: *"I did not take her. I took another. I did not speak to Yelka of it before I did it, and I did not speak to you of it after. **I left a daughter of this camp on the ground.**"*

The saying was broken there. From down the slope of the camp, a dozen shouts went up — a woman calling *up there,* a man calling *down. Get down,* and under both a long held refusing cry. **On the cliff lip above the camp, Ada had put herself on the wrong side of nothing.** *Watch me* embodied. Mira pulled her arm out of Korren's before he could account for it and ran — through the upper terrace (the witness-women parting), down the slope, past the chief's lodge, to the foot of the cliff trail. The kneeling Vahn-rii woman at the trail's base did not try to stop her. Mira started the climb; her foot left blood in the dust. Thaal was already on the steeper line; Korren came up the main trail three strides behind. *The hum two notes one note since the cave.*

Mira shouted Ada's name — first raised voice of her morning. Ada turned her head, found Mira's face and the cord at her hip, and her face *set.* *"You came up here in *that.* I'm not coming down, Mira."* Mira somehow reached the lip. Hand out. The ambiguity preserved — *she does not know, after, whether she meant to grab or only to reach; she does not know whether Ada turned to meet her or turned to step.* They went off together.

The water hit her in the back of the shoulders, the back of the head, the sole of her good foot. The river closed over her. She broke the surface coughing — *the current has her, the current has her* — and **Ada's hand found the back of her shoulder. Ada hauling Mira up onto the next breath the way she has hauled her up at three other moments since the abduction.** Behind them, on the lip: **Thaal stopped. Korren did not. Korren took one step further and went off it after them.** *He was in the air behind her before Mira had time to be afraid of him being in the air.* The river took the bend; the cliff went out of sight.

The current pulled Mira under. She held past wanting to give up — *the small voice in her that has begun, again, to want to give up the way she has wanted to give up at moments before in her life she does not name. She does not give up. She is here for Ada.* A good-foot kick off the river-bed; surface; air. Ada visible, two body-lengths off, alive. The bond-hum at Mira's ear — Korren in the water, closing. She angled the cord to drag her at the back, narrowed the gap to Ada; their fingers found each other (*the grip of two women who have hauled each other up before*). A snag of root and silt at the right-side bank, twenty yards down — the only purchase in fifty yards. Korren arrived: hand on Mira's neck, arm under both, one word: *"There."*

Mira pushed Ada into the safer position at the snag first and trusted Korren to keep her. Korren's hum **lifted a notch in pitch** at his shoulder — *the way it lifts when he is taking in something about her he had not had the language for yet.* *"Hold,"* he said. *"I have you."*

Out of the water, on the silt-bank, Mira said the first thing she had said aloud to him since the lodge: *"You came after me. You could have died."* His correction: *"I could not have. I came… There is no place you can be on this world where I do not come with you. If you go off a cliff, I go off a cliff. If you go down the river, I go down the river. Do not say *I could have died* to me again, Mira. I went."*

In the human tongue (privacy from Korren), **Ada finally spoke** for the first time since the cliff line. *"I was going to do it. I want you to know that. I wasn't bluffing… Then you came up. And I had not pictured **you.** … I still think there is a thing in your neck. I still think it makes you easier to keep. I am not unsaying any of that. … And him going off that cliff. A bond would do that. A male would also do that. I don't get to know which from where I am. But you came after me when nobody was going to come after me, and you put me on the snag first. That happened with your hands. Whatever's in your neck didn't make you do that — or if it did, it made you do the only thing in your skin that's still yours, and I am not going to be too proud to take it. I'm not going back to him, Mira. I would rather take the river again. Tell me what we are doing."*

Mira surfaced the consent-hole, aloud, human tongue: *"It's strange. They say going into the deep caves must be mutual — Thaal didn't ask you. Korren didn't really ask me either. I don't understand their customs."* **Her clear-eyed *what made me so* moved from fully-held to partially-shared with Ada in human tongue.** Ada heard it cleanly and named the strategic hole — *either the rock is doing something the words don't say, or the chiefs are breaking their own custom and the camp isn't calling them on it. Either way we have a hole. I want to know which one it is.* The deeper conversation was set aside for safety: *not now, but.*

Mira's offer: *"Do you want to go back to their camp? I'll be going back with Korren."* Ada turned it (*you're going back to him*), then took the choice she was given: not going back to that fire ring; would walk somewhere else. Mira's pitch back — Korren saved them, Mira can't survive the canopy alone, can Ada? Ada recalibrated: she would come back with **three conditions** — *he does not touch me; I do not go to that rock; we talk about getting off this planet together, in human tongue, when no chip-less male is in the room.* And — when Mira could, soon — *give me what you know about the necks. While I can still get it from you.*

Mira asked Korren to read his chief. Korren laid it out: *Thaal will not let her go for being asked. He has put his name on her in front of his own lodge. He cannot take that name off without a hand he can claim has done it for him.* He offered to give the hand: *the dark would not have her, brother; she has refused; you are owed the saying and the standing and not the woman.* He named the cost: *the chief who is twice unfooted by his own second remembers it.* Mira: *"Please. This is life or death for Ada."* Korren: *"It is done."* He spoke to Ada directly in Kael'tha — *no Vahn-rii hand on her she does not ask for; he would stand at his chief's fire and ask for her name laid down.*

They walked back. Korren carried Mira when the bank smoothed. **Senna and a Vahn-rii downstream-walker came down to meet them at a run** — Senna's face *halfway to I see you both* the first time it had done that thing in front of Mira; *"Thaal is on the lower terrace. He sent us to find you. He has not sent for the rock yet."*

At the lower terrace the half-circle was back — older males, witness-women, Vora and Yelka brought down from the upper lodge. Thaal stood at the centre, hands open and empty. **Senna tended Mira's foot** on a folded hide one of the older males laid down without a word (bone-needle, moss-pad, salve from a stoppered horn). Ada at Mira's other side, hands on her calf — *positioning herself invisible by doing the women's thing;* her thumb at Mira's ankle: *I'm here.* Korren crossed to his council place across the fire and gave Thaal the hand: *the dark would not have her, brother. She has been offered, and she has refused, and the dark heard the refusal and would not let the rocks have her either. You are not owed the woman, brother. You are owed the saying and the standing. Not the woman.*

Thaal accepted in three pieces:
1. **He laid Ada's name down.** *Ada is not mine. She is not to be put a hand on by any male of this camp. She walks on her own legs while she is here.*
2. **He acknowledged the river himself,** before any other mouth could: *I did not follow her into the river. You followed her into the river. The camp saw the difference. I will not be a chief who pretends what his people have seen.*
3. He gave Korren the courtesy back: *You have your hand, brother. Both of them. Will you accept it.* Korren did: *I accept it, brother. All three things.*

Under cover of Senna's work, Mira whispered: *"Will you house Ada?"* Senna, low Kael'tha, eyes on the foot: *Yes. She walks with me when this fire breaks. She sleeps in my lodge. No male hand at my door without my voice from inside it.* And the second half: *When we are alone, you and I, you and I are going to talk about your neck.*

Korren then turned to **Vora and Yelka** at the half-circle's edge and finished the morning's other saying. Three offerings to Yelka: **a name in this camp that is not less for what he did** (to be spoken before the camp as often as she asks); **a gift of standing** — his father's bone-handled blade, to her house; and **the right of refusal** at her door — any male of this lodge who asks for her hand must go through Vora, through Korren, through her own house's choice. **Yelka took the first and the third. Refused the blade.** *Your father's blade stays in your lodge. I do not want a thing of your house in my house. I will not take a piece of you into my keeping. You are not mine to keep. We are agreed on that.* Vora closed the form: *It sits between the camp's stones, daughter.* **The smoke turned black.**

Korren carried Mira home. Behind them the other human women converged on Ada — **Suvi's watcher voice broken open at last**, **Liesel back in her own mouth for the first time since the abduction**, **Ren running.** Mira did not turn her head; the kin-hand was being met by the rest of the hand and she did not need to be in the middle of it. In Korren's lodge he undid the wet dressing as he had done it — cord first, then top, then skirt, each laid beside her where her hand could find it. *Sleep.* He pulled a lighter fur up over her; lay down at her back; folded his hand around hers. **The hum became, again, one note.**
