# Session 06 — 27 May 2026 (real-world)

## Scene 1 — Korren's lodge → the lower terrace (Day 9 night → Day 10 morning)

The night ran in Korren's lodge. Mira named the women's hatred and Korren reframed it through Vahn-rii faith — *to want her chosen is the kindest thing one woman says of another* — without ever reaching the real wound: Ada's accusation that the made-thing in her neck makes her its advocate, the door Mira keeps shut. He held her without claiming; then they came together slowly, mutually, **unlike the cave** (no light-surge, nothing taken), and Mira slept against him with the bond-hum one note.

In the morning Mira woke **before him for the first time** and pleasured him awake. Lingering after, draped over him naked, she said shyly *"You don't need to do anything to be thanked,"* and Korren — turning the strange thought over like the foreign word *evil* in the dark — was moved: *"A male does. It is what he is for… I think I would like to believe it of you."*

Korren rose, told her to **stay and not dress,** and returned with a Vahn-rii mated-woman's outfit — deep river-green hide skirt, shoulder-tied top, and a **woven river-stone bead cord** (the mate-mark worn by women *taken to the rock, brought back, and named in front of her people*). He dressed her himself, hands-first: skirt with the practised single knot at her left hip, top tied at the shoulders, **and the cord twice at her waist with the polished-green stone at the front and the knot at her right hip.** Mira watched his face the whole way through. *"Now you are dressed as what you are. Come walk with me."* She reached for his hand unprompted — *as if he had been expecting her always to do this and not as a woman of his people would.* He walked her down through the waking camp openly mate-marked. The woman at the rinse-trough straightened; the men at the smoke-rack went still; the camp registered *like wind around a stone.* On the way down Mira whispered worry about what Thaal would say, and Korren gave her his brief: *"Walk close to me. Eyes on my face when he speaks. Not on his. Mine."*

At the lower terrace Thaal received them at the central fire with three or four older males in the **half-circle that, here, is the shape a meeting takes.** Thaal opened to Korren only: *"Brother. You walk her in this morning with the cord at her hip and the green on her, and you do not send word to me first. So. You have decided what we are going to talk about before I am out of my furs. Sit, then."* He gestured Mira's place implied: behind, in the proper way; not beside. Mira took the wife's not-quite-distance — a half-step back from his right shoulder, hands clasped, eyes on Korren's profile. Korren sat with ease; one thumb-press at her hand before releasing her: *"I have decided nothing for you, brother. I have done a thing of my own lodge in its own time."*

Thaal went to what he came for. He had **claimed Ada by power, no bond,** and he intended to make it real this morning by a **saying** before the chief's lodge in place of the rock — *and* he wanted Korren standing at his shoulder for it. Korren refused the form: *"I will stand at your shoulder, brother, when there is a bond to stand for. The saying is the camp's witness. It is not the binding. The binding is the rock. If you have taken her, take her to the rock… Not for a saying without the rock. The camp will not seal that. And I will not put my mouth to it."* — first public sign of his privately cracked certainty. The half-circle's stillness sided with him. Thaal, flat: *"You give me the rock back. Twelve years I have been chief of this camp, and you give me the rock back. We will see."*

Into the held silence a young Vahn-rii woman named **Yelka** broke in — temple-tattoos, hair undone, **no cord at her waist** — threw Thaal's *twelve years* back in his mouth, and crossed at Mira in three strides: *"He was to be mine."* She snatched at the cord. **Korren intercepted her wrist before the pull did its work; the single knot at Mira's right hip held; the river-stones clicked and settled.** Mira put one hand on the cord to keep the beads from breaking and the other on the band of Korren's wrap. Korren pried Yelka off **finger by finger without breaking her** — *"You will let go of her"* — and let her take her own arm back when she could. Yelka, broken into the open: *"You let me down in front of the whole camp before the sun was up, and now you stand at his fire and tell him he must take **her** to the rock — her — and not me."* She lifted her chin, **looked at the cord and not at Mira's face,** and walked out of her own dignity. An older woman, **Vora,** met her at the edge of the lodges, put an arm round her shoulders, and walked her out of sight. The half-circle did not speak through any of it.

Mira withdrew the hand from Korren's wrap and wrapped it round her own ribs. The cord-hand stayed. The ostracization landed: *no one had come out for her the way Vora came out for Yelka.* Senna pities from a distance she has not closed; Suvi reached for her name once and Mira has not answered it; Eshen has done nothing one way or the other. *The bead cord at her waist is the one thing on her today no one is going to take.*

Thaal pivoted. He **accepted the rock for Ada** and attached a parallel obligation: Korren must publicly answer for Yelka, via Vora, before the smoke turns black — *a saying of acknowledgement, a gift of standing, a speaking to her elder that witnesses hear, whatever your mouth and your hand decide.* *"You give me the rock. I give you the dust. We are even, and the morning gets older."* Korren took it: *"I will speak to Vora before the smoke is black. What I do in front of her, you will see."*

Then Thaal dropped from the formal voice into the **working voice** — same setting, council still — and asked the question of the morning. *"How did you make her so."* He itemised Mira openly: *standing behind you, hands kept, eyes down, the cord on her, the words mine; she does not move unless you have moved first; she does not speak* — and named openly the intent to break Ada into the same pattern. *"What did you do that I did not."* Korren refused the technique-framing: *"There is no *what,* brother. There is the rock. There is the keeping. There is the speaking of her name. I did not make her *so.* **The rock made her mine. She has made the rest.** If you want what I have, take her to the rock. Keep her. Speak her name. After that, what she does or does not do — that is hers. There is no other answer I have for you. And I would have one if there were."*

Behind him Mira's silent answer ran inside her own ribs — *not angry; just true.* She named, only to herself, the moment he found her (lift off her feet, press into the moss, **the claim spoken with his hand at her throat that was not a hurt and was also not a question**); the first night in his lodge (her dirty work clothes peeled off her piece by piece without asking; his mouth and hands where he wanted; *made clear by the doing of it what she was here to be*); the alternative being nowhere. **The rock was *after.*** *He took me to a place I could not leave. He named what I was on the floor. He uncovered me by his own hand. He kept me, and I followed, because the alternative was nowhere. The rest was not me making it. It was me arranging myself around what had already been done.* Held privately. Her face did not change. Korren did not perceive it.

Thaal filed Korren's answer without committing to it: *"We will see, I think, when I have done the one and the second and the third with mine, whether that is all of it. The morning has gotten old enough."* He stood; the half-circle stood with him. *"Smoke is climbing. Go and find Vora. We will speak again before this fire is cold."* He acknowledged Mira at the close **by a small tilt of the head at the cord, not at her face** — the chief's neutral nod to a mated wife at the close of a council in front of his lodge — and turned away into the chief's lodge. The morning is no longer in formal session.
