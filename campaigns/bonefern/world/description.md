# The world

## The planet (Kael'tha endonym: *Vethaal*)
A jungle world, single supercontinent so far as anyone here knows, with vast stretches of triple-canopy forest broken by river systems, highland karst, and the occasional clearing where something old has fallen and let in light. The air is wet. Things grow on things that grow on things. Sound travels strangely — close ranges feel loud, far ranges feel muffled, and the canopy hides whatever is above it.

Days run a little longer than Earth-standard, about 28 hours. There are two moons, one larger and one small enough to look like a bright star.

## Flora
- **Bonefern** — a tall pale fern with bone-white stalks and grey-green fronds, growing in dense stands at the mouths of certain highland caves. Marks the cave-systems the Kael'tha consider sacred. The flora the campaign is named for.
- **Lash-vine** — fast-growing climbing vine, edible inner pulp, hostile when crushed (releases a stinging sap).
- **Heart-melon** — large, low-hanging fruit, fist-sized to head-sized, deep red flesh, water-rich. A staple.
- **Whisperleaf** — broad-leaved canopy tree whose leaves rustle in still air; the Kael'tha use the sound to navigate at night.
- **Bite-leaf** — dark green leaf, crushed and rubbed on skin as insect repellent. Sharp, clean smell.

## Fauna
- Big predators move at dawn and dusk; the Kael'tha do not travel singly at those hours unless they have to.
- Small biting things are constant. Humans need bite-leaf or they will be eaten alive.
- **Wood-deer** — common ungulate; the broken-stone salt-lick east of the Vahn-rii camp is a known landmark.

## The Kael'tha
- The dominant sentient species of Vethaal. Endonym for themselves is **Kael'tha**; the word means roughly "the people of the warm ground." Tribes have their own additional names.
- Heavy predator-build, ~6 feet standard. Dark amber-bronze skin. Bioluminescent markings visible in low light — patterns are individual, somewhat tribal, somewhat hereditary, the way faces are. **Markings come up at dusk** as the canopy darkens; vivid by full night, faint to invisible in daylight. **Markings also brighten with arousal or focused desire** — a slow swell of luminescence across the chest in proximity to a wanted partner. This is involuntary; it cannot be hidden. Vertical-slit pupils that narrow in firelight and widen in dim. Retractable claws (blunted, bark-and-prey, not weapons of first resort). Fangs visible at speech. A ridge of harder skin runs down the spine and outer forearms.
- Sexually dimorphic: males larger, broader, heavier-muscled; females still tower over human women. Both sexes climb; both hunt; both bear young, though gestation is exclusively female.
- Long-lived (~150 standard years; the very old are rare and respected).
- Language: one root tongue with regional drifts. Mira's chip translates it.
- **Vocal hum** — adult Kael'tha carry a low body-hum that travels through skin-on-skin contact and (a touch more faintly) through close mouth contact. It is a vestige of their biology, not under conscious control; it changes pitch with mood. A Kael'tha humming low and steady is content; the hum drops in pitch with focus or arousal; it picks up to a faint chest-resonance when the body is alert to a desired thing.

### Gender, mating, and possession
The Kael'tha are a strongly dimorphic, strongly mated species. Male Kael'tha are physically larger, socially expected to provide and protect, and culturally — at the level of bone-deep, not consciously examined — **possessive** of their chosen women. This is not a flaw the men try to overcome; it is who they are, and the women of the tribe largely accept and expect it. A male who is interested in a female makes that interest visible and physical — keeping her close, speaking for her, deciding her movement — and the female reads it for what it is.

When a male declares interest in a woman who is not yet his mate, his prior claim is recognised by the rest of the men of the tribe. This is not negotiable except by formal challenge or by the woman's clear, public rejection. New women in particular — captives, strays, the unclaimed daughters of distant tribes — are treated as such until and unless they walk the ritual path (see below).

### Tribes (so far named)
- **The Vahn-rii** ("the river-walkers") — nomadic, follow the great river systems on long seasonal arcs. The tribe currently in possession of Mira and at least three other human women. Mid-sized — about forty adults. Camped at a river terrace at the base of a cliff.
- Others to be encountered. Some are nomadic like the Vahn-rii; some are settled in cave-mouth villages or high-canopy platforms. Territory is loosely respected; raids are not unknown.

## The spore caves and the ritual path
- Certain highland caves grow a particular spore on damp rock in the deep interior, where light does not reach.
- Inhaled, the spore sets a physical pull between two compatible individuals — historically Kael'tha to Kael'tha. Whether it pairs Kael'tha to human is one of the things the hidden experiment is testing.
- The pull is biological, undeniable, and audible at close range — bonded pairs hear a low harmonic from each other's bodies. The only inner signal. No telepathy beyond it.

### What the Kael'tha know — and don't
The Kael'tha **do not know about the spore.** They do not know that an organism in the cave-air causes the bond. They have walked the ritual path for as long as memory; they explain what happens there in their own terms — that the caves are sacred, that the deep rock listens, that the ancestors witness, that a pair who enters returns *mated* if the rock wills it and *not mated* if it does not. The pairing is treated as a gift from the deep places, granted or withheld.

### The ritual path
A male Kael'tha who wishes to claim a woman as mate walks the ritual path with her — a journey from village or camp to the chosen cave, ending in a night spent in the inner chamber. The journey is days long. Customs vary by tribe; the broad shape is universal.

Crucially: **the women are not always told what the path is for.** New women — captives, strays, traded daughters — are told they are walking somewhere, or being taken to a sacred place, or being shown an honour. They are not told that the man walking beside them intends to make them his mate. They learn this in the cave. The Kael'tha do not consider this deception; they consider it the proper order of things. The path is older than asking.

A woman who returns from the path bonded is the man's mate, full stop, by the recognition of the tribe and (per Kael'tha understanding) of the deep places. A woman who returns from the path *not* bonded is — depending on tribe — released, kept as a tribesperson but not a mate, or offered to another. There is no shame in the rock's refusal.

The bonefern stands at the cave mouths are the visible marker. A woman taken under the bonefern is on the path whether she has been told or not.

### Coded path-language
A Kael'tha male claiming a woman in front of his chief and tribe uses formal phrasing that signals path-intention without naming the cave or the rite. Korren's phrase: *"I will speak for her, eat for her, and bring her in time to be seen."* The phrase *to be seen* refers to being seen by the deep places — i.e. walked to the cave. The women, ignorant of the ritual, do not parse it.

### The pre-cave penetration taboo (Vahn-rii, near-universal Kael'tha)
A claimed woman is her man's to touch in every other way — kissed, undressed, fingered, brought to pleasure with mouth and hands, slept against. But **the man does not put himself inside her until the cave.** The Kael'tha frame this as: *the deep places must see her opened to him first.* In practice, the first penetration is the consummation of the bond, in the inner chamber, in the spore-air.

This is held religiously, not casually. A Vahn-rii male who violated the taboo would lose his claim and possibly his standing. The rule is also, for the man, a *cost* — the restraint is part of the gift the deep places eventually grant. Korren has named the rule to Mira directly: *"I am not going to be inside you tonight... The first time I am in you, it will be in the cave."*

## The humans
- Six women. All worked the colony ship *Helia-Voss* in different roles.
- All chipped with a translation implant before drop. None of them know it; the chip is a small surgical scar at the base of the skull they have not noticed yet.
- **At least four of the six have been found in Vahn-rii territory** — Mira and three more brought in on Day 1 evening. Whether this is the experimenters' design or coincidence is not yet known.
- None of them know about the others (though they may recognise faces from the ship).
- Whoever did this is watching. How they are watching, Mira does not yet know.
