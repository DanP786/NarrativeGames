# Campaign setup

Created: 19 May 2026 (real-world date)
Slug: bonefern

## Onboarding answers

**Genre / references:** Ice Planet Barbarians mechanics, but jungle-not-ice and closer to Avatar in tone. Cave-spore bond between humans and large humanoid aliens.

**Voice:** 3rd person, present tense.

**Length:** Open-ended sandbox. No fixed endpoint, no act tracker.

**Tone / content posture:** Nothing off the table. Graphic violence, on-page explicit sexuality, body-impact, moral despair, dub-con dynamics around the bond and the ritual path — all available when the fiction calls for them. Push hard. The possessive-male Kael'tha culture is accepted in-world and the prose does not soften it.

**Stakes / scope:** Personal arc for the PC, with a hidden larger experiment underneath that neither side knows about yet.

**Agency posture:** Gritty realism. Failure is frequent and consequential. The world does not bend to keep her safe.

**Death rule:** Hard death enabled.

**Mind-reading rule:** No telepathy. The bond is purely physical pull, no inner access. Thoughts stay private on both sides.

**The aliens — Kael'tha:**
- Dark amber-bronze skin
- Bioluminescent markings that surface in low light (faint by daylight, vivid in cave-dark or under thick canopy)
- Heavy predator-built muscle but climber-capable
- Vertical-slit pupils, otherwise human-ish eyes
- Retractable claws, blunted at the tips (bark and prey, not constant display)
- Fangs visible when they speak or smile
- A low ridge of harder skin down the spine and outer forearms — vestigial armour, not full plate
- ~6 feet tall standard; some larger
- Multiple tribes, some nomadic, some settled. Territory mostly respected.
- **Strongly mated, strongly dimorphic.** Males are culturally and biologically possessive of their chosen women; prior claim is recognised by other males. This is normalised in-world and not framed as a problem to overcome.

**The bond — cave-spore:**
- A spore that grows only in certain deep caves
- Inhaled, it sets a physical pull between two compatible individuals
- Pull is biological, undeniable, audible at close range (a low harmonic the bonded pair hear from each other's bodies — the only inner signal)
- No emotional bleed, no telepathy, no shared dreams. Thoughts are private.
- **The Kael'tha do not know about the spore.** They understand the bond as a gift of the deep places, granted by the sacred caves themselves.

**The ritual path:**
- A male who wishes to claim a woman walks her to the sacred cave; days-long journey, night in the inner chamber, return as mated pair if the rock wills it.
- **New women are not told what the path is for.** They learn in the cave.
- Bonefern stands at the cave mouths are the visible marker.
- The Kael'tha do not consider the unspokenness deception — they consider it the proper order of things.

**The humans — the six women:**
- Six women, crewmates from the same colony ship (worked together, were not friends)
- Delivered to this planet under cover of something else — neither they nor the Kael'tha know it's an experiment
- All chipped with a translation implant; will understand and speak Kael'tha tongue without knowing why
- Dropped naked, no kit, spaced across territories to maximise tribal contact

**External pressure:** TBD by Claude in-game. The player wants to be surprised.

**Character seed:**
- Mira Holloway, 5'2", hourglass build — tanned olive skin, brown wavy hair (frizzes in humid air), large breasts, small waist, wide hips, thick thighs, fine ankles
- Worked filtration on the colony ship — water and air systems, good with her hands, decent at making something function out of broken parts
- Shy and quiet, but a "moves first, thinks second" instinct when actually pushed.
- Has the translation chip but assumes it's standard colony issue.
- Naked and stripped on arrival.

**Opening situation:** Option B — she wakes already in alien hands. A Vahn-rii scouting party has found her; she's being carried. The bond hasn't triggered yet.

## Player guidance for the arc
- Lead Mira (and, by extension, the other women) toward walking the ritual path with their respective Kael'tha males.
- The Kael'tha do not tell the women what the path is for; that is canon, not a lapse on the GM's part.
- The males are possessive and almost controlling. That texture is wanted, not softened.
