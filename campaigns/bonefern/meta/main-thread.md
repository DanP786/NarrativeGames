## The PC's want
Survive first. Then — figure out what she actually is, stripped of the ship and the uniform that made her a filtration tech and nobody in particular. **S03 gave the want a second face:** to be *for* something here by her own hands (the water; Eshen) — a foothold of competence and standing that is *hers.* And underneath, increasingly owned: to be **kept** by Korren — she has found that she *likes* his possession, that being held this fiercely reads as safety after a life unseen, and she half-knows that pull may be the maker's design and cannot make herself care. **S05 sharpened the cost of that want into a price paid by others:** her leaning-in is now something she *advocates* — and it cost her Ada's trust and frightened Mari worse.

## The central question
What does a woman become when everything that defined her is taken and the world replaces it with appetite, weather, and a body it will *see*? 

The sharpened form: **was the choosing ever hers?** **S05 stopped letting her dodge it.** She said it out loud — *I gave in to it, I like him, he makes me feel safe* — and then urged the same surrender on a woman being claimed against her will, and Ada answered the unanswerable for her: *that's the thing talking; it gets in and makes you its advocate.* Mira could not prove her wrong. The question now has a moral edge as well as an existential one: whatever the truth, her acceptance is no longer only her own business — it greases the others' path, and the one woman refusing has marked her as lost. **The single counter-beat:** alone with Korren she chose *honest not-knowing* — her first unmanaged truth on Vethaal — keeping one door shut (her guesses) but not lying. Whether that honesty is a foothold of real self, or just a deeper, more comfortable surrender, is the open edge into S06.

A secondary question: with others here — does Mira move with them, against them, or only for herself? **S05 answer: she chose Korren and the bond over the kin-hand again, and lost Ada for it.** Ada (*watch me*) is now the counter-pole — the resistance Mira isn't mounting.
