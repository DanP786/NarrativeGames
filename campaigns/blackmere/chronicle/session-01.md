# Session 01 — 6 June 2026 (real-world)

## Scene 1 — Arrival at Blackmere (3rd of Frostmonth, late afternoon → evening)

Liane arrives up the mountain road by hired carriage, one trunk, in a cloak too thin for the cold; first sight of Blackmere above the black tarn. Received into a cold hall by Mara, the housekeeper, whose careful blankness tells Liane the household doesn't know what she's worth. The staff won't meet her eye — a linen girl flinches away.

Casimir Vael enters and the hall changes register without a word. He stops at a courteous distance, reads her like a page, and turns his coldness on Mara for failing to lay a fire — "She is half-frozen. Did no one think to send word ahead?" — then turns it aside for Liane: "They are afraid of me, and fear makes people stupid. You needn't be. Not of me." He orders the east room made ready and hands Liane her first choice under his roof: go up and get warm, or stand proving a point. He refuses to make it for her. She hesitates, then follows Mara.

Mara lays out the house rules (west wing is his; cellars and muniment rooms barred; pull the cord for the fire, not for company) and, not quite kindly, warns: "He's not gentle, my lady. Not with anyone. Whatever he was just now—" (doesn't finish). "Eat it. You'll want your wits about you in this house." The room is far better than a debtor's daughter should get — real bed, a proper **writing-table/desk**, a thick rug. A pale square over the mantel where a picture was recently and deliberately removed.

Liane investigates a small side door: a dressing room holding **costly cold-climate gowns made to her near-measure** (Casimir later: had them made when he knew she was coming, guessed the measurements from the family's old dressmaker — "I guessed high in the shoulder"). Also on the bench: a **small unmarked wooden box she did not bring and Casimir did not place.** She gathers a deep-blue gown, a shift, the fur-collared overdress, and the box back into the firelit room.

Casimir arrives with hot water and food. He acknowledges the clothes are hers; then his attention locks on the box. He asks permission to see it ("May I see it?" — does not take it; she steps back and allows him closer). Inside: a **dried sprig of pale flower and a torn scrap of writing.** It visibly disturbs him — jaw tight, then face deliberately re-ordered. He calls it "an old joke, in poor taste, from someone in this house who thinks I won't trace the hand," says it's nothing she did or need carry, and takes the box away with him. He asks whether she opened it; she truthfully says no, and he reads the truth on her despite her over-crisp delivery.

**Memorable lines (Casimir):**
- "You're a poor liar, Lady Verrold. I mean that as a kindness — it means I'll always know where I am with you... You've decided everything in this house is a hook. Sensible. Keep deciding it. I'd rather you wary than charmed."
- At the threshold: "I am glad you've come. Whatever else this house tells you, that part is true." — then nearly said more, and didn't.

**State at scene end:** Liane alone in the east room — warm fire, tepid water, food untouched, a bed she can bar from the inside. Casimir gone, box with him. Established: his winter-to-all / gentle-to-her asymmetry, shown directly; his habit of handing choices back to her; the seeded household antagonism (the planted box). Scene ended on a clean breath-point before the night.
