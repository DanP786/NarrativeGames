# Liane Verrold

## Vitals
- **Age:** early twenties
- **Station:** Second daughter of House Verrold, a minor coastal noble house, now ruined. Currently held at Blackmere as settlement of the Verrold debt — unnamed status, neither prisoner nor guest nor wife.
- **Family:** Father Aldous Verrold (alive, disgraced, near-penniless). At least one sister whose remaining standing is tangled up with Liane's fate. Mother: TBD.

## Backstory
Raised for a drawing-room life the family can no longer afford — taught letters, music, and the running of a household before the money to run one vanished. When her father's three ships went down on the Mawrock shoals, the Verrold fortune went with them. The debt passed to the Greywater Syndicate and then, quietly, to House Vael; when there was no coin to settle it, Lord Casimir Vael took Liane to Blackmere instead. She came up the mountain road in a hired carriage with one trunk and no clear idea what she was now meant to be.

## Current state
Watchful, frightened, and trying not to show either. She has decided the only safe assumption is that every kindness in this house is a hook. Casimir's warmth toward her — when he is winter to everyone else — frightens her more than cruelty would, because she cannot tell whether her own response to it is real or simply the only shape survival can take. She holds to her one piece of solid ground: she is genuinely, unusually good with letters and accounts, and that is a thing of value she actually possesses, not a thing being done to her.

## Goals
- Survive Blackmere and understand what Casimir actually wants from her.
- Protect what remains of her sister's / family's standing.
- Not be a fool — not mistake captivity for affection, or affection for captivity.
- (Buried, unadmitted) To be wanted as a person rather than a payment — and, worse, to find she wants back.

## Hard limits the character holds (consent spine)
- Hesitation is hers to voice and will be respected. Nothing happens to her that she has not said yes to aloud.
- Any role she takes (follow, submit) is one she grants and can revisit. Surrender, if it comes, is something she chooses out loud — not something done to her.
