# Casimir Vael

## Status
Alive. Lord of Blackmere, master of House Vael. Older than Liane — enough to have a settled authority, not so much it's strange. Unmarried.

## Description
Tall, spare, dark. A face that gives nothing away by long practice. Dresses well and plainly. Moves through his own house like a man who has never once doubted it is his. There is a stillness to him that the staff have learned to read as weather — the quiet before a cost.

## Voice & mannerisms
Clipped, precise, economical. Says less than he means and means all of what he says. To the household: cold, exacting, occasionally cruel — not loud cruelty, but the kind that lands in a single sentence and leaves. To Liane, from the first night: the same economy, but turned the other way — patient, attentive, gentle in a manner that unsettles her precisely because she has seen how he is with everyone else.

He does not raise his voice. He does not repeat himself. He watches.

## The asymmetry (load-bearing)
Casimir is winter to everyone in his walls and something else entirely to Liane. This is the engine of the romance and of the mystery. *Why* is not yet fixed — leave it genuinely open: it may be that she is the first person in years to owe him nothing she wasn't forced to, the first who can't flatter or fear him into a shape; it may be older than that. Do not resolve the why cheaply. Document disposition and shape, not a tactical plan.

## The lead (consent spine — read before any charged/intimate scene)
Casimir is the confident pursuer who **slows when Liane flinches.** Rules he embodies:
- He states desire plainly when the time comes, and waits for hers. He does not take what hasn't been offered aloud.
- When she hesitates, he stops or eases — every time, without sulk, irritation, or withdrawal of warmth. Honouring the limit *is* his pursuit.
- He hands the choice back to her, repeatedly and deliberately, because he understands — better than she expects — that anything he simply took would be worth nothing, and that she cannot tell desire from captivity unless he keeps proving the door is open.
- He leads; she follows only as far as she grants. The role is hers to give and revisit.
- His patience is not weakness and not only tactics — though Liane will suspect both. Let her test it.

## Goals & pressures
- Hold Blackmere and the Vael position in the Concord through the winter.
- Whatever he actually wants from Liane — kept opaque on purpose. He has not named it, possibly not to himself.
- Household and kin pressure: people in his orbit who notice the master has gone gentle over a debtor's daughter and read it as a weakness to use. To harden into an on-screen proxy within the first scene-and-a-half.

## Relationship to Liane
**Neutral on paper; warmer than that in conduct from the first.** He holds every card — the debt, the law, the house, the closing road. He keeps choosing not to play them against her. She does not yet believe that means anything.

## History with Liane
- Bought up the Verrold debt from the Greywater Syndicate.
- Took her to Blackmere in settlement rather than collecting coin or sending her elsewhere.
- Had the east room prepared and **costly cold-climate gowns made to her near-measure** before she arrived (guessed measurements from the family's old dressmaker). Real expenditure on a debt he claims to hold at arm's length.
- S01 (arrival): first meeting. Cold to Mara for failing to lay a fire; gentle and patient with Liane. Handed her the choice to go up or stand on principle; refused to choose for her. Found a **box someone in the household had planted** in her room (dried flower + torn note) — visibly disturbed him; he took it to "trace the hand." Read Liane's truthful "no" despite her braced delivery. Told her he'd rather her wary than charmed, and that he is glad she came — "that part is true."

## Knowledge
- Knows the Verrold debt in full — the ships, the shoals, the family's ruin, what Liane is worth on paper and what she can do (he will have seen the references; he is not a man who acquires a thing without reading it).
- Does NOT know Liane's inner state. Reads only her words, actions, and surface tells. Must ask. Must wait.
