# Campaign setup

Created: 6 June 2026 (real-world date)
Slug: blackmere

## Onboarding answers

**Genre / references:** Low fantasy, courtly. A great house in a cold political realm. No magic — power is purely human and social. GRRM-adjacent sensory texture where it serves.

**Core requirement — romance dynamic (player-specified, load-bearing):**
This is a negotiated power-exchange romance. The surrender lands *because* boundaries are spoken and held. Operating principles:
- Both characters clearly want it; intensity makes them stop overthinking, not stop consenting. "I don't care anymore, I want you" energy — but mutual, both actively participating.
- Explicit desire stated aloud ("I want you, I've wanted you all day"). Confident pursuit with clear acceptance. Strong chasing energy without ambiguity.
- Initial hesitation is **verbalised and respected**. The lead slows, checks in, builds trust. Arousal increases as safety and trust deepen — the "breakdown" is emotional surrender through safety, never coercion.
- Roles are **agreed** (dominant/submissive, lead/follow). Clear boundaries + ongoing consent. "You're in control of me because I gave you that role." The imbalance is real but rests on mutual agreement.
- "I trust you, take the lead." Surrender framed as relaxation, not force. Being guided, not overridden.
- **PC (Liane) is the hesitant, submissive partner.** Casimir is the confident lead who slows when she flinches.

**Voice:** 1st person, present tense.

**Length:** Medium / structured. ~30 prompts target. Three-act structure applies (see act-tracker.md).

**Content posture:** Graphic. Intimate scenes written explicitly when they come, with the negotiated-consent spine throughout (verbalised hesitation, the slow check-in, the spoken yes). Court darkness elsewhere — coercion as a *threat* in the world, violence, cruelty from others — real but not gratuitous; it exists to make the lead's restraint mean something.

**Agency posture:** Gritty realism. Liane can misread people, lose footing, make things worse. The house is a real political machine. The world does not bend to keep her comfortable — which is what makes earned safety land.

**Death rule:** Soft-fail only. Lethal-looking moments resolve as setback, tightening captivity, scars, lost standing. Not a meatgrinder.

**Mind-reading:** Off. Thoughts are private. Casimir cannot read Liane's interest — he must ask, watch for tells, wait for the spoken yes. This wall is structurally required by the dynamic.

## Character seed
- **Liane Verrold**, second daughter of a minor noble house.
- Father Aldous Verrold sank the family fortune into a shipping venture lost on the Mawrock shoals; the debt passed to the Greywater Syndicate financiers, then was quietly bought up by the Vael name. When coin couldn't answer, daughters did.
- Raised for a drawing-room life she can no longer afford. Reads/writes three hands, keeps ledgers better than her father, plays music passably, runs household accounts in her head.
- Cannot fight, has never held a blade, cannot lie to a face that's watching for it. Clever on paper, unguarded in person, and knows it.

## The one who holds the house
- **Lord Casimir Vael**, master of Blackmere. Bought up the Verrold debt and took Liane into his household rather than collecting coin.
- Winter to everyone in his walls — clipped, exacting, feared by his own stewards. To Liane, from the first night, something else, and she doesn't know why. Patient. Does not touch what hasn't been offered. Waits.

## The binding
A debt made flesh. Not quite a prisoner, not quite a guest, not quite a wife — an unnamed thing inside a cold house, with no clear right to leave and no clear status if she stays.
