# Main thread

**Status:** sketch

## Central question
Can something true be built on a foundation that was bought? Liane was taken as payment for a debt — owned, in all but the word. Casimir is the only warmth in a cold house, and warmth from the man who holds your leash is the most dangerous thing there is. The question the campaign asks: is what grows between them real, freely chosen, *hers* — or is it the only shape surrender can take when there's nowhere else to go? And can it become real anyway, if both of them keep choosing it out loud?

## What Liane wants
At the surface: to survive Blackmere, protect her sisters' remaining standing, and not be a fool. Underneath, and frightening to her: to be wanted as a person and not a payment — and, more frightening still, to want back.

## The obstacle
The situation itself. The debt. The power Casimir holds over her by law and circumstance, which poisons every kindness with the suspicion that she has no real choice. Plus the house around them — stewards, kin, court interests — who see Liane as a humiliation, a liability, or a lever, and Casimir's coldness to everyone but her as a crack to exploit.

## Antagonist / pressure
Not yet a single face. The Vael household and its politics; whoever in Casimir's orbit stands to lose if the master of Blackmere goes soft over a debtor's daughter. To be hardened into a proxy on-screen within the first scene-and-a-half.

## Notes
- The negotiated-consent spine is the campaign's moral engine, not just its bedroom rule. The whole arc is about a thing freely given inside a cage, and whether that can be enough.
