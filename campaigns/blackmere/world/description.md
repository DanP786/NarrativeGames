# The realm and the house

## The Pale Concord
A cold northern realm of bound houses — a loose federation of noble holdings sworn, on paper, to keep the peace among themselves and present one face to the powers beyond the mountains. The Concord is held together less by loyalty than by debt, marriage, and the memory of the wars that preceded it. Coin and obligation are the true currency of power; an old name with no money is a corpse that hasn't been told yet.

No magic. The world runs on weather, money, blood, and what people are willing to do to one another. Winters are long and absolute; a snowed-in house is a closed world for months at a time.

## The Greywater Syndicate
The realm's largest concern of financiers — they hold the paper on half the failing old houses in the Concord. Cold, correct, patient. They do not forgive debts; they *sell* them, and a debt sold to the wrong buyer can become anything the buyer wants. The Verrold debt passed through their hands before it was bought up, quietly, by the Vael name.

## House Verrold
Liane's family. Minor nobility, an old coastal name with a small port holding. Her father Aldous staked the family's liquid fortune on a three-hull shipping venture that went down on the Mawrock shoals — cargo, hulls, and the family's solvency with it. The Verrold name survives; the money does not. Liane has at least one sister whose standing now depends, in part, on what becomes of Liane. (Sisters: detail lazily as they matter. At minimum one — keep the cap honest, sketch as needed.)

## House Vael and Blackmere
House Vael is old, solvent, and quietly powerful within the Concord — the kind of house others are careful around. Its seat is **Blackmere**, a fortified manor high on a mountain road, above a black tarn that gives the house its name. Stone, cold corridors, more rooms than people, a household staff that runs with the silent precision of those who fear their master. When the snows come, the road closes and Blackmere becomes its own small kingdom for the winter.

Casimir Vael holds it. Why a solvent house bought a drowned family's debt — and why the master took the daughter into his own walls rather than sending stewards to collect — is a question the household is asking too, in lowered voices.

## Texture notes
- Register: restrained, physical, grave. Cold stone, banked fires, the smell of tallow and woodsmoke, breath visible in unheated rooms. GRRM-adjacent grounding — weather and bodies and small sensory facts, not lyrical excess.
- The cold is a character. Warmth is scarce and therefore meaningful — a fire, a fur, a hand, a kindness. Spend it deliberately.
