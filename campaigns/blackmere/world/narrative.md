# Narrative

Liane Verrold arrives at Blackmere as a debt made flesh. Her father drowned the family fortune on the Mawrock shoals; the Greywater Syndicate held the paper until the Vael name bought it up, and when coin could not answer, Lord Casimir Vael took the second Verrold daughter into his household instead. She is not quite a prisoner, not quite a guest, not quite a wife — an unnamed thing in a cold house, with no clear right to leave and no status if she stays.

This is the story of what grows between Liane and the man who owns her debt — and whether a thing freely given inside a cage can be real.
